# What molecular biology is

Molecular biology is the study of the molecular basis
of biological activity — how genetic information stored
in DNA is copied, expressed as proteins, regulated, and
inherited.  It bridges biochemistry, genetics, and cell
biology, and underwrites essentially every modern
biomedical advance.

## The central dogma

Crick's 1958 + 1970 formulations:

```
DNA  →  RNA  →  Protein
   replication
       │   ↓ transcription
       │       ↓ translation
       └→ reverse transcription (retroviruses, retro-
          transposons, telomerase)
```

What flows + what doesn't:
- **DNA → DNA** (replication) — semiconservative; DNA
  polymerase + accessory factors.
- **DNA → RNA** (transcription) — RNA polymerase II for
  mRNA in eukaryotes; I + III for rRNA + tRNA.
- **RNA → Protein** (translation) — ribosomes + tRNAs
  + aminoacyl-tRNA synthetases + initiation / elongation /
  termination factors.
- **RNA → DNA** (reverse transcription) — viral
  reverse transcriptases + telomerase (a specialised
  RT) + LINE-1 retrotransposition.
- **NOT documented**: protein → RNA / DNA (the central
  dogma's barring of reverse translation remains
  unbroken).
- **Prion-like protein-conformation propagation** is
  a separate non-genetic information channel (yeast
  prion + mammalian PrP).

## The genome — three scales

- **Nucleotide** (~ 0.34 nm) — the unit base; A / T /
  G / C in DNA; A / U / G / C in RNA.
- **Gene** (~ kb) — a transcription unit; in humans
  ~ 20 K protein-coding genes + tens of thousands of
  ncRNA genes.
- **Genome** (~ Gb) — total DNA per cell; human ~ 3.2
  Gb; *E. coli* ~ 4.6 Mb; *Mycoplasma genitalium* ~
  580 kb (smallest free-living); pufferfish 0.4 Gb;
  lungfish 130 Gb (largest sequenced).

Most of the human genome is non-coding (~ 1.5 % protein
coding; ~ 8 % regulatory; ~ 50 % transposable elements;
the rest mixed).  The "junk DNA" framing of the 1970s
has been replaced by recognition that much non-coding
DNA serves regulatory or structural roles.

## What molecular biologists actually do

The discipline encompasses:

- **Sequence determination** — Sanger + NGS + long-read
  technologies (covered in detail in the GM-1.0
  techniques catalogue).
- **Cloning + expression** — building recombinant
  constructs to study or produce proteins.
- **Genome editing** — precise modification with CRISPR
  + base editors + prime editors.
- **Expression profiling** — bulk + single-cell + spatial
  transcriptomics.
- **Chromatin + epigenetic profiling** — ChIP-seq +
  ATAC-seq + bisulfite-seq.
- **Protein-protein interactions** — Y2H + AP-MS +
  proximity labelling.
- **Structural biology** — X-ray crystallography +
  cryo-EM + NMR + AlphaFold prediction.
- **Computational + bioinformatic analysis** — alignment
  + variant calling + de novo assembly + comparative +
  phylogenetic analysis + ML.

## A brief history

- **1869** — Friedrich Miescher isolates "nuclein"
  from pus cells (DNA's first chemical isolation).
- **1928** — Frederick Griffith demonstrates
  transformation in *S. pneumoniae*.
- **1944** — Avery, MacLeod + McCarty identify DNA as
  Griffith's transforming principle.
- **1949-1950** — Erwin Chargaff's base-composition
  rules (A=T, G=C).
- **1952** — Hershey-Chase phage experiment confirms
  DNA as the genetic material.
- **1953** — Watson + Crick (with Wilkins + Franklin's
  X-ray data) propose the double helix.
- **1958** — Meselson + Stahl prove semi-conservative
  replication.
- **1961** — Jacob + Monod's lac operon model;
  Brenner + Crick + others crack the codon.
- **1965** — Holley sequences the first tRNA.
- **1970** — Smith + Wilcox isolate the first
  restriction enzyme (HindII).
- **1972** — Boyer + Cohen pioneer recombinant DNA.
- **1975-1977** — Sanger (dideoxy) + Maxam-Gilbert
  (chemical) sequencing methods published.
- **1985** — Mullis invents PCR.
- **1990-2003** — Human Genome Project.
- **2005-2010** — Next-generation sequencing
  revolution (454, Solexa / Illumina, ABI SOLiD).
- **2012** — Doudna + Charpentier publish CRISPR-Cas9
  as a programmable genome editor.
- **2020** — DeepMind's AlphaFold2 cracks protein
  structure prediction.
- **2023** — First CRISPR therapeutic (Casgevy / exa-cel)
  approved for sickle cell + β-thalassaemia.

## Why molecular biology matters

### Medicine
- Diagnostic genetics (carrier testing, NIPT,
  pharmacogenomics, oncology mutation panels).
- Personalised medicine (HLA matching, somatic-tumour
  genotyping, monogenic disease diagnosis).
- Gene + cell therapies (CAR-T, AAV, CRISPR clinical
  programmes).
- mRNA + viral-vector vaccines (COVID-19 transformed
  the modality).
- Antibiotic + antiviral discovery via genomic
  surveillance + structure-based design.

### Agriculture
- Crop biofortification (Golden Rice).
- Pest + pathogen resistance (Bt crops).
- Climate-resilient breeding.
- CRISPR-edited livestock (PRRSV-resistant pigs;
  hornless cattle).

### Industry
- Recombinant protein production (insulin, EPO,
  monoclonal antibodies).
- Industrial enzymes (laundry proteases, biofuel
  cellulases, food-processing enzymes).
- Bioremediation + sustainable chemistry.

### Forensics
- DNA fingerprinting since the late 1980s; STR
  profiling now standard.
- Familial searching + investigative genetic
  genealogy (Golden State Killer 2018).

### Conservation + paleogenomics
- eDNA monitoring of biodiversity.
- Ancient DNA + Pääbo Nobel 2022 — Neanderthal +
  Denisovan introgression with modern humans.
- De-extinction efforts (mammoth, thylakine, dodo
  programmes).
