# What is a cell?

The cell is the smallest unit of life — every living thing is
either one cell or made of many cells working together.

## The two great cell lineages

Biology splits cells into two major architectures based on
whether they have a membrane-bound nucleus:

- **Prokaryotes** (bacteria + archaea): no nucleus, no
  membrane-bound organelles, DNA loose in the cytoplasm as a
  nucleoid, single circular chromosome. ~ 1-10 µm.
- **Eukaryotes** (animals + plants + fungi + protists):
  nucleus with double membrane (nuclear envelope), elaborate
  organelle system, linear chromosomes packaged with histones.
  ~ 10-100 µm.

Eukaryotic cells are roughly 1000× larger by volume than
prokaryotic ones — and that scale is what made the
endomembrane system + organelles necessary.

## What every cell has

Even the most stripped-down cell carries:

- A **plasma membrane** — phospholipid bilayer separating
  inside from outside.
- **Cytoplasm** — aqueous gel containing all the soluble
  enzymes + metabolites + ribosomes.
- **DNA** — the genome, prokaryotic single circular or
  eukaryotic chromosomes.
- **Ribosomes** — the protein-synthesis machine. Bacterial
  70S vs eukaryotic 80S ribosomes are the basis of selective
  antibiotic action.
- **Metabolism** — the network of chemical reactions that
  extract energy + build cellular components.

## Eukaryotic organelles

Eukaryotic cells additionally have:

- **Nucleus** — DNA storage + transcription + RNA processing.
- **Endoplasmic reticulum** — RER (ribosome-studded, secretory
  protein synthesis) + SER (lipid synthesis + detox).
- **Golgi apparatus** — protein modification + sorting.
- **Mitochondria** — ATP factory; ~ 1500 per liver cell.
- **Lysosomes** — acidic degradation compartment.
- **Peroxisomes** — fatty-acid β-oxidation + reactive-oxygen
  detox.
- **Cytoskeleton** — microfilaments (actin) + intermediate
  filaments + microtubules (tubulin).

Plants add chloroplasts + a large central vacuole + a
cellulose cell wall.

## Why size matters

Diffusion time scales with the square of distance. A
prokaryote can diffuse small molecules across its diameter
in ~ 1 ms. A eukaryote across 100 µm takes ~ 10 s — too slow
for unaided metabolism. Hence:

- The endoplasmic-reticulum network spreads across the
  cytoplasm to deliver translation sites everywhere.
- Mitochondria fuse + fission + traffic along microtubules
  to where ATP is needed.
- Vesicular transport (motor proteins on cytoskeletal
  tracks) moves cargo at ~ µm/s, much faster than
  diffusion at scale.

## The endosymbiotic origin

Mitochondria + chloroplasts have:

- Their own DNA (small, circular, prokaryotic-like).
- Their own ribosomes (70S, prokaryotic-like).
- Double membranes (the inner membrane is the original
  prokaryote, the outer is the host's engulfing membrane).
- Cardiolipin in their inner membrane (a bacterial lipid).

These are the molecular fossils of an ancient symbiosis: an
α-proteobacterium engulfed by a proto-eukaryote (~ 2 Gya)
became the mitochondrion; later, a cyanobacterium engulfed
by a primitive eukaryote (~ 1.5 Gya) became the chloroplast.

The endosymbiotic theory was championed by Lynn Margulis
(1967) against substantial initial scepticism — now standard
textbook biology.
