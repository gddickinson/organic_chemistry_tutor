# Organelles deep-dive

The eukaryotic cell is a city of specialised
compartments — organelles — each with a defined
membrane, characteristic protein + lipid composition,
and distinct biological function.  This lesson tours
the major ones in turn.

## Why compartmentalisation matters

Eukaryotic cells are 1 000-10 000× larger by volume
than typical bacteria.  Without compartments,
diffusion times would be impossibly long + competing
biochemistries would clash.  Membranes solve both
problems: they create distinct chemical environments
+ shorten effective diffusion paths by concentrating
reactants.

The endosymbiotic origin of mitochondria (~ 2 Ga)
+ chloroplasts (~ 1 Ga) added two more
compartments by absorption rather than partition —
each retains its own genome + ribosomes as
evolutionary signatures.

## Nucleus

The defining eukaryotic organelle.

- **Nuclear envelope** — double membrane (~ 30-50
  nm separation); continuous with the rough ER on
  the cytoplasmic face.
- **Nuclear pore complexes (NPCs)** — ~ 2 000-
  5 000 per nucleus; ~ 60 MDa rings of
  ~ 30 nucleoporin (Nup) protein types; selective
  transit gate for proteins > 40 kDa via importin /
  exportin / Ran-GTPase.
- **Nuclear lamina** — intermediate-filament-like
  meshwork (lamins A/C + B1/B2) lining the inner
  membrane; structural + chromatin-organisation
  role.  Lamin mutations cause progeria
  (Hutchinson-Gilford LMNA).
- **Chromatin** — DNA wrapped in nucleosomes
  (octamer of H2A + H2B + H3 + H4 + linker H1);
  hierarchical packaging into 10-nm fibre →
  30-nm fibre (debated) → topologically associating
  domains (TADs) → A/B compartments → chromosome
  territories.
- **Nucleolus** — non-membrane-bound condensate;
  rRNA transcription + processing + ribosome
  subunit assembly.

Nuclear functions: DNA replication, transcription,
RNA processing (splicing + 5'-capping + 3'-poly-
adenylation), ribosome biogenesis, DNA-damage
sensing + repair coordination.

## Endoplasmic reticulum (ER)

A continuous membrane network — perhaps the
largest cellular membrane system by area.

### Rough ER

- Studded with ribosomes (the "rough" appearance).
- Co-translational translocation of secretory +
  membrane proteins via the SRP / Sec61 translocon.
- N-linked glycosylation (Glc3Man9GlcNAc2 core)
  added to most secretory + membrane proteins.
- Disulfide-bond formation (PDI family).
- Quality control via calnexin / calreticulin +
  ERAD (ER-associated degradation; misfolded
  proteins retro-translocated to cytoplasm for
  proteasomal degradation).
- Unfolded protein response (UPR) — IRE1 / PERK /
  ATF6 sensors trigger transcriptional + translational
  remodelling under ER stress.

### Smooth ER

- Lipid biosynthesis (phospholipids, cholesterol,
  steroid hormones).
- Detoxification (CYP-rich; especially in liver).
- Calcium storage (sarcoplasmic reticulum in
  muscle is a specialised SR with high SERCA +
  RyR density).
- Glucose-6-phosphatase activity (glucose release
  from glycogen; liver gluconeogenesis).

## Golgi apparatus

Cisternal stack with cis-medial-trans polarity +
trans-Golgi network (TGN) at the trans face.

- Receives ER-derived vesicles at cis face.
- Stepwise modification of N-glycans (mannose
  trimming + GlcNAc + Gal + sialic acid addition);
  O-glycosylation begins here.
- Sulfation, sialylation, proteolytic processing
  of pro-hormones.
- Sorting at the TGN — proteins leave for plasma
  membrane (constitutive secretion), regulated
  secretion (insulin granules + neuronal vesicles),
  endolysosomal system (M6P receptor sorting of
  lysosomal hydrolases), or retrograde back to
  ER (KDEL receptor + COPI).

## Mitochondria

The "powerhouse" + much more.

- Double membrane: outer (porous; VDAC + TOM
  translocon) + inner (highly impermeable +
  invaginated into cristae; TIM translocon).
- Cristae — folded inner membrane; site of the
  electron-transport chain + ATP synthase.
- Matrix — TCA cycle, β-oxidation, FeS cluster
  biogenesis, urea cycle (partial), heme
  biosynthesis (partial), mtDNA + 70S
  mitochondrial ribosomes (the bacterial heritage).
- Maternally inherited; mtDNA encodes 13 proteins
  + 22 tRNAs + 2 rRNAs in mammals; remaining
  ~ 1 500 mitochondrial proteins encoded by the
  nuclear genome + imported.
- Mitochondrial dynamics — fusion (MFN1/2 + OPA1)
  + fission (DRP1) + mitophagy (PINK1/Parkin).
- Apoptosis gatekeeper — outer-membrane
  permeabilization (BAK/BAX) releases cytochrome c
  → apoptosome → caspase-9 → caspase-3 (intrinsic
  pathway).

Mitochondrial diseases (LHON, MELAS, MERRF, Leigh)
covered in **BC-3.0 advanced "Oxidative
phosphorylation + chemiosmosis"** lesson.

## Lysosomes

Acidic (pH ~ 4.5-5.0) degradative compartment.

- ~ 60 acid hydrolases (proteases — cathepsins;
  glycosidases; lipases; nucleases; sulfatases).
- V-ATPase pumps protons IN to maintain low pH.
- LAMP1/LAMP2 are the canonical luminal-face
  membrane glycoproteins; protect lipid bilayer
  from luminal hydrolases.
- Receive cargo via endocytosis (PM proteins +
  fluid + receptor-bound ligands), autophagy
  (cytosolic content + organelles), and direct
  Golgi-to-lysosome trafficking of new
  hydrolases (M6P-receptor sorted).
- Recently appreciated as signalling hubs (mTORC1
  activation on the lysosomal surface;
  lysosomal Ca²⁺ release; TFEB nuclear-import-
  on-starvation regulation).

Lysosomal storage diseases (LSDs) covered in the
**CB-4.0 advanced "Lysosomal degradation +
lysosomal storage diseases" lesson** (next).

## Peroxisomes

Single-membrane organelles + ~ 0.1-1 µm; populated
in liver + kidney especially.

- Very-long-chain fatty acid β-oxidation (the
  > C20 fatty acids that mitochondria can't
  handle).
- Plasmalogen biosynthesis (myelin lipids).
- Hydrogen peroxide generation (oxidases) +
  decomposition (catalase) — the "peroxide
  source-and-sink" balance.
- Glyoxylate cycle in plant glyoxysomes (a
  peroxisome subtype) — converts seed-storage
  lipids to sugars during germination.
- PEX gene mutations cause peroxisome biogenesis
  disorders (Zellweger spectrum).

## Endosomes

Sorting hub of the endocytic pathway.

- **Early endosomes** — receive newly endocytosed
  cargo; pH ~ 6.0-6.5; sort recyclable cargo
  back to PM (transferrin receptor) vs lysosome-
  bound cargo (LDL).
- **Late endosomes / multivesicular bodies (MVBs)**
  — pH ~ 5.0-6.0; ESCRT machinery internalises
  ubiquitinated PM-protein cargo into intraluminal
  vesicles → degradation in lysosome on fusion.
- **Recycling endosomes** — slow recycling pool
  (Rab11-positive).

## Plasma membrane (PM)

Not strictly an "organelle" but the cell's
outermost membrane:
- Selective barrier + signalling platform.
- Lipid composition: phosphatidylcholine +
  sphingomyelin + cholesterol on outer leaflet;
  phosphatidylserine + phosphatidylinositol +
  PIP₂ on inner leaflet (the "inner-leaflet
  signalling lipids").
- PS-flip outward → apoptosis "eat me" signal.
- Lipid rafts — ordered cholesterol + sphingolipid-
  enriched microdomains (covered in CB-4.0
  beginner "Membrane lipids + rafts").

## Plant-specific organelles

(See also **BT-3.0 beginner "Plant cell
architecture"**.)

- **Chloroplasts** — site of photosynthesis;
  double membrane + thylakoid membranes; own
  genome.
- **Vacuole** — large central; up to 90 % of cell
  volume; turgor + storage + lytic functions.
- **Plasmodesmata** — cytoplasmic bridges between
  adjacent cells across the cell wall.

## Bacterial "organelles"

Bacteria lack membrane-bound organelles but have:
- **Carboxysomes** — protein-shell-bound CO₂-
  fixation factories in cyanobacteria.
- **Magnetosomes** — magnetite particles in
  magnetotactic bacteria.
- **Gas vesicles** — buoyancy regulators.
- **Anammoxosomes** — anaerobic-ammonium-oxidising
  bacteria.

These illustrate that compartmentalisation evolved
multiple times in different ways across the tree
of life.

## Liquid-liquid phase separation (LLPS)

A revolutionary 2010s+ recognition: many
"organelle-like" structures lack membranes +
form by LLPS:
- **Nucleoli, P-bodies, stress granules,
  Cajal bodies, paraspeckles, PML bodies** —
  membrane-less condensates.
- **Synaptic-vesicle pools** (RIM + RIM-BP +
  Munc13).
- **Postsynaptic-density** (PSD-95 etc.).
- **Heterochromatin** (HP1).

Membrane-less compartments concentrate enzymes +
substrates by demixing (intrinsically disordered
regions + multivalent low-affinity interactions).
Hyman + Brangwynne + others established the
field; growing recognition of disease relevance
(ALS-causing FUS / TDP-43 aggregation; tau
condensates in Alzheimer's; mutant huntingtin
condensates).
