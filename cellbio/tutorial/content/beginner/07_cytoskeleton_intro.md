# Cytoskeleton + motility (intro)

The cytoskeleton is the protein scaffolding that
gives a cell its shape, internal organisation,
ability to divide, and capacity to move.  This
beginner-tier overview introduces the three filament
systems + their motors.  The graduate-level **CB-1.0
graduate "Cytoskeleton + motility"** lesson goes
deeper on dynamics + polarisation + drug pharmacology.

## The three filament systems

| Filament | Diameter | Subunit | Polarity | Major roles |
|----------|----------|---------|----------|-------------|
| Microfilaments (actin) | ~ 7 nm | G-actin (ATP / ADP) | Yes (+/-) | cell shape, motility, cytokinesis, muscle contraction |
| Microtubules | ~ 25 nm | α / β tubulin (GTP / GDP) | Yes (+/-) | mitotic spindle, vesicle transport, cilia / flagella |
| Intermediate filaments | ~ 10 nm | tissue-specific | No | mechanical resilience, nuclear lamina, hair / nails |

## Actin microfilaments

### Architecture

- **G-actin** — globular monomer (~ 42 kDa);
  binds ATP or ADP at the centre.
- **F-actin** — filamentous polymer; double-helical;
  POLAR (barbed + end vs pointed - end).
- Polymerisation kinetics: barbed end ~ 10× faster
  growth than pointed end.

### Treadmilling

- ATP-G-actin adds at the barbed end.
- Filament-bound actin slowly hydrolyses ATP →
  ADP-Pi → ADP.
- ADP-actin at the pointed end depolymerises.
- Net result: filament length steady; subunits
  flow through.

### Major regulators

- **Profilin** — recharges G-actin with ATP;
  presents to barbed end.
- **Arp2/3 complex** — nucleates branched
  networks (lamellipodia at the leading edge of a
  migrating cell).
- **Formins (mDia, Daam, FMN)** — nucleate +
  elongate unbranched filaments (filopodia,
  contractile rings).
- **Cofilin / ADF** — severs + accelerates ADP-
  actin depolymerisation.
- **Capping protein (CapZ)** — caps barbed ends.
- **Tropomyosin** — stabilises F-actin in muscle
  + cytoplasm.
- **Cross-linkers** — α-actinin (z-discs),
  filamin (gel-like cortex), fimbrin / fascin
  (parallel bundles in microvilli + filopodia).

### Roles

- **Cortical actin** — sub-PM meshwork; cell
  shape + cortical tension.
- **Lamellipodia + filopodia** — migration-
  driving protrusions at the leading edge.
- **Contractile ring** — actin + myosin II band
  that constricts during cytokinesis.
- **Stress fibres** — bundled actomyosin in
  adherent cells; force transmission to the
  ECM.
- **Sarcomeres** — striated-muscle contractile
  units (covered in the **AB-3.0 intermediate
  "Comparative animal physiology"** lesson).

### Major drugs

- **Cytochalasins** — bind barbed end + block
  polymerisation; research tool.
- **Phalloidin** — stabilises F-actin; used as
  fluorescent stain (Alexa-488-phalloidin etc.).
- **Latrunculin A / B** — sequester G-actin.

## Microtubules

### Architecture

- **α + β tubulin heterodimers** — head-to-tail
  polymerisation forms protofilaments; 13
  protofilaments form a hollow tube.
- POLAR (+ + - ends with α exposed at - and β
  exposed at +).
- Nucleation at γ-tubulin ring complexes (γ-TuRC)
  in centrosomes + non-centrosomal sites.

### Dynamic instability

- GTP-β-tubulin caps growing + ends.
- GTP hydrolysis follows polymerisation; GDP-
  tubulin lattice is unstable.
- When the GTP cap is lost (e.g. growth stalls
  + hydrolysis catches up) → catastrophe →
  rapid depolymerisation.
- Rescue events restart growth.

This bistability lets microtubules SEARCH cellular
space stochastically (mitotic spindle assembly,
chromosome capture).

### Major regulators

- **+TIPs** (EB1, CLIP-170, CLASP, dynactin
  p150) — track growing + ends; couple to
  cargo + cortex.
- **Stathmin / Op18** — sequesters tubulin;
  destabilises microtubules.
- **MAPs (MAP1, MAP2, MAP4, tau)** — stabilise
  microtubules; abundance ↑ in neurons.
- **Katanin + spastin** — sever microtubules.
- **γ-TuRC** — nucleator at centrosomes /
  pericentrosomal material / Golgi.

### Motors

- **Kinesins** — large family (45+ in mammals);
  most walk toward + end (anterograde transport
  in neurons).  KIF1A motor neuropathies.
- **Cytoplasmic dynein** — single mammalian
  cytoplasmic dynein (DYNC1H1); walks toward -
  end (retrograde + Golgi positioning + spindle).
  Plus axonemal dyneins in cilia / flagella.

Both motors are AAA+ ATPases that step ~ 8 nm per
ATP cycle along the lattice.

### Roles

- **Mitotic spindle** — chromosome segregation.
- **Cilia + flagella** — axoneme has 9+2
  microtubule arrangement; axonemal dyneins
  drive bending.  PCD (primary ciliary
  dyskinesia) when defective.
- **Cell polarity** — microtubule orientation
  drives organelle + vesicle positioning.
- **Vesicle + organelle transport** — kinesin-
  dynein traffic on microtubule highways.

### Major drugs

- **Taxanes** (paclitaxel, docetaxel,
  cabazitaxel) — stabilise microtubules → mitotic
  catastrophe; chemo workhorse for breast +
  ovarian + prostate + lung.
- **Vinca alkaloids** (vincristine, vinblastine,
  vinorelbine) — destabilise; chemo for
  haematologic malignancies + sarcomas + lung.
- **Colchicine** — destabilises; gout +
  familial Mediterranean fever + Behçet's; not
  used in oncology because of narrow TI.
- **Eribulin** — newer tubulin inhibitor;
  sponge-derived halichondrin B analogue;
  metastatic breast + liposarcoma.
- **Demecolcine + nocodazole** — research
  tools.

## Intermediate filaments

### Architecture

- ~ 70 different proteins encoded by ~ 70
  genes in mammals; tissue-specific
  expression.
- Coiled-coil dimers → tetramers → 32-mer
  unit-length filaments → 10-nm rope-like
  filaments.
- NON-polar.
- Strong + flexible — provide mechanical
  resilience.

### Major classes

- **Type I + II keratins** — epithelial cells;
  hair, nail, skin.  Mutations → epidermolysis
  bullosa simplex, ichthyosis.
- **Type III** — vimentin (mesenchymal),
  desmin (muscle), GFAP (astrocytes),
  peripherin (PNS neurons).
- **Type IV** — neurofilaments (NF-L, NF-M,
  NF-H) in axons; α-internexin in CNS.
  Mutations → Charcot-Marie-Tooth, ALS.
- **Type V** — lamins (A/C, B1, B2) in nuclear
  lamina.  Mutations → progeria (HGPS), Emery-
  Dreifuss muscular dystrophy, lipodystrophy.
- **Type VI** — nestin (neural progenitors),
  synemin (muscle).

### Roles

- **Mechanical resilience** — distribute force
  across cytoplasm; shock-absorbers vs actin's
  active dynamics.
- **Nuclear lamina** — nuclear-envelope shape +
  chromatin organisation.
- **Cell-type identification** — IF expression
  is tissue-specific; useful diagnostic
  immunohistochemistry markers (cytokeratin
  for epithelium / carcinoma; vimentin for
  mesenchymal / sarcoma; GFAP for astrocyte
  tumours; desmin for muscle; CD45 for
  haematopoietic).
- **Hair + skin + nail** structural integrity.

## Cell migration — the integrated picture

A migrating cell coordinates all three systems:

1. **Polarisation** — front-back axis established
   by Rho-GTPase signalling (Rac1 at front,
   RhoA at rear).
2. **Protrusion** — Arp2/3-driven branched actin
   pushes the leading edge forward
   (lamellipodium).
3. **Adhesion** — integrin-based focal adhesions
   anchor to ECM.
4. **Translocation** — actomyosin contraction
   pulls cell body forward; rear adhesions
   detach.
5. **Cytoskeletal reorganisation** continues
   throughout — microtubules direct vesicle
   delivery; intermediate filaments distribute
   force.

Critical contexts:
- Embryonic gastrulation + organogenesis.
- Wound healing + tissue regeneration.
- Immune cell trafficking (extravasation +
  chemotaxis).
- Cancer metastasis (covered in **CB-4.0
  advanced "Cell migration + cancer invasion"
  lesson**).

## Cilia + flagella

- **Motile cilia** — beat in coordinated waves
  (respiratory epithelium clears mucus; oviduct
  + ependyma; sperm flagella drive swimming).
- **Primary cilia** — non-motile; on most
  vertebrate cells; signalling antenna for
  Hedgehog, Wnt-PCP, mechanotransduction.
- **Ciliopathies** — Bardet-Biedl, polycystic
  kidney disease, Joubert syndrome, primary
  ciliary dyskinesia (PCD), Meckel-Gruber,
  nephronophthisis.
