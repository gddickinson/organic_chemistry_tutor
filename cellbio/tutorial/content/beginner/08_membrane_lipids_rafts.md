# Membrane lipids + rafts

The lipid bilayer is more than a passive
permeability barrier.  It's a dynamic, asymmetric,
laterally-organised, and chemically diverse platform
that determines which proteins concentrate where +
how cellular signals are transmitted.

## Bilayer architecture

A cell membrane is ~ 5 nm thick, consists of two
leaflets of amphipathic lipids with hydrophilic
head groups facing the aqueous phase + hydrophobic
acyl chains facing each other.

Lipid composition by class (% of total mammalian
PM lipid):

- **Phosphatidylcholine (PC)** — ~ 40-50 %.
  Outer-leaflet enriched.
- **Phosphatidylethanolamine (PE)** — ~ 20 %.
  Inner-leaflet enriched.
- **Sphingomyelin (SM)** — ~ 10-15 %.  Outer-
  leaflet.
- **Cholesterol** — ~ 30-40 % of PM lipid.
- **Phosphatidylserine (PS)** — ~ 5-10 %.
  Inner-leaflet enriched.
- **Phosphatidylinositol (PI) + phosphoinositides**
  — ~ 1-5 %.  Inner-leaflet only.
- **Glycolipids + gangliosides** — outer-leaflet
  only.

## Asymmetric distribution

The two leaflets carry different lipid populations:

- **Outer leaflet** — PC + SM + glycolipids +
  gangliosides.
- **Inner leaflet** — PE + PS + PI + PIP / PIP₂
  / PIP₃.

Asymmetry maintained by:
- **Flippases** (P4-ATPases) — flip PS + PE from
  outer to inner; ATP-dependent.
- **Floppases** (ABC transporters) — flop in the
  reverse direction.
- **Scramblases** — randomise leaflet distribution
  in response to specific signals (Ca²⁺-activated
  TMEM16 family in apoptosis + platelet activation).

Functional consequences:
- **PS exposure** on the OUTER leaflet → "eat me"
  signal for macrophages → marks apoptotic +
  injured cells for phagocytic clearance.
- Annexin V binds outer-leaflet PS — clinical /
  research apoptosis marker.
- **PIP / PIP₂ / PIP₃** on the inner leaflet —
  signalling lipids that recruit downstream
  effectors (PH-domain-containing proteins).

## The major lipid classes

### Glycerophospholipids

Glycerol backbone + 2 acyl chains + a phosphate +
a head group.

- **Phosphatidylcholine (PC)** — choline head;
  bilayer-forming workhorse.
- **Phosphatidylethanolamine (PE)** — ethanolamine
  head; cone-shaped (small head, fat tail);
  promotes negative curvature.
- **Phosphatidylserine (PS)** — serine head;
  net negative charge.
- **Phosphatidylinositol (PI)** — inositol-ring
  head; can be phosphorylated at multiple ring
  positions.
- **Phosphatidylglycerol (PG)** — bacterial /
  mitochondrial.
- **Cardiolipin** — diphosphatidylglycerol; mostly
  inner mitochondrial membrane; required for
  ETC function.

### Sphingolipids

Sphingosine backbone (long-chain amino-alcohol)
+ amide-linked fatty acid + head group.

- **Ceramide** — central intermediate; pro-
  apoptotic signal.
- **Sphingomyelin (SM)** — phosphocholine head;
  PM outer leaflet workhorse.
- **Glucosylceramide / galactosylceramide** —
  precursors of complex glycosphingolipids.
- **Gangliosides** (GM1, GD1a, GT1b etc.) —
  sialic-acid-containing; brain-enriched;
  receptor for cholera + tetanus toxins (GM1)
  + influenza haemagglutinin.

### Cholesterol

A 4-ring hydrophobic sterol; rigid + planar.

- ~ 30-40 % of PM lipid; lower in intracellular
  membranes.
- Inserts between phospholipid acyl chains;
  reduces fluidity at high T but increases at
  low T (the "cholesterol buffer").
- Essential for raft formation (below).
- Synthesis from acetyl-CoA via the mevalonate
  pathway (HMG-CoA reductase = statin target;
  see **BC-3.0 graduate "Computational
  enzymology"** + **PH-1.0 statin entries**).

### Phosphoinositides

PI phosphorylated on the 3-, 4-, 5-positions of
the inositol ring → 7 species (PI3P, PI4P, PI5P,
PI(3,4)P₂, PI(3,5)P₂, PI(4,5)P₂, PI(3,4,5)P₃).

- Each species has a characteristic compartment:
  - **PI(4,5)P₂** — plasma membrane (most abundant
    in PM among PIPs).
  - **PI4P** — Golgi.
  - **PI3P** — early endosomes (Vps34-PI3K
    product).
  - **PI(3,5)P₂** — late endosomes / lysosomes.
  - **PI(3,4,5)P₃** — PM (PI3K-AKT signalling).
- Recruit effector proteins via FYVE / PH / PX /
  PHO domains.
- PI(4,5)P₂ is the substrate of PLC (→ IP₃ +
  DAG) + PI3K (→ PIP₃) + 5-phosphatases (→ PI4P).
- Hijacked by viruses (poliovirus PI4KIIIB +
  influenza M1).

## Lipid rafts

Cholesterol + sphingolipid + saturated-PC enriched
microdomains in the outer leaflet (with leaflet-
coupled inner-leaflet rafts).

### Properties

- Liquid-ordered (Lo) phase: more tightly packed
  + thicker + less fluid than the surrounding
  liquid-disordered (Ld) bilayer.
- ~ 10-100 nm in size; transient (ms-s
  timescales for many).
- Detergent-resistant membrane (DRM) fractions
  isolated by Triton X-100 cold extraction —
  classical biochemical assay (with caveats:
  detergent extraction can artefactually create
  "rafts").

### Concentrating proteins

- **GPI-anchored proteins** (CD55 + CD59 + CD52
  + CD58 + folate receptor) partition into
  rafts.
- **Doubly-acylated proteins** (Src-family
  kinases, Lck + Fyn + Lyn; G-protein α subunits
  Gαi + Gαo + Gαq).
- **Caveolins** — define caveolae (a stable raft
  subtype with characteristic flask-shaped
  invagination).
- **Flotillins** — define non-caveolar rafts.

### Functional roles

- **Receptor signalling** — GPI-anchored TCR
  co-receptors + BCR signalling concentrate +
  exclude phosphatases (rafts have less CD45 →
  Lck stays active).
- **Pathogen entry** — many viruses (HIV-1,
  influenza, Ebola, dengue) hijack rafts /
  caveolae.
- **Toxin entry** — cholera + tetanus + botulinum
  + Shiga toxins use ganglioside-raft entry.
- **Apoptosis signalling** — Fas + DR4/5 cluster
  in rafts.
- **Membrane curvature + sorting** at the TGN
  + endocytic vesicles.

## Membrane fluidity + Tm

Bilayer phase behaviour:
- **Gel phase** below Tm — rigid + ordered.
- **Liquid-crystalline (Lα) phase** above Tm —
  fluid + disordered.
- **Liquid-ordered (Lo) phase** with cholesterol
  — tightly packed but laterally mobile.

Determinants:
- **Acyl-chain length** — longer chains → higher
  Tm (more vdW contacts).
- **Saturation** — saturated chains pack tighter
  → higher Tm.
- **Cholesterol** — buffers Tm; broadens
  transition.
- **Headgroup** — minor effect.

Cells modulate fluidity via:
- Saturated:unsaturated acyl-chain ratio.
- Cholesterol content.
- Sphingomyelin:PC ratio.
- Acyl-chain length.

Bacteria use unsaturated + branched-chain
fatty acids (UFA / BCFA) + hopanoids / sterol-
analogues for cold + heat adaptation.

## Lipid synthesis + remodelling

- **De novo PC, PE, PS** in ER (Kennedy + Bremer
  pathways).
- **Sphingolipid de novo** in ER + Golgi.
- **Cholesterol** — mevalonate pathway in ER /
  cytoplasm.
- **PIP / PIP₂ / PIP₃** — kinases / phosphatases
  cycle (PI4K, PIP5K, PI3K; PTEN, INPP4, INPP5,
  SHIP1/2).
- **Lipid-droplet biogenesis** — neutral lipid
  storage; perilipin-coated.
- **Flippases** + **scramblases** maintain
  asymmetry.

## Lipid signalling families

Beyond IP₃/DAG (covered in **CB-1.0 second
messengers** lesson):
- **Lysophosphatidic acid (LPA)** — GPCR ligands
  (LPA1-6 receptors); growth + migration +
  fibrosis.
- **Sphingosine-1-phosphate (S1P)** — GPCR
  ligands (S1P1-5); fingolimod is an S1P1
  modulator for MS.
- **Eicosanoids** — prostaglandins (PGE2 etc.),
  thromboxanes, leukotrienes; arachidonic-acid-
  derived; COX/LOX pathways; NSAID + leukotriene-
  receptor-antagonist drug targets.
- **Endocannabinoids** — anandamide + 2-AG; CB1
  + CB2 GPCR ligands.

## Lipid-targeted drugs

- **Statins** (lovastatin → atorvastatin →
  rosuvastatin) — HMG-CoA reductase inhibitors;
  cholesterol synthesis.
- **Ezetimibe** — NPC1L1 inhibitor; intestinal
  cholesterol absorption.
- **Fingolimod** — S1P1 modulator (MS).
- **NSAIDs** + **coxibs** — COX-1/2 inhibitors.
- **Leukotriene-receptor antagonists**
  (montelukast, zafirlukast) — asthma.
- **Liposomal-formulation chemotherapeutics**
  (Doxil, AmBisome, lipo-irinotecan) — improved
  pharmacokinetics + reduced toxicity.
- **mRNA-LNP delivery** — exploits ionisable lipid
  + cholesterol + helper-PL + PEG-lipid mixes
  (covered in **GM-1.0 `lipid-nanoparticle-
  delivery` entry**).

## Lipidomics

- LC-MS-based quantification of hundreds-thousands
  of lipid species per sample.
- Standards: LIPID MAPS database + community
  shorthand notation (e.g. PC 18:0_18:1 for the
  acyl-chain composition).
- Tools: LipidSearch, LipidXplorer, Skyline,
  MS-DIAL, MetaboAnalyst.
- Increasingly used for biomarker discovery (CV
  risk panels, metabolic-disease subtyping,
  ageing biomarkers).
