# Organelle contact sites

The "organelle isolation" model of textbook cell
biology has been progressively replaced by the
recognition that organelles communicate
extensively through specialised membrane contact
sites (MCSs).  These sub-cellular compartments
are too small to see by conventional microscopy
yet are central to lipid + Ca²⁺ + metabolite +
signalling exchange across the cell.

## What an organelle contact site is

- Two organelles' membranes apposed at ~ 10-30 nm
  separation (no fusion).
- Tethered by specific protein-protein +
  protein-lipid interactions.
- Specialised local lipid + protein composition.
- Distinct functions per organelle pair.

ER is the dominant "hub" — connected to
mitochondria, plasma membrane, Golgi, endosomes,
lipid droplets, lysosomes, peroxisomes,
chloroplasts (in plants).  ER occupies up to
20 % of the PM surface in close contact in
some cell types.

## ER-mitochondria contacts (MAMs)

The earliest-recognised + best-characterised
contact site.  Initially called "mitochondria-
associated membranes (MAMs)."

### Tethers

- **Mfn2** — outer-mitochondrial-membrane
  mitofusin; forms homo- + hetero-oligomers
  with ER-localised Mfn2 (controversial whether
  Mfn2 tethers or repels — both reports
  published).
- **VAPB-PTPIP51** — VAP-B (ER) + PTPIP51 (OMM)
  bridge.
- **GRP75 (HSPA9 / mortalin)** scaffolds IP3R
  (ER) + VDAC (OMM) for Ca²⁺ transfer.
- **Mdm complex (Mdm10 / Mdm12 / Mdm34 / Mmm1)**
  — yeast ERMES (ER-mitochondria encounter
  structure) tethering; metazoan equivalent
  unclear.

### Functions

- **Ca²⁺ transfer** — IP3R (ER) → MCU (OMM →
  IMM) microdomains.  Local [Ca²⁺] reaches
  10-100 µM at the contact site (vs ~ 100 nM
  bulk cytosol).  Activates matrix
  dehydrogenases; couples Ca²⁺ signalling to
  metabolic upregulation.
- **Lipid transfer** — phosphatidylserine (made
  in ER) → mitochondria → decarboxylated to PE
  (made by mitochondrial PSD).  PE then
  traffics back to ER + can be methylated to
  PC.
- **Mitochondrial fission** — DRP1 recruitment +
  pre-fission constriction at ER contact sites.
- **Apoptosis regulation** — local Ca²⁺
  overload triggers MOMP; BCL-2 family proteins
  enriched at MAMs.
- **mTORC2 + PI3K signalling** components MAM-
  enriched.

## ER-PM contacts

### Tethers

- **STIM1 / STIM2** + **Orai1-3** — store-operated
  Ca²⁺ entry (SOCE) — covered in **CB-4.0
  advanced "Calcium signalling"**.  ER-luminal
  Ca²⁺ depletion → STIM oligomerisation +
  translocation to ER-PM junctions → Orai
  recruitment + activation.
- **Junctophilins** — JPH1 (skeletal muscle),
  JPH2 (cardiac), JPH3 + JPH4 (brain).  Bridge
  T-tubule (PM) + SR for excitation-contraction
  coupling; RyR clusters here.
- **Extended synaptotagmins (E-Syts; SYT13-15
  in mammals; tricalbins in yeast)** — Ca²⁺-
  regulated bridging.  Lipid transfer activity
  via SMP domain.

### Functions

- **Ca²⁺ signalling** — SOCE + Cav-RyR cross-
  talk in muscle.
- **Lipid transfer** — non-vesicular PI4P +
  PI(4,5)P₂ + diacylglycerol exchange between
  PM + ER.
- **PI4P maintenance at PM** — Sac1 (ER) hydrolyses
  PI4P at contact sites for "PI4P countertransport"
  to maintain PM identity.

## Mitochondria-PM contacts

- Less-studied than ER-PM but documented in
  cardiomyocytes + neurons.
- T-tubule + mitochondria contacts in skeletal
  muscle.
- Functional role in local energy supply +
  Ca²⁺ buffering.

## ER-endosome / lysosome contacts

### Tethers

- **VAP-A / VAP-B** + various FFAT-motif-containing
  endosomal proteins.
- **ORP family** — VAP-binding lipid-transfer
  proteins (oxysterol-binding proteins).
- **STARD3** — late-endosome/lysosome cholesterol
  transfer to ER.
- **Protrudin / Spastin / FYCO1** — ER-late-
  endosome contacts that direct endosome
  movement along microtubules.

### Functions

- **Cholesterol transfer** — late endosomes /
  lysosomes deliver internalised LDL-derived
  cholesterol to the ER via NPC1 + STARD3.
  Defects cause Niemann-Pick C.
- **Endosome maturation + sorting** — ER-endosome
  contacts mark endosome positions for fission
  + sorting.
- **Lysosomal membrane repair** — ER-derived
  lipids transferred to damaged lysosomal
  membranes.
- **Endosome-driven mTORC1** — late-endosomal /
  lysosomal mTORC1 receives amino-acid signals
  from ER-derived lipids.

## ER-Golgi contacts

- Less-recognised distinct contact-site biology
  vs traditional COPI / COPII vesicle traffic.
- VPS13A + VPS13B + ATG2-WIPI4 may mediate.
- Lipid transfer + Golgi cisternal-architecture
  maintenance.

## ER-lipid droplet contacts

- Lipid droplets BUD from the ER + remain
  connected via thin membrane bridges + protein
  contact sites in many cells.
- Seipin (BSCL2; mutations cause congenital
  generalised lipodystrophy) is essential for
  lipid-droplet biogenesis at ER contact sites.
- Lipid + neutral-lipid transfer between ER +
  LDs via OSBP-related proteins + perilipin
  scaffolds.

## ER-peroxisome contacts

- ACBD5 (peroxisome) + VAP-B (ER) tether.
- VLCFA + plasmalogen + ether-lipid biosynthesis
  requires substrate exchange between ER +
  peroxisome.
- ACBD5 mutations cause Heimler syndrome (mild
  peroxisome biogenesis disorder).

## Mitochondria-lysosome contacts

- Documented in 2018+ (Wong + Krainc *Nature*).
- Tethered by Rab7-GTP (lysosome).
- Mediate Ca²⁺ + lipid exchange.
- Disrupted in Charcot-Marie-Tooth 2B (Rab7
  mutation) + Parkinson's (GBA mutations).

## Plant-specific contacts

- ER-chloroplast contact sites — mediate galacto-
  glycerolipid transfer for chloroplast envelope
  biogenesis; TGD complex involved.
- Plasmodesmata-ER continuity — desmotubule (ER
  in plasmodesmata) is unique to plants.

## Lipid-transfer proteins at MCSs

A growing family of soluble + membrane-tethered
proteins that catalyse non-vesicular lipid
transfer:

### OSBP / ORP family

- ~ 12 ORPs in mammals.
- FFAT motif binds VAP-A/B at ER.
- ORD (OSBP-related domain) binds + transfers
  one lipid molecule (cholesterol, PS, PI4P,
  PE, etc.).
- Most function as PI4P-counter-transporters
  (PI4P down-gradient drives lipid up its own
  gradient).

### CERT (ceramide transfer protein)

- Ceramide ER → Golgi for sphingomyelin synthesis.
- Defects affect SM biosynthesis.

### StAR / STARD family

- Cholesterol transfer; STARD1 (mitochondrial
  cholesterol import for steroid biosynthesis,
  defective in lipoid CAH); STARD3 (endosome-ER).

### VPS13 family

- Bulk lipid-transfer "bridges" at ER-mito,
  ER-PM, ER-endosome contacts.
- VPS13A / VPS13B / VPS13C / VPS13D mutations
  cause:
  - VPS13A — chorea-acanthocytosis.
  - VPS13B — Cohen syndrome.
  - VPS13C — Parkinson's-disease risk.
  - VPS13D — spastic ataxia + chorea + neuro-
    degeneration.

### ATG2

- Bulk lipid transfer for autophagosome growth at
  ER contact sites.

## Methods to study MCSs

### Imaging

- **Electron microscopy + ET (electron
  tomography)** — gold standard; identifies
  ~ 10-30 nm appositions.
- **Super-resolution (PALM, STORM, STED, lattice
  light-sheet)** — diffraction-unlimited
  visualisation in live cells.
- **Split-fluorescent-protein contact-site
  reporters** — split GFP / mNeonGreen / Venus
  reconstituted only when two organelles touch.
- **Proximity-labelling (BioID + TurboID +
  APEX2)** — covalent biotin tagging of MCS
  proximal proteome (covered in **GM-1.0
  `proximity-labelling` entry**).

### Manipulation

- **Synthetic tethers** — FKBP-FRB rapamycin-
  inducible recruitment; light-induced (CRY2-
  CIB1) optogenetic.
- **Knockdown / knockout** of tether proteins.
- **Lipid-transfer assays** in vitro with
  purified proteins + LUVs.

### Functional readouts

- **Genetically encoded Ca²⁺ indicators** at
  organelle surfaces (mtGCaMP, ER-G-CEPIA,
  PM-GCaMP).
- **Fluorescent lipid sensors** (PI4P + PI(4,5)P₂
  + cholesterol biosensors).
- **Metabolite + protein-translocation reporters**.

## Disease relevance

Increasingly clear MCS dysfunction underlies
human diseases:

- **Niemann-Pick C** — ER-late-endosome cholesterol
  transfer defect (NPC1 / NPC2).
- **Hereditary spastic paraplegia (HSP)** —
  multiple subtypes affect ER-endosome
  contacts (REEP1, atlastin, spastin).
- **Charcot-Marie-Tooth 2B** — RAB7 mutations
  affect mito-lysosome contacts.
- **VPS13-related neurodegeneration** — chorea-
  acanthocytosis + Cohen + Parkinson's-risk +
  spastic-ataxia.
- **Wolfram syndrome** — WFS1 / WFS2 mutations
  affect ER-mito contacts; diabetes + optic
  atrophy + deafness.
- **Familial ALS** — TDP-43 + FUS aggregates
  perturb ER-mito Ca²⁺ transfer; SIGMAR1
  mutations cause juvenile ALS.
- **Spinocerebellar ataxia 5** — β-III spectrin
  mutations affect ER-mito contacts.
- **Lipodystrophies** — Seipin / BSCL2 affects
  ER-LD biogenesis.

## Open questions

- What dictates contact-site specificity (which
  organelles + how often)?
- How do MCSs assemble + disassemble dynamically?
- What's the evolutionary origin of MCSs?
- Can MCSs be drugged?  The space of small-
  molecule MCS modulators is largely unexplored.
- How do MCSs integrate signalling with
  metabolism vs trafficking?
