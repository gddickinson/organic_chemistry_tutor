# Intracellular pH + organelle pH

The cell is not a uniform-pH solution.  Different
compartments maintain distinct + actively-
regulated pH ranges that shape protein folding,
enzyme activity, ion-channel + transporter
function, signalling, trafficking, and viral
+ pathogen entry.

## The compartmental pH atlas

A typical mammalian cell:

| Compartment | pH |
|-------------|----|
| Cytosol | 7.0-7.4 |
| Nucleus | ~ 7.2 (similar to cytosol via NPC) |
| Mitochondrial matrix | 7.8-8.0 (alkaline relative to cytosol) |
| Mitochondrial intermembrane space | ~ 6.9 |
| ER | ~ 7.2 |
| cis-Golgi | ~ 6.7 |
| medial-Golgi | ~ 6.4 |
| trans-Golgi / TGN | ~ 6.0-6.2 |
| Early endosome | ~ 6.0-6.5 |
| Late endosome | ~ 5.0-6.0 |
| Lysosome | 4.5-5.0 |
| Secretory granule | ~ 5.5 |
| Phagosome (mature) | ~ 4.5-5.0 |
| Autophagosome (after lysosomal fusion) | ~ 5.0 |

The acidification gradient ER → Golgi → endosome
→ lysosome is one of the most striking features
of the secretory + endocytic pathway, controlled
by V-ATPase abundance + accessory regulators +
counter-ion conductance.

## Cytosolic pH regulation

Cytosolic pH (pHi) is tightly buffered + actively
regulated:

### Buffers

- **Bicarbonate** (CO₂ / HCO₃⁻ / H₂CO₃) — open
  buffer (CO₂ readily exchanges with extracellular
  fluid + lung); pK ≈ 6.1 (low for cytosol but
  high effective buffering due to open exchange).
- **Phosphate** (H₂PO₄⁻ / HPO₄²⁻; pK ≈ 6.8) —
  closed buffer.
- **Protein side chains** — histidine (pKa ~ 6.0)
  + N-termini + C-termini + Cys + Glu / Asp /
  Lys / Arg side chains all contribute.
- **Organic anions** + **Mg²⁺-coordinated
  bases** — additional capacity.

Total cytosolic buffer capacity ~ 20-50 mM /
pH unit.

### H⁺ extrusion / OH⁻ import (alkalinisers)

- **NHE1-9 (Na⁺/H⁺ exchangers, SLC9 family)** —
  electroneutral 1 Na⁺ in : 1 H⁺ out.  NHE1 is
  ubiquitous.  NHE3 is apical-renal + intestinal.
  Driven by Na⁺ gradient (set by Na/K-ATPase).
- **NBCe1, NBCn1, AE family (SLC4)** — Na⁺/HCO₃⁻
  cotransporters + Cl⁻/HCO₃⁻ exchangers.
- **MCT family (SLC16)** — H⁺/lactate +
  H⁺/pyruvate exchange; significant H⁺ load
  during glycolytic activity.
- **Vacuolar-H⁺-ATPase** — pumps protons OUT in
  some specialised cells (osteoclast ruffled
  border; intercalated cells of distal nephron;
  epididymal narrow / clear cells).

### H⁺ import (acidifiers)

- **AE1-3 (Cl⁻/HCO₃⁻ exchangers)** — bring HCO₃⁻
  out, Cl⁻ in (acidifies cytosol after alkaline
  load).
- **Carbonic anhydrases (CA II + IV + IX + XII)**
  — reversibly hydrate CO₂ to H₂CO₃ → H⁺ +
  HCO₃⁻.

### Regulation

- pHi sensors include WNK kinases + GPCR receptors
  (GPR4, GPR65, GPR68, GPR132, OGR1).
- Acid stress → NHE1 + NBC activated → restore
  pHi.
- Alkaline stress → AE + Cl⁻/HCO₃⁻ exchanger
  activated.

## Organelle pH regulation

### V-ATPase — the master acidifier

A massive multi-subunit (~ 1 MDa) F-type ATPase
that pumps H⁺ across membranes using ATP.

- **V₁ domain** — peripheral; ATP hydrolysis;
  8 subunits (A₃B₃CDEFG₂H).
- **V₀ domain** — membrane-embedded; H⁺
  translocation; 6 subunits (a, c-ring [c, c',
  c''], d, e, ATP6AP1).
- Reversible assembly — V₁ can dissociate from
  V₀ to switch off pumping (regulated by
  glucose levels, amino-acid signalling +
  RAVE complex).
- Subunit-isoform-driven targeting (a1 to TGN,
  a2 to early endosome, a3 to lysosome, a4 to
  apical PM in kidney intercalated cells +
  epididymis).

### Counterion conductance

V-ATPase pumps H⁺ but the net charge needs
balancing or pumping stalls.  Counterion routes:
- **Cl⁻ channels** — CLC-3, CLC-5, CLC-7 in
  endo-lysosomes; mutations cause lysosomal
  storage + osteopetrosis (CLC-7).
- **Ostm1** — CLC-7 partner; required for
  function.
- **TMEM175** — lysosomal K⁺ channel;
  Parkinson's-risk variants.
- **TPC1 / TPC2** — Na⁺ + Ca²⁺.
- **TRPML1** — Ca²⁺.

### Why each organelle has its specific pH

- **ER (~ 7.2)** — protein folding + chaperone
  function (calnexin, calreticulin, BiP); QC
  + ERAD; disulfide-bond formation.
- **cis-Golgi (~ 6.7)** — Mn²⁺ chemistry; sialyl-
  + galactosyl-transferase activity.
- **trans-Golgi (~ 6.0-6.2)** — sorting; M6P-
  receptor binding optimal.
- **Endosome (~ 5.0-6.5)** — receptor-ligand
  dissociation (LDL-LDLR, transferrin-Tf, IGF1-
  R).
- **Lysosome (~ 4.5-5.0)** — acid-hydrolase
  optima; LAMP1/2 protection limits.
- **Secretory granule (~ 5.5)** — storage form
  of insulin / catecholamines / chromogranins;
  Ca²⁺ + Zn²⁺ binding.
- **Mitochondrial matrix (~ 7.8)** — ATP
  synthase rotor needs intermembrane-side H⁺
  > matrix-side; matrix is alkaline.

## pH in disease + pharmacology

### Receptor-ligand dissociation

- **LDL receptor recycling** — pH 5.5-6.0 in
  endosome dissociates LDL from LDLR.  Statins
  lower hepatic cholesterol → upregulate LDLR
  → enhance plasma-LDL clearance.
- **Transferrin recycling** — pH 5.5 dissociates
  Fe³⁺ from transferrin (apo-transferrin
  remains receptor-bound + recycles).
- **Antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs)** — pH-
  cleavable linkers (hydrazone) release
  cytotoxic payloads in lysosome.

### Viral + pathogen entry

Many viruses use endosomal acidification for
fusion / penetration:
- **Influenza** — HA conformational change at
  pH 5.0 → fuses with endosome membrane → RNP
  release.  Amantadine + rimantadine block M2
  proton channel that acidifies viral interior;
  largely obsolete due to resistance.
- **Ebola** — GP cleavage by lysosomal cathepsins
  + endosome pH-dependent NPC1 binding.
- **SARS-CoV-2** — endosomal entry route via
  cathepsin L (alongside membrane-fusion route
  via TMPRSS2).
- **Diphtheria toxin** — A-fragment translocation
  in acidic endosome.
- **Anthrax toxin** — PA (protective antigen)
  pore formation pH-dependent.

Hydroxychloroquine + chloroquine alkalinise
endosomes / lysosomes → block these pathways.
Used in malaria (chloroquine — though resistance
is widespread), rheumatology (HCQ for SLE + RA).
Repurposed unsuccessfully for COVID-19.

### Cancer

- **Tumour microenvironment** is acidic
  (extracellular pH 6.5-7.0 vs normal 7.4) due
  to glycolysis (Warburg effect → lactate
  excretion) + hypoxia + impaired perfusion.
- **Reverse pH gradient** — tumour cells maintain
  alkaline pHi (~ 7.4) despite acidic
  extracellular pH.  Driven by high NHE1 +
  MCT1/4 activity.
- pH-driving metastasis + drug resistance +
  immune evasion.
- **NHE1 inhibitors** (cariporide) tested but
  developmental setbacks.
- **MCT1 inhibitors** (AZD3965) in oncology
  trials.
- **CA IX inhibitors** (acetazolamide derivatives;
  SLC-0111) for hypoxic tumours.

### Renal physiology

- Distal tubule + collecting duct intercalated
  cells use V-ATPase + AE1 to acidify (α-IC) or
  alkalinise (β-IC) urine.
- Distal renal tubular acidosis (dRTA) — V-ATPase
  subunit (ATP6V0A4 + ATP6V1B1) or AE1 (SLC4A1)
  mutations.

### Osteoclast + bone resorption

- Osteoclasts seal a "ruffled border" against
  bone + V-ATPase pumps H⁺ into the resorption
  lacuna → pH ~ 4.5 → dissolves hydroxyapatite
  + activates cathepsin K → bone resorption.
- **Osteopetrosis** — V-ATPase a3 (TCIRG1) or
  CLC-7 mutations.
- **Bisphosphonates + denosumab** — anti-osteoclast
  drugs; treat osteoporosis + bone metastases.

### Sperm + epididymis

- Epididymal lumen acidified (~ 6.5) by clear-
  cell V-ATPase → keeps sperm immotile during
  storage.
- pH rise + Ca²⁺ + bicarbonate at ejaculation
  + ascent → sperm activation.

## Methods to measure pH

### Probes

- **BCECF + SNARF + carboxy-SNARF** — ratiometric
  pH-sensitive small-molecule fluorophores.
- **pHrodo + pHrodo Red / Green / Deep Red** —
  acid-activated dyes (great for endosome /
  lysosome work).
- **LysoSensor / LysoTracker** — lysosome-
  selective pH probes.
- **GFP-tagged probes**:
  - **pHluorin + super-ecliptic pHluorin** —
    pH-dependent GFP variant; bright at neutral,
    dark at acidic.  Synaptic-vesicle exocytosis
    visualisation.
  - **mCherry-pHluorin** + **GFP-mApple** dual-
    colour ratiometric probes.
  - **Sub-cellular-targeted pHluorin variants**
    (mito, ER, Golgi).

### Live-cell ratiometric imaging

Standard approach: dual-excitation or dual-
emission sensors normalise for concentration +
photobleaching.

### Phluorin-based exocytosis assays

- VAMP2-pHluorin (or synaptophysin-pHluorin)
  reports synaptic-vesicle fusion: vesicle
  lumen pH 5.5 keeps pHluorin dark; fusion
  exposes to pH 7.4 extracellular → bright;
  re-internalisation to acidified endosome
  quenches.

### Patch-clamp + pH-sensitive electrodes

- BCECF + microelectrodes for cytosolic pH.

## V-ATPase + signalling

A modern frontier: V-ATPase is not just an H⁺
pump but a signalling hub:
- **mTORC1 activation on lysosomal surface** —
  V-ATPase couples to Ragulator + Rags for
  amino-acid sensing.
- **Wnt signalling** — V-ATPase prorenin-receptor
  (PRR / ATP6AP2) is a Wnt co-receptor.
- **Notch signalling** — endosomal acidification
  + S2 cleavage requirements.

## Mitochondrial pH

- Matrix is ~ 7.8-8.0 (alkaline relative to
  cytosol).
- IMS is ~ 6.9.
- Drives ATP synthase rotation (proton-motive
  force + ΔpH + Δψ).
- mtDNA-mutation diseases + ETC dysfunction
  collapse the gradient → loss of ATP synthesis +
  altered Ca²⁺ handling.
- **mt-pHluorin / SypHer-mt** — mitochondrial-
  targeted GFP-derived pH sensors.
