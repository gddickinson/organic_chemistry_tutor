# Cell migration + cancer invasion

Cell migration drives embryonic development,
wound healing, immune-cell trafficking — and the
metastatic cascade that makes most cancers
lethal.  Cancer migration shares the same
machinery as physiological migration but with
hijacked + dysregulated regulation.

## The basic migration cycle

Five integrated steps:

1. **Polarisation** — front-back axis established.
2. **Protrusion** — leading-edge actin pushes
   forward (lamellipodium, filopodia).
3. **Adhesion** — new integrin-based focal
   adhesions form at the front.
4. **Translocation** — actomyosin contracts; cell
   body advances; rear adhesions detach.
5. **Cytoskeletal reorganisation** — microtubules
   + intermediate filaments redistribute; vesicle
   trafficking polarised.

The full process can take seconds (neutrophils
chasing chemoattractants) to days (mesenchymal
fibroblasts).

## Modes of migration

### Mesenchymal

- Spindle-shaped cell.
- Strong integrin-ECM adhesion.
- Actomyosin force generation.
- Slow (~ µm/min); proteolytic ECM remodelling.
- Fibroblasts, smooth-muscle cells, mesenchymal
  cancer cells (sarcomas + EMT-undergone
  carcinomas).

### Amoeboid

- Round / blebby cell shape.
- Weak adhesion; squeezes through ECM gaps.
- Rho-ROCK-myosin-II contractility dominant.
- Fast (~ 10-50 µm/min).
- Lymphocytes, dendritic cells, melanoma cells,
  some leukaemias.

### Epithelial / collective

- Sheets or clusters of adherent cells.
- Maintain cell-cell junctions while moving.
- Wound-healing fronts; gastrulation; some
  carcinoma invasion.

### Lobopodial

- Pressure-driven (rather than actomyosin-flow-
  driven); rare.

Cells switch between modes (mesenchymal-amoeboid
transition, MAT) depending on confinement,
adhesion, ROCK / Rac signalling, and matrix
stiffness.

## Rho-GTPase signalling

Three master regulators of cytoskeletal dynamics:

### Rac1

- Activates **WAVE / SCAR complex** → Arp2/3 →
  branched actin → **lamellipodia** at the
  leading edge.
- **GEFs** — Tiam1, Vav, P-Rex.
- **GAPs** — many; ABR, BCR, ARHGAP families.

### Cdc42

- Activates **N-WASP / WASP** → Arp2/3 +
  formin (mDia2) → **filopodia** + invadopodia.
- Polarity establishment via Par complex (Par3 +
  Par6 + aPKC).

### RhoA

- Activates **ROCK** → MLC phosphorylation →
  actomyosin contraction.
- mDia1 → unbranched actin bundles (stress
  fibres).
- Rear retraction + cell-body translocation.

These three are antagonistic spatially:
- Rac1 + Cdc42 dominant at FRONT.
- RhoA dominant at REAR.

GEFs / GAPs polarise this distribution; small-
molecule manipulation (Rho activators C3 toxin,
inhibitors NSC23766 for Rac, Y-27632 for ROCK)
disrupts migration.

## Adhesion dynamics

Focal adhesions are dynamic, not static:

- **Nascent adhesions** — small (~ 0.1 µm); short-
  lived (1-3 min); form at lamellipodial leading
  edge; integrin + paxillin + talin.
- **Focal complexes** — intermediate; Rac1-
  driven assembly.
- **Mature focal adhesions** — large (~ 1-5 µm);
  stable (10-30+ min); RhoA-driven; vinculin +
  zyxin + tensin recruited; force transmission to
  actomyosin stress fibres.
- **Fibrillar adhesions** — elongated; α5β1
  integrin + tensin; fibronectin assembly.

Adhesion turnover at the rear is required for
migration — calpain-mediated talin cleavage,
PTP1B-mediated FAK dephosphorylation, microtubule-
catastrophe-driven release all play roles.

## Mechanotransduction in migration

Cells SENSE matrix stiffness + topography:
- **Durotaxis** — migration up stiffness gradients.
- **Topotaxis** — migration along grooves /
  fibrils.
- **YAP / TAZ** — Hippo-pathway effectors;
  nuclear translocation responds to ECM
  stiffness + cell spreading.
- Soft (~ kPa) → adipogenesis or neural lineage
  in MSCs; intermediate → muscle; stiff (~ tens
  of kPa) → bone (Engler 2006 *Cell*).

## Chemotaxis

Cells migrate up chemical gradients:
- **Neutrophils** — fMLF + IL-8 + LTB4 + C5a;
  GPCR-Gαi-PI3K → PIP₃-driven Rac activation at
  leading edge.
- **T cells** — CXCL10 / 11 / 12 + CCL19 / 21;
  CXCR3 / CXCR4 / CCR7.
- **Tumour-cell chemotaxis** — CXCR4-CXCL12
  axis drives metastasis to bone marrow + lung.
- **Embryonic guidance** — FGF, Wnt, BMP, Slit /
  Robo, netrin / DCC, semaphorin / plexin /
  neuropilin gradients.

Drugs:
- **Maraviroc** — CCR5 antagonist; HIV entry
  inhibition (CCR5-utilising strains).
- **Plerixafor (AMD3100)** — CXCR4 antagonist;
  HSC mobilisation.

## The metastatic cascade

The ~ 90 % of cancer deaths attributable to
metastasis follow a stereotyped sequence:

### 1. Local invasion

- Loss of cell-cell adhesion (E-cadherin
  downregulation; β-catenin redistribution).
- ECM degradation by MMPs + cathepsins +
  serine proteases.
- Invadopodia — Cdc42 / N-WASP / Arp2/3-driven
  protrusions packed with MMP-14 + cortactin +
  Tks5.
- Migration into local stroma.

### 2. EMT (epithelial-mesenchymal transition)

A transcription-factor-driven programme:

- **Drivers**: TGF-β, Wnt, Notch, FGF, EGF,
  hypoxia (HIF), inflammation (IL-6).
- **EMT-TFs**: Snail (SNAI1), Slug (SNAI2),
  Twist (TWIST1, TWIST2), ZEB1, ZEB2.
- **Outcomes**:
  - E-cadherin → N-cadherin (cadherin switch).
  - Cytokeratin → vimentin.
  - Tight + adherens junctions disassembled.
  - Increased motility + invasiveness +
    stem-like properties.
  - **Partial / hybrid EMT** is increasingly
    recognised — cells retain some epithelial
    features while gaining mesenchymal ones.

EMT also occurs physiologically in development
(gastrulation, neural crest delamination) +
wound healing + fibrosis.

### 3. Intravasation

- Tumour cells enter blood vessels or lymphatics.
- Penetrate basement membrane via MMPs.
- Often facilitated by tumour-associated
  macrophages (TMEM = tumour microenvironment of
  metastasis: TIE2-Mφ + endothelial cell +
  cancer cell).

### 4. Survival in circulation

- Most circulating tumour cells (CTCs) die from
  shear stress, anoikis (loss-of-anchorage
  apoptosis), immune attack (NK + macrophage),
  or vascular trapping.
- Survival mechanisms: platelet-cloaking
  (P-selectin + GPIb), neutrophil interactions,
  clustering of CTCs (more metastatically
  competent than single CTCs).

### 5. Extravasation

- CTC arrest in capillary bed (size-based or
  receptor-mediated).
- Adhesion to endothelium via E-selectin / L-selectin
  / integrins.
- Transmigration across endothelium (similar to
  leukocyte diapedesis).

### 6. Colonisation + metastatic outgrowth

- Most extravasated cells remain dormant.
- A subset reactivates after months-years to
  decades, driven by:
  - Immune escape.
  - Angiogenic switch.
  - Microenvironment niche remodelling
    (osteoclast-osteoblast rebalance for bone
    metastasis; Kupffer-cell + hepatocyte
    interactions for liver metastasis).
  - Acquired stem-like properties.

### 7. Organ tropism

Different cancers metastasise preferentially to
different organs (Stephen Paget's "seed-and-soil"
hypothesis, 1889).

| Cancer | Common metastatic sites |
|--------|--------------------------|
| Breast | Bone, lung, liver, brain |
| Prostate | Bone (osteoblastic) |
| Colorectal | Liver, lung, peritoneum |
| Lung | Brain, bone, liver, adrenal |
| Melanoma | Brain, lung, liver, skin |
| Pancreatic | Liver, lung, peritoneum |
| Renal | Lung, bone, liver, brain |
| Ovarian | Peritoneum, omentum |

Tropism is driven by chemokine gradients (CXCR4-
CXCL12 to bone marrow), endothelial-receptor
expression, microenvironmental niches, and
exosome / pre-metastatic-niche factors.

## Pre-metastatic niche

A surprising finding: primary tumours condition
distant organs BEFORE colonising them.

Mechanisms:
- **Tumour-derived exosomes** carry integrins
  + miRNAs + proteins that home to specific
  organs based on integrin-tissue matching
  (α6β4 to lung, αvβ5 to liver — Lyden 2015
  *Nature*).
- **Soluble factors** (LOX, S100A8 / S100A9,
  TIMP1) recruit bone-marrow-derived cells to
  the future metastatic site.
- **VEGFR1+ haematopoietic progenitors** seed
  the niche.
- **Fibronectin upregulation** + fibroblast /
  endothelial / immune-cell remodelling
  prepares the matrix.

## Circulating tumour cells (CTCs)

A minimally-invasive way to monitor metastasis:

- **CellSearch** (Veridex) — first FDA-cleared
  CTC platform.
- **Parsortix + Vortex + RareCyte** — newer
  microfluidic systems.
- Single-cell sequencing of CTCs reveals
  heterogeneity + clonal evolution.
- Clinical utility: prognosis (high CTC count =
  worse outcome) + treatment monitoring.

cfDNA / ctDNA (covered in **GM-3.0 advanced
"Cancer genomics"**) is another liquid-biopsy
modality — increasingly preferred over CTC
counting.

## Therapeutic targeting

Migration / invasion are difficult to drug
selectively, but several approaches are mature:

### Anti-angiogenic

- **Bevacizumab** (VEGF mAb).
- **Sorafenib + sunitinib + axitinib + pazopanib**
  (VEGFR multi-kinase inhibitors).
- **Aflibercept** (VEGF-trap fusion).

### Adhesion / migration

- **Cilengitide** (αv integrin antagonist,
  glioblastoma trial fail) — illustrates the
  difficulty.
- **Volociximab** (α5β1 antagonist; trials).
- **GM-CSF + immune-cell-recruitment** strategies.

### Direct anti-EMT / CSC

- **STAT3 inhibitors** (BBI608, napabucasin).
- **Wnt-pathway inhibitors** (vantictumab; PORCN
  inhibitors WNT974 / LGK974).
- **Notch inhibitors** (γ-secretase inhibitors:
  RO4929097; AL101 + Notch-targeted mAbs).
- **Hedgehog inhibitors** (vismodegib + sonidegib
  for BCC).

### Microenvironment

- **Stromal-CAF targeting** (FAP-CAR-T trials).
- **Immune-checkpoint inhibitors** (anti-PD-1 +
  anti-CTLA-4 + anti-LAG-3) — covered in
  **PH-3.0 advanced "Biologics"**.
- **CXCR4 antagonists** (plerixafor, balixafortide
  in trials).

### Tumour-organ-axis disruption

- **Bisphosphonates + denosumab** — bone
  metastasis prevention (RANKL antagonism).
- **Bone-marrow microenvironment** modulators.
