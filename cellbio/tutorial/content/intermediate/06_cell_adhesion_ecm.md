# Cell-cell adhesion + extracellular matrix

Multicellular organisms depend on selective + tunable
adhesion: cells stick to each other through
specialised junctions, and they anchor to a
biochemically rich extracellular matrix (ECM) that
provides both physical scaffolding + signalling
cues.

## Cell-cell junction types

Four major types in vertebrate epithelia:

| Junction | Function | Key proteins |
|----------|----------|--------------|
| Tight junctions (zonula occludens) | Seal paracellular space; create polarised barrier | Claudins, occludin, ZO-1/2/3 |
| Adherens junctions | Mechanical adhesion + actin coupling | E-cadherin, β/α-catenin, p120-catenin |
| Desmosomes | High-tensile mechanical adhesion + IF coupling | Desmogleins, desmocollins, plakoglobin, plakophilin, desmoplakin |
| Gap junctions | Direct cell-cell communication via small molecules | Connexins (6 per hemichannel; 12 per channel) |

### Tight junctions

- Strands of claudin family (~ 26 in humans) +
  occludin form sealing strands.
- ZO-1 / ZO-2 / ZO-3 link to cortical actin.
- **Claudin selectivity** — claudin-2 + claudin-15
  are leaky (allow Na⁺ + water); claudin-1 + 3 +
  4 + 5 + 19 are tight.  Tissue-specific
  expression patterns determine paracellular
  permeability (kidney TAL has claudin-16 + 19
  for Mg²⁺/Ca²⁺ paracellular flux).
- Polarisation function — separate apical from
  basolateral PM domains.
- Targeted by pathogens — *Clostridium perfringens*
  enterotoxin binds claudin-3/4; HCV E2 binds
  claudin-1.

### Adherens junctions

- **Cadherin** family — Ca²⁺-dependent
  homophilic transmembrane adhesion.
- **E-cadherin** — epithelium.
- **N-cadherin** — neural + mesenchymal +
  fibroblast.
- **VE-cadherin** — endothelium.
- **P-cadherin** — placenta.
- ~ 100 cadherin family members in mammals
  (classical + protocadherins + Fat / Dachsous
  + Flamingo).

Cytoplasmic linkage:
- β-catenin binds cadherin C-terminus.
- α-catenin links β-catenin to F-actin.
- p120-catenin stabilises cadherin at the
  surface (modulates internalisation).

E-cadherin loss = a hallmark of EMT (epithelial-
mesenchymal transition) → cancer invasion.

### Desmosomes

- High-tensile mechanical resilience (skin,
  cardiac muscle).
- Cadherin-family transmembrane proteins
  desmoglein + desmocollin.
- Cytoplasmic plaque: plakoglobin + plakophilin
  + desmoplakin.
- Anchored to keratin intermediate filaments.

Disease links:
- **Pemphigus vulgaris + foliaceus** —
  autoantibodies to desmoglein 3 + 1; intra-
  epidermal blistering.
- **Arrhythmogenic right ventricular
  cardiomyopathy (ARVC)** — desmoplakin /
  plakophilin / desmoglein 2 mutations.
- **Striate palmoplantar keratoderma** —
  desmoplakin mutations.

### Gap junctions

- Direct cytoplasmic continuity between cells
  for ions + metabolites + sec-messengers up
  to ~ 1 kDa.
- Each hemichannel = hexamer of connexins (6
  subunits); two hemichannels (one per cell)
  dock to form a channel.
- ~ 21 connexins in humans; tissue-specific
  expression.
- Cardiac conduction (Cx43); inner-ear K⁺
  recycling (Cx26 mutations cause non-syndromic
  hearing loss); developmental signalling;
  bystander effects in cancer chemotherapy.
- Pannexins are connexin-related but form mainly
  cell-surface channels (not cell-cell channels);
  signalling roles.

## Cell-ECM adhesion — integrins + focal adhesions

### Integrins

- Heterodimeric αβ transmembrane receptors;
  18 α + 8 β subunits → 24 distinct heterodimers.
- Bind ECM via short peptide motifs:
  - **RGD** (Arg-Gly-Asp) — fibronectin, vitronectin,
    fibrinogen, von Willebrand factor.
  - **LDV** — VCAM-1.
  - **IDV** — fibronectin alternative-spliced.
- **Bidirectional signalling** — outside-in
  (ligand binding triggers intracellular signals)
  + inside-out (intracellular signals modulate
  affinity for ECM).
- "Integrin activation" — talin + kindlin binding
  to β cytoplasmic tail switches integrin from
  low- to high-affinity state.

### Focal adhesions

Multi-protein structures linking integrins to
the actin cytoskeleton + signalling machinery.

Layers (from membrane outward):
1. **Integrin** — at the membrane.
2. **Integrin signalling layer** — talin + kindlin
   + paxillin + FAK + Src.
3. **Force-transduction layer** — vinculin (force-
   dependent recruitment, conformational change).
4. **Actin-regulatory layer** — VASP + zyxin +
   tensin + cortactin.
5. **F-actin** stress fibres anchored to focal
   adhesion.

Focal-adhesion kinase (FAK):
- Auto-phosphorylates Y397 on integrin engagement.
- Recruits Src + PI3K + p130Cas + Crk / Grb2.
- Drives adhesion-dependent signalling (survival,
  migration, proliferation).

Hemidesmosomes — basement-membrane-anchored
counterpart in epithelium (α6β4 integrin + plectin
+ BPAG1 + BPAG2; linked to keratin intermediate
filaments instead of actin).

## The extracellular matrix (ECM)

A 3D network of secreted proteins + glycos-
aminoglycans + glycoproteins that surrounds + supports
cells.

### Major components

#### Collagens

- Most-abundant protein in mammals (~ 30 % of
  body protein).
- Triple-helical Gly-X-Y repeat (X often Pro,
  Y often hydroxyproline).
- 28 distinct collagen types in humans.
- **Type I** — bone, skin, tendon (most abundant).
- **Type II** — cartilage.
- **Type III** — vasculature, reticular fibres.
- **Type IV** — basement membrane.
- **Type V + XI** — assembled with I + II as
  heterotypic fibrils.
- **Type VII** — anchoring fibrils (epidermolysis
  bullosa target).
- **Type X** — hypertrophic cartilage.

Synthesis steps:
1. Pre-procollagen translated + signal-peptide-
   cleaved.
2. Pro-collagen with N + C propeptides assembles
   as triple helix in ER.
3. Pro + Lys hydroxylation by P4H + LH (vitamin
   C-dependent → scurvy).
4. Glycosylation of hydroxylysines.
5. Secretion in COPII vesicles.
6. Pro-peptide cleavage by ADAMTS-2 + BMP1
   extracellularly → tropocollagen.
7. Lysyl oxidase (LOX) cross-linking.
8. Fibrillogenesis (with leucine-rich proteoglycans
   + integrins regulating).

Collagen disorders:
- **Osteogenesis imperfecta** (OI) — COL1A1 / COL1A2
  mutations.
- **Ehlers-Danlos syndromes** (EDS) — multiple
  subtypes; COL3A1 (vascular EDS), COL5A1/2
  (classical EDS), tenascin-X, lysyl hydroxylase.
- **Alport syndrome** — COL4A3/4/5 mutations
  affecting basement-membrane type IV collagen.
- **Bullous diseases** — COL7A1 (DEB), COL17
  (BPAG2 / linear IgA disease).

#### Elastin + microfibrils

- **Elastin** — high-resilience polymer; recoils
  after stretch; dominant in arteries + lung +
  ligaments.
- Cross-linked by lysyl oxidase (desmosines).
- **Fibrillin-1 + fibrillin-2** — microfibril
  scaffolds where elastin polymerises.  Marfan
  syndrome (FBN1 mutations) → aortic aneurysm
  + ectopia lentis + tall stature.

#### Proteoglycans + glycosaminoglycans

- **Glycosaminoglycans (GAGs)** — long unbranched
  polysaccharides:
  - **Heparan sulfate** — many proteoglycans;
    growth-factor sequestration + presentation.
  - **Chondroitin sulfate / dermatan sulfate** —
    cartilage + skin.
  - **Keratan sulfate** — cornea + cartilage.
  - **Hyaluronic acid** — non-sulfated; ground
    substance + embryonic ECM + lubrication
    + drug-delivery vehicle (cosmetic +
    intra-articular).
- **Proteoglycans** — protein cores with attached
  GAGs:
  - Cell-surface — syndecans + glypicans
    (heparan-sulfate proteoglycans).
  - Pericellular — perlecan (basement-membrane
    HS-proteoglycan).
  - ECM — aggrecan (cartilage; massive multi-
    GAG), versican, decorin, biglycan, fibromodulin.

#### Glycoproteins

- **Fibronectin** — RGD-containing; 20+ alternatively-
  spliced variants; cell adhesion + migration +
  wound healing.
- **Laminin** — αβγ trimer; ~ 16 isoforms; key
  basement-membrane component; α-DG + integrin
  receptors.
- **Tenascin** — embryonic + tumour-associated.
- **Thrombospondin** — anti-angiogenic; CD36 +
  CD47 + integrin ligand.
- **Nidogen / entactin** — basement membrane.
- **Osteopontin** — bone, also tumour signalling.

### Basement membrane

A specialised ECM sheet underlying epithelium +
endothelium + surrounding muscle / nerve / adipose.

Major components:
- **Laminin** — heterotrimer; cell-surface
  attachment.
- **Type IV collagen** — network-forming.
- **Nidogen** — bridges laminin + collagen IV.
- **Perlecan** — heparan-sulfate proteoglycan.
- **Agrin** — concentrates AChR at NMJ.

Functions: cell anchorage; barrier (e.g. glomerular
filtration); polarisation; signalling;
differentiation cues.

### Matrix remodelling

- **Matrix metalloproteinases (MMPs)** — > 25
  zinc-dependent endopeptidases.  Collagenases
  (MMP-1, -8, -13), gelatinases (MMP-2, -9),
  stromelysins, matrilysins, MT-MMPs.
  Inhibited by TIMPs.
- **ADAMs + ADAMTS** — disintegrin + metalloprotease
  family; ADAMTS-13 cleaves vWF (TTP).
- **Plasmin / uPA / tPA / PAI-1** — fibrinolytic
  + tissue-remodelling proteases.
- **Cathepsins** — cysteine + serine + aspartate
  proteases of lysosomes; secreted forms remodel
  ECM.
- **LOX + LOXL family** — lysyl oxidases;
  collagen + elastin cross-linking.

## Mechanotransduction

Cells SENSE matrix stiffness + topography +
deformation + convert it to biochemical signals.

Major sensors + pathways:
- **Integrin-FAK-Src signalling** — tension-
  responsive.
- **YAP / TAZ** — Hippo pathway effectors;
  nuclear translocation responds to ECM stiffness
  + cell spreading + actomyosin tension.
- **Piezo1 / Piezo2** — mechano-gated cation
  channels; stretch + shear sensing in
  endothelium + RBC + sensory neurons (Patapoutian
  Nobel 2021).
- **TRPV4** + **TRPC** — other mechano-sensitive
  channels.
- **Stretch-activated K + Cl channels**.
- **Lamin A/C** — nuclear-envelope stiffness;
  mechanotransduction relay to chromatin.

Functional roles:
- Stem-cell differentiation directed by substrate
  stiffness (Engler 2006 — neural on soft, muscle
  on intermediate, bone on stiff).
- Tumour-stiffness-driven malignant phenotype.
- Vascular shear-flow responses.
- Mechanically driven hair-cell + touch + pain
  sensation.

## Cell-ECM in disease

- **Cancer invasion + metastasis** — covered in
  the **CB-4.0 advanced "Cell migration + cancer
  invasion" lesson** (next).
- **Fibrosis** — pathological ECM accumulation;
  myofibroblast differentiation; TGFβ + CTGF +
  PDGF drivers.  Pulmonary fibrosis (IPF), liver
  cirrhosis, scleroderma, kidney fibrosis,
  cardiac fibrosis — major unmet medical need.
- **Atherosclerosis** — vascular ECM remodelling;
  matrix proteoglycans bind LDL.
- **Wound healing dysfunction** — diabetic foot
  ulcers, chronic wounds.
- **Genetic ECM disorders** — OI, EDS, Marfan,
  Alport, epidermolysis bullosa, MPS.
