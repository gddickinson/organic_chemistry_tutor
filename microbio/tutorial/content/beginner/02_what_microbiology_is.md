# What microbiology is

Microbiology is the study of organisms too small to see
with the naked eye — bacteria, archaea, fungi, protists,
and viruses.  These invisible neighbours run the
biosphere: they fix nitrogen, decompose organic matter,
fix carbon, drive the geochemical cycles that sustain
the climate, and live INSIDE us as essential symbionts.

## The five microbial classes

Microbiology spans five biological domains:

- **Bacteria** — prokaryotes (no nucleus); ~ 1-5 µm;
  millions of species; both pathogens + benefactors.
- **Archaea** — also prokaryotes but a separate domain;
  same size scale; ether-linked isoprenoid lipids;
  extremophiles + methanogens; remarkably, no
  archaeal pathogens of humans are known.
- **Fungi** — eukaryotes; from single-celled yeast
  (~ 5-10 µm) to multicellular molds + macroscopic
  mushrooms; chitin cell wall; saprophytes + plant +
  human pathogens.
- **Protists** — eukaryotes; mostly single-celled;
  amoebae, ciliates, flagellates; many parasitic
  (Plasmodium, Trypanosoma, Giardia, Leishmania).
- **Viruses** — non-cellular obligate intracellular
  parasites; not always considered "alive"; range
  from 20-30 nm picornaviruses to 1.5 µm pandoraviruses.

## Why we study microbes

### Disease

Infectious disease was the leading cause of death in
humans for almost all of history.  As of 2026,
infectious disease remains responsible for ~ 14 million
deaths / year globally — even after antibiotics +
vaccines.

The "big seven" infectious-disease killers:
- Lower respiratory infections (~ 2.5 M deaths / year).
- Tuberculosis (~ 1.3 M).
- Diarrhoeal diseases (~ 1.5 M).
- HIV (~ 0.6 M, dramatically reduced from peak).
- Malaria (~ 0.6 M).
- Hepatitis B + C (~ 0.6 M total).
- Sepsis (~ 11 M attributable, overlapping with
  the above).

### Industrial + biotechnology

- **Fermentation** — beer, wine, bread, cheese, yoghurt,
  kimchi, sauerkraut, kombucha (yeasts + LAB +
  acetobacter).
- **Antibiotic production** — most antibiotics are
  natural products from soil actinomycetes
  (*Streptomyces*) + fungi.
- **Industrial enzymes** — laundry proteases, cellulases,
  lipases, phytases.
- **Bioremediation** — oil spill cleanup, heavy-metal
  detoxification, perchlorate reduction.
- **Biofuels** — yeast bioethanol, algal biodiesel,
  cyanobacterial hydrogen.
- **Recombinant protein expression** — insulin (E. coli,
  yeast), monoclonal antibodies (CHO cells), vaccines
  (HEK293, Vero, eggs).

### Microbiome science

Every adult carries ~ 10¹³-10¹⁴ microbial cells —
roughly comparable to our human cell count (the
"10× more microbes" claim is now considered closer to
1:1).  Collectively the human microbiome carries 100×
more genes than our own genome.

Microbiome insights from the past decade:
- Gut microbiota influence immunity, metabolism,
  neurology, cancer-immunotherapy response.
- Faecal microbiota transplantation (FMT) cures
  recurrent *C. difficile* in > 90 %.
- Microbiome composition correlates with obesity, IBD,
  autism, depression, cardiovascular disease, response
  to chemotherapy.
- Skin / oral / vaginal / lung microbiomes each have
  distinct community structures + clinical relevance.

### Ecology + biogeochemistry

Microbes drive global cycles:
- **Carbon** — phytoplankton fix half of global CO2;
  oceans dominated by *Prochlorococcus* + *Synechococcus*.
- **Nitrogen** — N2 fixation, nitrification, denitrification
  — entirely microbial.
- **Sulfur + phosphorus** — microbial cycling drives
  ocean + soil chemistry.
- **Methane** — methanogens (archaea) produce
  ~ 600 Tg / year; methanotrophs consume some.

## A brief history

- **1676** — Antonie van Leeuwenhoek observes "animalcules"
  in pond water with home-made microscopes.
- **1796** — Edward Jenner vaccinates with cowpox
  against smallpox (the word "vaccine" comes from
  *vacca*, cow).
- **1850s-1860s** — Louis Pasteur disproves spontaneous
  generation; Joseph Lister introduces antiseptic
  surgery.
- **1876** — Robert Koch isolates *B. anthracis* +
  formulates Koch's postulates.
- **1880s-1890s** — Golden age of bacteriology:
  cholera, TB, typhoid, plague, diphtheria, tetanus
  agents identified.
- **1892** — Dmitri Ivanovsky discovers tobacco
  mosaic virus by filterable agent.
- **1928** — Alexander Fleming notices penicillin
  inhibition by *Penicillium*.
- **1944** — Avery + MacLeod + McCarty show DNA is
  the transforming principle.
- **1953** — Watson + Crick double helix.
- **1977** — Carl Woese defines Archaea as a third
  domain via 16S rRNA.
- **1995** — Haemophilus influenzae genome (TIGR) —
  first cellular genome sequenced.
- **2003** — SARS coronavirus identified within months.
- **2005-2010** — Human Microbiome Project + Earth
  Microbiome Project transform community microbiology
  via NGS.
- **2012** — Doudna + Charpentier publish CRISPR-Cas9
  as a programmable nuclease.
- **2019-** — SARS-CoV-2 pandemic; mRNA vaccines
  developed in months; CRISPR therapeutics enter
  clinic.

## Modern microbiology toolkit

- **Microscopy** — bright field, phase contrast, DIC,
  fluorescence, confocal, super-resolution, electron
  (TEM, SEM, cryo-EM, cryo-ET).
- **Culture** — selective + differential + chromogenic
  media; chemostats + bioreactors; anaerobic chambers.
- **Identification** — MALDI-TOF (rapid + cheap +
  routine in clinical labs), 16S rRNA sequencing,
  whole-genome sequencing.
- **Molecular** — PCR / qPCR / dPCR, multiplex panels,
  CRISPR-Cas13 diagnostics.
- **Functional** — proteomics, metabolomics,
  transcriptomics, dual-RNA-seq (host + pathogen
  simultaneously).
