# Microbial diversity

The microbial world is vastly more diverse than the
animal + plant + fungal worlds combined.  Most of it
remains uncultivated — recent genomic surveys
estimate ~ 1 trillion microbial species globally.

## The three domains of life

Carl Woese's 1977 16S rRNA phylogeny redrew biology
into THREE domains rather than the older
prokaryote-vs-eukaryote dichotomy:

- **Bacteria** — prokaryotes; ~ 30 phyla cultivated;
  100s more known only from metagenomics.
- **Archaea** — prokaryotes by morphology, but more
  closely related to eukaryotes by genetics.
- **Eukarya** — nucleated cells; the four "kingdoms"
  (animals, plants, fungi, protists) all sit here.

The Asgard archaea (Lokiarchaeota, Thorarchaeota,
Heimdallarchaeota) discovered 2015-2017 are the
closest known prokaryotic relatives of eukaryotes —
the 2-domain "eocyte" hypothesis is now favoured by
many over the classical 3-domain tree.

## Bacterial phyla snapshot

A handful of phyla account for most cultivated +
clinically-relevant species:

- **Pseudomonadota / Proteobacteria** — five classes;
  most gram-negatives.  Includes most clinical
  pathogens (*E. coli*, *Salmonella*, *Klebsiella*,
  *Pseudomonas*, *Helicobacter*, *Neisseria*,
  *Vibrio*, *Brucella*, *Yersinia*, *Bordetella*,
  *Legionella*, *Rickettsia*).
- **Bacillota / Firmicutes** — gram-positives with
  low-GC content (*Staphylococcus*, *Streptococcus*,
  *Enterococcus*, *Bacillus*, *Clostridium*,
  *Listeria*, *Lactobacillus*, *Mycoplasma*).
- **Actinomycetota / Actinobacteria** — high-GC
  gram-positives (*Mycobacterium*, *Corynebacterium*,
  *Streptomyces* — the antibiotic factory,
  *Bifidobacterium*, *Nocardia*).
- **Bacteroidota / Bacteroidetes** — gram-negatives;
  major gut commensals (*Bacteroides*, *Prevotella*).
- **Spirochaetota** — corkscrew-shaped (*Borrelia*,
  *Treponema*, *Leptospira*).
- **Chlamydiota** — obligate intracellular
  (*Chlamydia*, *Chlamydophila*).
- **Cyanobacteria** — oxygenic photosynthesis;
  ancestors of plant chloroplasts.
- **Planctomycetota / Verrucomicrobiota** — anaerobic
  ammonium oxidation; *Akkermansia muciniphila*
  (mucin-degrader, gut-microbiome favourite).

Many phyla are entirely or largely uncultivated +
known only from environmental sequencing (Candidate
Phyla Radiation, encompassing > 70 candidate phyla,
mostly < 1 µm cells with tiny genomes).

## Archaeal diversity

Archaea were originally identified as extremophiles +
methanogens.  We now recognise five major super-
phyla:

- **Euryarchaeota** — methanogens (*Methanobrevibacter*,
  *Methanosarcina*), halophiles (*Halobacterium*),
  thermophiles (*Thermococcus*).
- **Thaumarchaeota / TACK** — ammonia-oxidising
  archaea (AOA); abundant in soil + ocean nitrogen
  cycle.
- **Crenarchaeota** — hyperthermophiles (*Sulfolobus*).
- **Korarchaeota** — hot-spring archaea.
- **Asgard archaea** — sister to eukaryotes; key for
  understanding eukaryogenesis.
- **DPANN** — small-genome archaea, often symbionts /
  parasites of larger archaea.

Notable archaeal niches:
- **Methanogenic** — anoxic sediments, ruminant gut,
  human colon, hydrothermal vents.  Methane production
  is exclusively archaeal — no bacterium does it.
- **Hyperthermophilic** — > 80 °C optima; deep-sea
  vents, hot springs.  *Pyrococcus* + *Pyrolobus*
  + *Methanopyrus* live > 100 °C.
- **Halophilic** — salt lakes, salterns, brines.
  Live > 5 M NaCl.
- **Acidophilic** — *Picrophilus* lives at pH < 0.
- **Anaerobic methane oxidation (ANME)** — partner
  with sulfate-reducing bacteria; consume the methane
  produced at hydrocarbon seeps.

Crucially: **no archaeal pathogens of humans are
known**, despite archaea being abundant in our gut.
Why this should be is one of microbiology's curious
open questions.

## Fungal diversity

Fungi range from single-celled yeasts to multicellular
moulds + macroscopic mushrooms, with > 6 million
species estimated.

Major divisions:
- **Ascomycota** (sac fungi) — yeasts (*S. cerevisiae*,
  *Candida*, *Cryptococcus*), moulds (*Aspergillus*,
  *Penicillium*), filamentous (*Neurospora*), edible
  morels + truffles.
- **Basidiomycota** (club fungi) — mushrooms, smuts,
  rusts, *Cryptococcus neoformans*.
- **Mucoromycota** — mucormycetes (*Rhizopus*,
  *Mucor*).
- **Chytridiomycota** — zoospore-producing aquatic
  fungi; the *Bd* chytrid causes amphibian declines.
- **Microsporidia** — obligate intracellular
  parasites; *Encephalitozoon* + *Enterocytozoon*
  cause opportunistic infections.

Cell architecture:
- Eukaryotic.
- Chitin cell wall.
- Ergosterol membrane (target of azole antifungals).
- Membrane biosynthesis vulnerable: azoles inhibit
  CYP51 (lanosterol 14α-demethylase).
- 1,3-β-D-glucan synthesis vulnerable: echinocandins
  (caspofungin) inhibit FKS-encoded synthase.

Key human-pathogenic fungi:
- **Yeasts** — *Candida albicans / C. glabrata /
  C. auris* (emerging MDR), *Cryptococcus neoformans*
  + *C. gattii* (meningitis).
- **Moulds** — *Aspergillus fumigatus* (invasive
  aspergillosis in immunocompromised),
  *Pneumocystis jirovecii*.
- **Dimorphic fungi** — *Histoplasma*, *Coccidioides*,
  *Blastomyces*, *Paracoccidioides*, *Sporothrix*,
  *Talaromyces marneffei*; switch between mould form
  in soil + yeast form in tissue.
- **Mucormycetes** — *Rhizopus*, *Mucor*; rapidly-
  fatal rhino-orbital-cerebral mucormycosis in
  diabetes / steroids / COVID-19.

## Protist diversity

The most heterogeneous "kingdom" — really a wastebasket
of single-celled eukaryotes.  Key groups:

- **Sarcomastigophora** — flagellates (*Trypanosoma*,
  *Leishmania*, *Trichomonas*, *Giardia*) + amoebae
  (*Entamoeba histolytica*, *Naegleria fowleri*,
  *Acanthamoeba*).
- **Apicomplexa** — *Plasmodium* (malaria —
  *P. falciparum*, *vivax*, *ovale*, *malariae*,
  *knowlesi*), *Toxoplasma gondii*, *Cryptosporidium*,
  *Babesia*, *Cyclospora*.
- **Ciliophora** — *Balantidium coli*.
- **Microsporidia** — now reclassified as fungi.

Major parasitic-disease burdens:
- Malaria — > 200 M cases / year, ~ 600 K deaths.
- Toxoplasmosis — ~ 30 % global seroprevalence.
- Leishmaniasis — > 1 M new cases / year.
- Trypanosomiasis (sleeping sickness, Chagas).

## Helminths (multicellular but worth mentioning)

Strictly outside microbiology but often grouped with
parasitology:
- **Nematodes** — *Ascaris*, *Trichuris*,
  *Strongyloides*, *Necator* / *Ancylostoma*
  (hookworms), filariae (*Wuchereria*,
  *Onchocerca*).
- **Cestodes** (tapeworms) — *Taenia*, *Echinococcus*.
- **Trematodes** (flukes) — *Schistosoma*, *Fasciola*,
  *Clonorchis*.

## Extremophiles + the limits of life

Microbes thrive across an extraordinary parameter
range:
- **Temperature**: psychrophiles in -20 °C brine
  pockets to hyperthermophiles at > 120 °C
  (*Methanopyrus kandleri*).
- **pH**: acidophiles at pH 0 (*Picrophilus*) to
  alkalophiles at pH 13 (*Plectonema*).
- **Salinity**: halophiles to saturated NaCl
  (*Halobacterium*).
- **Pressure**: piezophiles at deep-ocean trench
  > 100 MPa (*Colwellia* etc.).
- **Radiation**: *Deinococcus radiodurans* tolerates
  > 5 000 Gy (vs ~ 5 Gy lethal for humans) by
  efficient DSB repair.
- **Desiccation**: tardigrades + some bacteria
  survive years of dry.

Astrobiology bets on extremophiles as proxies for
possible life elsewhere — Mars, Europa, Enceladus,
Titan.

## How we measure diversity

Old: culture + microscopy + biochemistry.
New: 16S rRNA amplicon sequencing + metagenomics.

The "great plate count anomaly": only ~ 0.1-1 % of
microbes from any environment can be cultivated on
standard media.  Metagenomics revealed the other
99 %.

Modern surveys:
- **Earth Microbiome Project** — > 30 000 environmental
  samples globally.
- **Tara Oceans** — global ocean plankton survey.
- **Human Microbiome Project + iHMP** — 16 body sites
  longitudinally.
- **GenBank + GTDB** — > 700 K bacterial + 30 K
  archaeal genomes (2026).
