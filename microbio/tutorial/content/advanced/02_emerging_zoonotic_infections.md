# Emerging + zoonotic infections

~ 75 % of emerging infectious diseases are zoonotic
— spillover from non-human hosts to humans.  The pace
of emergence has accelerated as humans encroach on
wildlife habitats, intensify livestock production,
and connect globally via travel + trade.

## What "emerging" means

WHO + IOM defined three flavours:
- **Newly emerging** — not previously recognised
  (HIV in 1981, SARS in 2003, MERS in 2012,
  SARS-CoV-2 in 2019).
- **Re-emerging** — previously controlled, returning
  (measles in unvaccinated populations, monkeypox
  outside endemic areas, polio cVDPVs, dengue
  expansion to new latitudes).
- **Deliberately emerging** — bioterrorism release.

## The zoonotic catalogue

### Filoviruses

- **Ebolaviruses** (Zaire, Sudan, Bundibugyo, Tai
  Forest, Reston): hemorrhagic fever; CFR 25-90 %.
  Reservoir bats; spillover via bushmeat + nosocomial.
  rVSV-ZEBOV vaccine (2019) for Zaire ebolavirus.
  Inmazeb + Ebanga monoclonals for Zaire.
- **Marburg** — similar; sporadic; recent outbreaks
  Equatorial Guinea + Tanzania + Rwanda 2023-2024.

### Coronaviruses

- **SARS-CoV** — 2002-2003 outbreak; civet intermediate
  + horseshoe bat reservoir; CFR ~ 10 %; ~ 8 000
  cases globally.
- **MERS-CoV** — 2012-present; dromedary camels
  intermediate / reservoir; CFR ~ 35 %; mostly
  Arabian peninsula + nosocomial bursts.
- **SARS-CoV-2** — 2019-present; horseshoe bat
  ancestral; pangolin intermediate (uncertain);
  > 7 M reported deaths globally + many more excess.

Pandemic preparedness now actively tracks novel
sarbecoviruses + merbecoviruses for spillover risk.

### Henipaviruses

- **Hendra virus** — Australia, 1994; flying-fox
  reservoir; horse intermediate; > 50 % CFR in
  humans (rare).
- **Nipah virus** — Malaysia 1998 (pig intermediate);
  Bangladesh + India recurring (date-palm sap
  contamination); 40-75 % CFR; respiratory + neural;
  pandemic potential under WHO blueprint.
- **Langya virus** (LayV) — 2022 China; shrew
  reservoir; mild illness so far.

### Arenaviruses

- **Lassa fever** — West Africa; *Mastomys natalensis*
  rodent reservoir; ~ 100-300 K cases / year; ribavirin
  treatment; vaccine in trials.
- **Argentine** + **Bolivian** + **Brazilian** + **Venezuelan**
  hemorrhagic fevers — endemic to specific regions.
- **Lujo virus** — Zambia / South Africa, 2008.

### Hantaviruses

- **Hemorrhagic fever with renal syndrome (HFRS)** —
  Eurasia; *Hantaan / Seoul / Puumala / Dobrava*
  serotypes; rodent reservoirs.
- **Hantavirus pulmonary syndrome (HPS)** — Americas;
  *Sin Nombre / Andes / Bayou / Black Creek Canal*;
  CFR ~ 35 %.

### Bunyaviruses

- **Crimean-Congo hemorrhagic fever** — tick-borne;
  Eurasia + Africa; CFR 10-40 %; ribavirin partial
  benefit.
- **Rift Valley fever** — Africa + Middle East;
  mosquito + livestock-contact; livestock vaccine.
- **Severe fever with thrombocytopenia syndrome
  (SFTS)** — China + Korea + Japan; tick-borne; CFR
  10-30 %.

### Flaviviruses

- **Zika** — 2015-2016 Americas outbreak; congenital
  microcephaly + Guillain-Barré; mostly cleared from
  endemic threat though ongoing low-level transmission.
- **Dengue** — > 100 M symptomatic cases / year;
  expanding range; 4 serotypes; primary vs secondary
  infection severity; Dengvaxia + Qdenga vaccines.
- **Yellow fever** — Africa + Americas; sylvatic +
  urban cycles; effective 17D vaccine.
- **West Nile** — Old World + Americas; *Culex*
  mosquitoes; mostly subclinical; ~ 1 / 150 develops
  neuroinvasive disease.
- **Japanese encephalitis** — Asia; pig + bird
  reservoirs; vaccine.
- **Tick-borne encephalitis** — Eurasia; vaccines.
- **Powassan** — North America; emerging.

### Paramyxoviruses

- **Measles** — re-emerging globally where vaccine
  coverage gaps; R0 12-18.
- **Mumps** — periodic re-emergence in vaccinated
  populations (waning immunity).
- **Rubella** — eliminated in some regions.
- **RSV** — recurring annual outbreaks.

### Mpox

- Family Poxviridae, Orthopoxvirus genus.
- Two clades: clade I (Congo Basin, more severe) +
  clade II (West African, less severe).
- 2022 global outbreak (clade IIb) — > 90 K cases in
  > 100 countries; primarily MSM transmission.
- 2024 clade I outbreak in DRC + neighbours; PHEIC
  declared again.
- Jynneos vaccine (modified vaccinia Ankara) +
  tecovirimat (TPOXX) treatment.

### Avian influenza

- **H5N1** highly-pathogenic avian influenza ongoing
  pandemic-potential threat; sporadic human cases
  with high CFR.
- **H7N9** China 2013-present.
- **H5N1 in dairy cattle** (US 2024) — unusual
  spillover with low-pathogenicity human cases.

### Others on watchlists

- Rift Valley fever expansion.
- Zika resurgence.
- Chikungunya, Mayaro, Oropouche.
- Plague (*Y. pestis*) — endemic in many regions;
  rare outbreaks (Madagascar 2017).
- Q fever (*Coxiella burnetii*) — livestock-associated.
- Brucellosis — livestock + dairy.
- Leptospirosis — water + rodent contact.
- Tularemia (*Francisella tularensis*) — rabbits,
  ticks.

## One Health framework

The recognition that human + animal + environmental
health are interconnected.  Drives:
- **Surveillance integration** — animal + human +
  environmental signals.
- **Vector-borne disease ecology**.
- **Antimicrobial use stewardship** across human +
  veterinary + agricultural sectors.
- **Spillover prediction** — Disease X / pandemic
  preparedness.

WHO Quadripartite (WHO + FAO + WOAH + UNEP)
coordinates global One Health activities.

## Spillover ecology

Risk factors for novel zoonotic emergence:
- Bat reservoir (filoviruses, henipaviruses, sarbeco-
  viruses, lyssaviruses).
- Wildlife + livestock interface (poultry markets,
  pig farming, camel husbandry).
- Forest fragmentation + bushmeat trade.
- Climate change shifting vector ranges.
- Anthropogenic activities — mining, dam-building,
  urbanisation.
- Intensive livestock production.

Predictors:
- **DAMA** (D = donor; A = adaptation; M = movement;
  A = adaptation in humans).
- **EcoHealth Alliance** + **PREDICT** + **PREPARE**
  programmes survey wildlife viromes.

## Pandemic preparedness landscape

### WHO blueprint priority pathogens

Updated list:
- COVID-19 (SARS-CoV-2 + sarbecoviruses).
- Crimean-Congo HF.
- Ebola virus disease + Marburg.
- Lassa fever.
- MERS + SARS.
- Nipah + henipavirus.
- Rift Valley fever.
- Zika.
- "Disease X" — unknown future.

### CEPI 100-day mission

Develop a vaccine for any new pandemic pathogen
within 100 days of recognition.  Driven by:
- Pre-existing platform investment (mRNA, viral
  vector, protein subunit).
- Prototype-pathogen vaccine portfolios.
- Standby manufacturing capacity.
- Regulatory science (rolling reviews).

### Pandemic Treaty + IHR amendments

WHO member-state negotiations for global pandemic
agreement + revised International Health
Regulations.  Pathogen sharing + vaccine equity +
surveillance + financing key issues.

### National pandemic preparedness

US: HHS + ASPR + BARDA; Project NextGen.  EU: HERA.
UK: UKHSA.  Most G20 countries have pandemic
preparedness plans.

### Surveillance technology

- **Global Influenza Surveillance + Response System
  (GISRS)** + **GISAID** — virological surveillance.
- **PathoGenN + GenomeTrakr** — pathogen genomics.
- **Wastewater surveillance** — SARS-CoV-2 + polio
  + mpox.
- **Syndromic + EHR-based** — early-warning systems
  (BlueDot, ProMED, HealthMap, EIOS).
- **Wildlife genomic surveillance** — HHS + SPILLOVER
  + USAID PREDICT (programme curtailment in 2020 a
  policy concern).

## Outbreak response

Typical sequence:
1. **Detection** — alert from clinicians + lab
   surveillance + ProMED.
2. **Verification** — rapid characterisation (PCR,
   sequencing, serology).
3. **Risk assessment** — IHR Annex 2 decision tree.
4. **Containment** — isolation + contact tracing +
   PPE + IPC.
5. **Treatment** — supportive + specific
   therapeutics if available.
6. **Vaccination** — ring vaccination (rVSV-ZEBOV
   in Ebola; smallpox elimination paradigm).
7. **Communication** — risk communication + community
   engagement.
8. **Long-term** — vaccine / drug development +
   surveillance maintenance.

## Lessons from past outbreaks

- **HIV** — slow recognition (years); decades of
  research; ART transformation; ongoing prevention
  challenges.
- **SARS 2003** — fast control via classical IPC;
  vaccine never deployed before extinction.
- **H1N1 2009** — vaccine ready ~ 6 months; mortality
  modest.
- **MERS** — limited human-to-human; nosocomial
  amplification; sporadic continuing.
- **Ebola West Africa 2013-2016** — > 11 K deaths;
  rVSV-ZEBOV deployed in late stages + DRC outbreaks
  since.
- **Zika 2015-2016** — congenital microcephaly
  recognised retrospectively.
- **COVID-19** — global; mRNA vaccine triumph;
  policy + equity failures; long COVID legacy.
- **Mpox 2022** — sexual transmission network
  recognised; ring + targeted vaccination + behaviour
  change reduced incidence.
