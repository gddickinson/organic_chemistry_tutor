# Mycology + parasitology

Bacteria + viruses dominate clinical attention but
fungi + parasites contribute disproportionately to
mortality + morbidity, particularly in low-resource
settings + in immunocompromised patients.  These two
fields share a methodological + therapeutic toolkit
distinct from bacteriology.

## Mycology — fungal infections

### The fungal pathogen landscape

GAFFI estimates > 300 million people suffer serious
fungal infections each year + > 1.6 million deaths.
Yet fungi receive a fraction of the research +
clinical attention given to bacteria.  Recent WHO
prioritisation of fungal pathogens (2022) is a
welcome correction.

### Categories of clinical mycosis

- **Superficial** — *Malassezia* (pityriasis
  versicolor), *Trichophyton* + *Microsporum* +
  *Epidermophyton* (dermatophytes — tinea capitis /
  corporis / cruris / pedis / unguium).
- **Subcutaneous** — sporotrichosis (*Sporothrix
  schenckii*), chromoblastomycosis, mycetoma
  (eumycetoma).
- **Endemic dimorphic mycoses** — geographic.
- **Opportunistic / invasive** — risk-population-
  specific.
- **Allergic** — ABPA, fungal sinusitis.

### Endemic dimorphic mycoses

Mould → yeast morphological transition during
infection.  Geographic + immune status determine
disease spectrum.

- **Histoplasma capsulatum** — Mississippi + Ohio
  river valleys + Caribbean + Central America;
  bird / bat guano associated.
- **Blastomyces dermatitidis** — North American
  midwest + south-east + Great Lakes.
- **Coccidioides immitis / posadasii** — Southwest
  US + Mexico + South America (Valley fever).
- **Paracoccidioides brasiliensis** — South America.
- **Talaromyces marneffei** — Southeast Asia
  (esp. HIV).
- **Sporothrix schenckii** — global; rose-gardener's
  disease.

Treatment: itraconazole or voriconazole / posaconazole
for mild-moderate; amphotericin B for severe.

### Opportunistic invasive mycoses

#### Candida species

- *C. albicans* — most common; oropharyngeal /
  oesophageal / vulvovaginal / intertriginous /
  invasive candidaemia.
- *C. glabrata* — increasing fluconazole resistance.
- *C. krusei* — intrinsic fluconazole resistance.
- *C. parapsilosis* — central-line-associated.
- *C. tropicalis* — tropical.
- ***C. auris*** — emerging MDR pathogen since 2009;
  hospital outbreaks; fluconazole + amphotericin
  + echinocandin resistance increasingly reported;
  infection-control nightmare.

Treatment: echinocandins (caspofungin, micafungin,
anidulafungin) first-line for invasive disease;
fluconazole step-down if susceptible; amphotericin
for refractory.

#### Aspergillus

- *A. fumigatus* dominant; *A. flavus* +
  *A. terreus* + *A. niger* + *A. nidulans*.
- Spectrum: ABPA → aspergilloma → chronic pulmonary
  aspergillosis → invasive (immunocompromised).
- Voriconazole + isavuconazole first-line for
  invasive; amphotericin for salvage; combination
  with echinocandin sometimes.
- Azole-resistant *A. fumigatus* (TR34 / TR46
  mutations from agricultural azole use) is a
  growing concern.

#### Cryptococcus

- *C. neoformans* + *C. gattii*.
- Encapsulated yeast; pigeon / tree-bark reservoirs.
- Meningitis dominant clinical presentation,
  especially in HIV.
- 10-week intensive treatment: amphotericin +
  flucytosine → fluconazole consolidation.
- Single-dose liposomal amphotericin (AMBITION
  trial) shifting standard of care.

#### Pneumocystis jirovecii

- Cyst-forming fungus; HIV-defining illness.
- Treatment: TMP-SMX (oral / IV) + adjunctive
  steroids if PaO2 < 70 or A-a > 35.
- Prophylaxis with TMP-SMX in CD4 < 200 or other
  immunosuppression.

#### Mucormycetes (mucor / rhizopus / lichtheimia)

- Rhino-orbital-cerebral or pulmonary or cutaneous
  or GI.
- Diabetes / DKA, steroid use, neutropenia, post-
  COVID surge.
- Rapidly fatal without urgent surgical debridement
  + amphotericin / posaconazole / isavuconazole.

### Antifungal classes summary

- **Polyenes** — amphotericin B (deoxycholate, lipid
  complex, liposomal); broad; nephrotoxic.
- **Azoles** (CYP51 inhibitors) — fluconazole
  (Candida + Cryptococcus), itraconazole (broader,
  endemic), voriconazole (Aspergillus standard,
  CYP-mediated DDIs + visual + hepatotox), posaconazole
  (broad including mucor), isavuconazole (Aspergillus +
  mucor).
- **Echinocandins** (1,3-β-D-glucan synthase
  inhibitors) — caspofungin, micafungin, anidulafungin;
  Candida + invasive aspergillosis.  Rezafungin =
  long-acting (weekly dosing).
- **Allylamines** (squalene epoxidase) — terbinafine
  (dermatophytes).
- **Pyrimidine analogue** — flucytosine (5-FC) →
  5-FU; combination only (rapid resistance as
  monotherapy).
- **Newer** — fosmanogepix (GPI-anchor synthesis),
  olorofim (DHODH), opelconazole (inhaled),
  ibrexafungerp (oral β-glucan).

## Parasitology

Two major branches:
- **Protozoa** — single-celled eukaryotes.
- **Helminths** — multicellular worms.
- (Arthropods — ticks, mites, lice — also clinically
  relevant.)

### Major protozoal infections

#### Malaria — *Plasmodium*

- *P. falciparum* (most severe), *P. vivax*, *P.
  ovale*, *P. malariae*, *P. knowlesi*.
- ~ 247 M cases / year, ~ 600 K deaths (2021), mostly
  African children < 5.
- Anopheles mosquito transmission.
- Drugs:
  - **Artemisinin combination therapy (ACT)** —
    artemether-lumefantrine, artesunate-amodiaquine,
    artesunate-mefloquine, dihydroartemisinin-
    piperaquine — first-line.
  - **Chloroquine** — historical, useful for
    chloroquine-sensitive *P. vivax / ovale /
    malariae* + *P. falciparum* (rare).
  - **Quinine + doxycycline** — second-line.
  - **Atovaquone-proguanil** (Malarone) — prophylaxis
    + treatment.
  - **Mefloquine** — prophylaxis (neuropsych SE).
  - **Primaquine + tafenoquine** — radical cure for
    *vivax / ovale* hypnozoites; G6PD-screen first.
  - **Artemisinin resistance** spreading from
    Southeast Asia to Africa (PfK13 mutations).
- Vaccines: RTS,S (Mosquirix) + R21 (Matrix-M);
  partial efficacy; multi-dose.

#### Leishmaniasis — *Leishmania*

- Cutaneous, mucocutaneous, visceral (kala-azar)
  forms.
- Sandfly transmission.
- ~ 700 K-1 M new cases / year.
- Drugs: liposomal amphotericin (visceral first-line),
  miltefosine (oral), pentavalent antimony
  (historical), paromomycin.

#### Trypanosomiasis

- **African trypanosomiasis (sleeping sickness)** —
  *T. brucei gambiense* + *rhodesiense*; tsetse fly.
  Treatment: nifurtimox-eflornithine (NECT) for
  late-stage gambiense; fexinidazole (oral, single
  course) since 2019.
- **American trypanosomiasis (Chagas)** — *T. cruzi*;
  triatomine bug.  Treatment: benznidazole or
  nifurtimox; better in early phase.

#### Toxoplasmosis — *Toxoplasma gondii*

- ~ 30 % global seroprevalence.
- Cats definitive host; humans incidental.
- Severe in congenital + immunocompromised
  (encephalitis in AIDS).
- Treatment: pyrimethamine + sulfadiazine + leucovorin
  (severe / immunocompromised); spiramycin in
  pregnancy.

#### Other protozoa

- **Giardia lamblia** — water-borne diarrhoea;
  metronidazole / tinidazole / nitazoxanide.
- **Entamoeba histolytica** — amoebic dysentery +
  liver abscess; metronidazole + paromomycin.
- **Cryptosporidium** — water-borne; severe in HIV;
  nitazoxanide.
- **Cyclospora** — Cayetanensis; raspberry / herb
  outbreaks; TMP-SMX.
- **Trichomonas vaginalis** — STI; metronidazole.
- **Naegleria fowleri** — primary amoebic
  meningoencephalitis; almost uniformly fatal;
  miltefosine / amphotericin combination.
- **Babesia** — tick-borne; immunocompromised /
  asplenic; atovaquone + azithromycin or quinine +
  clindamycin.

### Major helminth infections

#### Nematodes

- **Ascaris lumbricoides** — > 800 M infected;
  faecal-oral; treat with albendazole / mebendazole.
- **Trichuris trichiura** (whipworm) — ~ 600 M.
- **Hookworms** (*Necator americanus / Ancylostoma
  duodenale*) — ~ 400 M; iron-deficiency anaemia.
- **Strongyloides stercoralis** — autoinfection
  cycle; hyperinfection in immunocompromised
  (steroid users); ivermectin.
- **Enterobius vermicularis** (pinworm) — paediatric.
- **Filaria** —
  - **Wuchereria bancrofti / Brugia** — lymphatic
    filariasis (elephantiasis); DEC + albendazole +
    ivermectin.
  - **Onchocerca volvulus** — river blindness;
    ivermectin (annual mass administration; APOC).
  - **Loa loa** — eye worm; can complicate ivermectin.

#### Cestodes (tapeworms)

- **Taenia solium** (pork) — neurocysticercosis.
- **Taenia saginata** (beef).
- **Hymenolepis nana** — small dwarf tapeworm.
- **Echinococcus granulosus** — hydatid disease;
  surgery + albendazole.
- **Echinococcus multilocularis** — alveolar; severe.
- Treatment: praziquantel + albendazole.

#### Trematodes (flukes)

- **Schistosoma mansoni / haematobium / japonicum** —
  schistosomiasis; ~ 250 M infected; mass praziquantel
  in endemic regions.
- **Fasciola hepatica / gigantica** — liver flukes;
  triclabendazole.
- **Clonorchis sinensis** + **Opisthorchis viverrini**
  — cholangiocarcinoma risk in Asia.
- **Paragonimus** — lung fluke.

### Anthelmintic drug classes

- **Benzimidazoles** (albendazole, mebendazole,
  thiabendazole) — broad nematode + tissue
  cestode + flukicidal at high dose.
- **Macrocyclic lactones** (ivermectin, moxidectin)
  — filariae, scabies, river blindness, *Strongyloides*.
- **Pyrazinoisoquinoline** (praziquantel) — flukes +
  cestodes.
- **Triclabendazole** — *Fasciola*.
- **Pyrantel pamoate** — pinworm + roundworm.
- **Diethylcarbamazine (DEC)** — lymphatic filariasis,
  loiasis.
- **Nitazoxanide** — protozoa + helminths + viruses
  (broad).

## Neglected tropical diseases (NTDs)

WHO list of 20 NTDs — many parasitic — that
disproportionately affect poor populations:
chagas, lymphatic filariasis, leishmaniasis,
onchocerciasis, schistosomiasis, soil-transmitted
helminths, trachoma, leprosy, HAT, dengue, rabies,
mycetoma, scabies, snakebite envenoming, taeniasis /
cysticercosis, food-borne trematodiases, dracunculiasis,
yaws, Buruli ulcer, mycoses (added 2017).

Mass drug administration (MDA) programmes have
slashed prevalence:
- **Onchocerciasis** — APOC + OEPA — near-elimination
  in Americas + several African countries.
- **Lymphatic filariasis** — > 740 M people treated;
  17 countries eliminated.
- **STH** — > 600 M children treated annually.
- **Schistosomiasis** — 2030 elimination targets.
- **Dracunculiasis (guinea worm)** — < 30 cases / year
  globally; on the verge of eradication (Carter
  Center programme).
