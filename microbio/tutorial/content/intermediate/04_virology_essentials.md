# Virology essentials

Viruses are obligate intracellular parasites with no
metabolism of their own — yet they outnumber
cellular organisms in the biosphere by ~ 10:1 (most
of them bacteriophages).  Clinically, viruses cause
~ 80 % of acute infections, the majority of pandemics,
and a growing share of cancers.

## What is a virus?

Minimal architecture:
- **Nucleic-acid genome** — DNA or RNA, single- or
  double-stranded, linear or circular, 1.7 kb (HBV,
  hepatitis delta) to ~ 2.5 Mb (pandoravirus).
- **Capsid** — proteinaceous shell (icosahedral,
  helical, or complex).
- **Envelope** (in ~ 50 % of human-pathogenic viruses)
  — host-derived lipid bilayer with viral glycoproteins.
- **Sometimes accessory proteins** for replication or
  immune evasion.

That's it.  No ribosomes, no metabolism, no
homeostasis.

## Baltimore classification

David Baltimore's 1971 framework groups viruses by
genome strategy + how they make mRNA:

| Class | Genome | Examples |
|-------|--------|----------|
| I | dsDNA | herpesviruses, adenoviruses, papovaviruses, poxviruses |
| II | (+)ssDNA | parvoviruses |
| III | dsRNA | reoviruses (rotavirus) |
| IV | (+)ssRNA | picornaviruses (polio, rhinovirus, HAV), flaviviruses (Dengue, Zika, WNV, HCV), coronaviruses (SARS-CoV-2), togaviruses (Rubella, Chikungunya), caliciviruses (norovirus) |
| V | (-)ssRNA | orthomyxoviruses (influenza), paramyxoviruses (measles, mumps, RSV), rhabdoviruses (rabies), filoviruses (Ebola, Marburg), bunyaviruses (Hantavirus), arenaviruses (Lassa) |
| VI | (+)ssRNA-RT | retroviruses (HIV, HTLV) |
| VII | dsDNA-RT | hepadnaviruses (HBV) |

Classes IV (positive-sense ssRNA) is the largest in
human disease (just translate the genome on entry).

## The viral life cycle (generic)

1. **Attachment** — viral protein binds host receptor.
2. **Entry / penetration** — fusion (enveloped) or
   endocytosis.
3. **Uncoating** — release genome into cytoplasm or
   nucleus.
4. **Replication** — viral genome copied by host
   and / or viral polymerase.
5. **Translation** — viral mRNAs translated by host
   ribosomes.
6. **Assembly** — capsid + nucleic acid packaging.
7. **Maturation** — viral protease cleavage of
   polyproteins.
8. **Release** — budding (enveloped) or lysis.

The whole cycle takes 6-72 hours depending on virus
+ cell.

## Receptor + tropism examples

- **HIV** — CD4 + CCR5 (or CXCR4) → T-helpers,
  macrophages.
- **SARS-CoV-2** — ACE2 + TMPRSS2 → respiratory
  epithelium, vascular endothelium, gut.
- **Influenza** — α2,6-sialic acid (human upper
  airway) vs α2,3-sialic acid (avian gut + human
  lower airway).
- **Rabies** — nicotinic AChR + others → CNS
  via axonal transport.
- **HBV** — NTCP (Na+-taurocholate cotransporting
  polypeptide) → hepatocytes.
- **HCV** — CD81 + claudin-1 + occludin + SR-BI →
  hepatocytes.
- **EBV** — CD21 + HLA class II → B cells.
- **Measles** — CD150 / SLAM + nectin-4 → lymphocytes
  + epithelium.
- **Norovirus** — histo-blood-group antigens (HBGA)
  on intestinal epithelium.
- **Rotavirus** — sialic acid + integrins.
- **HSV** — nectin-1/2 + HVEM.

Tropism explains organ-specific disease + transmission
routes.

## Lytic vs lysogenic

For phages + some animal viruses:
- **Lytic** — replicate + lyse the cell.
- **Lysogenic / latent** — integrate or remain as
  episome; periodic reactivation.

Latency examples in human viruses:
- **HSV-1 / HSV-2** — sensory ganglia.
- **VZV** — dorsal root ganglia → reactivates as
  shingles.
- **EBV** — B cells (lifelong).
- **CMV** — myeloid lineage (lifelong).
- **HHV-8 (KSHV)** — B cells + endothelium.
- **HIV** — integrated provirus in CD4+ memory T cells
  → the reservoir that prevents cure.
- **HBV** — cccDNA in hepatocyte nuclei.

## Antiviral drug classes

### Nucleoside / nucleotide analogues

Mimic natural NTPs; chain-terminate or cause lethal
mutations.

- **HSV / VZV** — acyclovir, valacyclovir,
  famciclovir, penciclovir.  Phosphorylated by viral
  TK → selective activation in infected cells.
- **CMV** — ganciclovir, valganciclovir, foscarnet
  (non-nucleotide).
- **HBV** — entecavir, tenofovir (TDF, TAF).
- **HCV** — sofosbuvir (NS5B inhibitor).
- **HIV — NRTIs** — zidovudine (AZT), tenofovir,
  emtricitabine, lamivudine, abacavir, didanosine
  (historical).
- **Influenza** — favipiravir (RdRp inhibitor; broad
  spectrum, in some markets).
- **SARS-CoV-2** — molnupiravir (mutagen),
  remdesivir (RdRp NTP analogue).

### Non-nucleoside polymerase inhibitors

- **HIV — NNRTIs** — efavirenz, etravirine,
  rilpivirine, doravirine.
- **HCV — NS5B NNRTIs** — dasabuvir.
- **Ebola / Marburg** — galidesivir, GS-5734
  experimental.

### Protease inhibitors

- **HIV PIs** — atazanavir, darunavir, lopinavir,
  ritonavir (now used as a CYP3A4 booster).
- **HCV PIs** — grazoprevir, glecaprevir, voxilaprevir.
- **SARS-CoV-2 PIs** — nirmatrelvir (Paxlovid; with
  ritonavir boost).

### Integrase strand transfer inhibitors (INSTIs)

- **HIV** — raltegravir, elvitegravir (CYP-boosted),
  dolutegravir, bictegravir, cabotegravir
  (long-acting injectable).

### NS5A inhibitors

- **HCV** — ledipasvir, daclatasvir, velpatasvir,
  pibrentasvir.
- Combination DAA (direct-acting antiviral) regimens
  cure > 95 % of HCV in 8-12 weeks — among the
  greatest pharmacological achievements of the 2010s.

### Fusion / entry inhibitors

- **HIV** — enfuvirtide (T-20, gp41 fusion peptide
  mimic), maraviroc (CCR5 antagonist), fostemsavir
  (gp120 attachment), ibalizumab (CD4 mAb).
- **RSV** — palivizumab (F protein mAb prophylaxis),
  nirsevimab (extended-half-life mAb).
- **SARS-CoV-2** — many spike-targeting mAbs
  (largely obsolete due to variant escape).

### Neuraminidase inhibitors (influenza)

- Oseltamivir (Tamiflu), zanamivir (Relenza), peramivir
  (Rapivab), laninamivir (Asia).
- Block release of progeny virions from infected
  cells.

### Cap-snatching endonuclease inhibitor

- **Baloxavir marboxil** — single-dose influenza
  treatment; PA endonuclease inhibitor.

### Capsid inhibitors

- **HIV — lenacapavir** — long-acting (every 6 months)
  capsid-targeting drug.
- **HBV — capsid assembly modulators (CAMs)** — in
  development.

## Major human pathogenic viruses

### Influenza

- Segmented (-)ssRNA orthomyxovirus.
- Genome reassortment + antigenic drift / shift drive
  ongoing seasonal epidemics + pandemics (1918, 1957,
  1968, 2009, plus zoonotic risk H5N1, H7N9).
- Annual vaccination (TIV / QIV); LAIV nasal.
- Antivirals: oseltamivir, baloxavir.
- Pandemic preparedness — universal flu vaccines in
  development (Mt Sinai chimaeric HA, mRNA-based).

### HIV

- (+)ssRNA-RT retrovirus.
- 38 M living with HIV globally; ~ 600 K deaths /
  year (down from peak > 1.7 M).
- Standard of care: ART (3-drug regimens; INSTI-based
  preferred — e.g. bictegravir/TAF/FTC).
- PrEP: tenofovir/emtricitabine oral or cabotegravir
  long-acting injectable.
- Cure attempts: stem-cell transplant from CCR5-Δ32
  donors (Berlin + London + Düsseldorf patients —
  ~ 5 documented cures); broadly neutralising
  antibodies + latency-reversing agents in trials;
  CRISPR excision of provirus in clinical trials.

### Hepatitis viruses

- **HAV** — picornavirus; faecal-oral; vaccine.
- **HBV** — hepadnavirus; blood / sexual / vertical;
  vaccine; treat with TAF / TDF / entecavir; chronic
  HBV → cirrhosis / HCC; functional cure rare; cccDNA
  the reservoir.
- **HCV** — flavivirus; blood-borne; DAAs cure > 95
  % in 8-12 weeks.
- **HDV** — requires HBsAg coat; super-infection in
  HBV+; severe; bulevirtide (NTCP entry inhibitor)
  approved EU.
- **HEV** — picornavirus-like; faecal-oral;
  high mortality in pregnancy.

### Herpesviruses

8 human herpesviruses; lifelong latency:
- **HSV-1, HSV-2** — orolabial + genital herpes;
  acyclovir family.
- **VZV** — chickenpox + shingles; vaccine.
- **CMV** — congenital + transplant + AIDS;
  ganciclovir.
- **EBV** — mononucleosis + Burkitt + nasopharyngeal
  + Hodgkin + post-transplant lymphoproliferative
  disorders + multiple sclerosis association.
- **HHV-6, HHV-7** — roseola + reactivation in
  transplant.
- **HHV-8 (KSHV)** — Kaposi sarcoma + primary
  effusion lymphoma + multicentric Castleman.

### SARS-CoV-2

- (+)ssRNA coronavirus.
- Spike-ACE2 entry; TMPRSS2 priming.
- Variants of concern: Alpha, Delta, Omicron + sub-
  lineages.
- Vaccines: mRNA, viral vector, protein subunit;
  bivalent + monovalent updates.
- Antivirals: nirmatrelvir/ritonavir (Paxlovid),
  remdesivir, molnupiravir.
- Long COVID / PASC — major ongoing public-health
  burden.

### Common respiratory viruses

- **Rhinoviruses** — > 100 serotypes; common cold;
  no vaccine.
- **RSV** — major paediatric + elderly pathogen;
  nirsevimab + maternal vaccination + adult
  vaccines transformed prevention 2023+.
- **Adenoviruses** — > 50 serotypes; pharyngo-
  conjunctival fever, ARDS in military recruits;
  type-7 + type-4 vaccines for military.
- **Parainfluenza, metapneumovirus** — paediatric
  respiratory.
- **Coronaviruses** (HCoV-OC43, 229E, NL63, HKU1) —
  common cold; SARS, MERS, SARS-CoV-2 are emergent.

### Common GI viruses

- **Norovirus** — faecal-oral; rapid outbreaks; no
  vaccine; mRNA + viral vector candidates.
- **Rotavirus** — vaccine has dramatically reduced
  paediatric mortality.
- **Adenovirus 40/41**.

### Pandemic potential

- **Influenza** (zoonotic spillover from birds /
  pigs).
- **Coronaviruses** (SARS, MERS, SARS-CoV-2 +
  potential future).
- **Filoviruses** (Ebola, Marburg).
- **Henipaviruses** (Nipah, Hendra).
- **Arenaviruses** (Lassa).
- **Bunyaviruses** (Crimean-Congo HF, hantavirus,
  Rift Valley).
- **Mpox** (clade I + II outbreaks 2022-2024).

## Phages — the other 99 %

Bacteriophages are the most abundant biological
entities on Earth (~ 10³¹ globally).  They:
- Drive bacterial evolution via transduction +
  selection.
- Enable phage therapy (graduate-tier MB-3.0 lesson).
- Encode CRISPR-Cas defences in their bacterial
  hosts (also graduate-tier).
- Used as cloning + display + vaccine vectors.
