# Vaccines

Vaccines are the most cost-effective public-health
intervention ever developed.  They have eliminated
smallpox + nearly eliminated polio + drastically
reduced measles + diphtheria + pertussis + Hib +
pneumococcal + HPV + hepatitis B + rotavirus + mortality
worldwide.

## How vaccines work

Antigen exposure → adaptive immune response → memory
B + T cells.  Subsequent encounter with the actual
pathogen triggers rapid recall response → infection
prevented or attenuated.

Key parameters:
- **Efficacy** (clinical-trial-measured infection /
  disease prevention).
- **Effectiveness** (real-world prevention).
- **Duration** of protection.
- **Reactogenicity** (local + systemic side effects).
- **Cost + cold chain** (transport + storage).
- **Population coverage** + herd-immunity threshold.

## The major vaccine platforms

### Live attenuated

Weakened replicating pathogen.  Strong + long-lasting
immunity (often single-dose).  Cannot be given to
immunocompromised (replication risks reversion or
dissemination).

Examples:
- **MMR** (measles, mumps, rubella).
- **Varicella** (chickenpox) + **zoster** (shingles —
  Zostavax legacy; Shingrix recombinant + adjuvanted is
  now preferred).
- **Rotavirus** (Rotarix, RotaTeq).
- **Yellow fever** (17D).
- **Oral polio (OPV)** — being phased out (vaccine-
  associated paralysis + cVDPVs).
- **BCG** — Mycobacterium bovis attenuated.
- **Smallpox** (vaccinia) + **mpox** (Jynneos).
- **Influenza (LAIV)** — FluMist.
- **Typhoid Ty21a** oral.

### Inactivated whole-pathogen

Killed bacteria or inactivated viruses.  Generally
safer than live but less immunogenic + multiple doses
+ adjuvants needed.

Examples:
- **Inactivated polio (IPV)** — Salk vaccine.
- **Hepatitis A**.
- **Influenza (TIV / QIV)** — split + subunit.
- **Rabies**.
- **Tick-borne encephalitis**.
- **Cholera** (oral inactivated WC-rBS).
- **Pertussis (whole-cell wP)** — older formulation,
  replaced by acellular in most high-income countries.

### Subunit / recombinant protein

Purified or recombinant antigen — no whole pathogen.
Safer for immunocompromised; less immunogenic — needs
adjuvants + boosters.

Examples:
- **Hepatitis B** — recombinant HBsAg.
- **HPV** (Gardasil 4 / 9, Cervarix) — virus-like
  particles assembled from L1 capsid protein.
- **Acellular pertussis (aP)** — purified PT, FHA,
  pertactin, fimbriae.
- **Tetanus + diphtheria toxoids** — formalin-
  inactivated toxins.
- **Shingrix** — recombinant gE + AS01B adjuvant.
- **Novavax SARS-CoV-2 (NVX-CoV2373)** — nanoparticle
  Spike + Matrix-M adjuvant.
- **RSV vaccines** (Arexvy GSK, Abrysvo Pfizer,
  Moderna mResvia mRNA) — pre-fusion stabilised F
  protein.

### Conjugate

Polysaccharide antigen + protein carrier → recruits
T-cell help → induces IgG class-switching + memory
+ effective in infants (whose plain polysaccharide
responses are weak).

Examples:
- **Hib** (Hib-TT, Hib-CRM197).
- **Pneumococcal conjugates** — PCV13, PCV15, PCV20
  (in addition to PPSV23 plain polysaccharide for
  adults).
- **Meningococcal conjugates** — MenACWY (Menveo,
  Menactra, Nimenrix), MenB (Bexsero, Trumenba), MenABCWY
  (Penbraya).
- **Typhoid conjugate (TCV)** — Typbar TCV.

### Toxoid

Inactivated bacterial toxin.

Examples:
- **Tetanus toxoid**.
- **Diphtheria toxoid**.
- Often combined: DTaP / Tdap / DT / Td.

### Viral vector

A non-replicating (or single-cycle replicating) viral
vector encodes the target antigen.  Strong T-cell
response; induced anti-vector immunity may limit
re-dosing.

Examples:
- **AstraZeneca / Oxford ChAdOx1-S** (chimpanzee
  adenovirus, COVID-19 — Vaxzevria).
- **Janssen Ad26.COV2.S** (human adenovirus 26).
- **Sputnik V** (heterologous Ad26 + Ad5).
- **Ebola — rVSV-ZEBOV** (recombinant VSV).
- **Mpox** — Jynneos (modified vaccinia Ankara,
  technically replication-competent but attenuated).

### mRNA

Synthetic mRNA in lipid nanoparticles encodes the
antigen.  Cell takes up mRNA → translates → protein
displayed → adaptive immune response.

Pioneered by Karikó + Weissman (Nobel Prize 2023).
Game-changing pandemic-response technology.

Examples:
- **Pfizer-BioNTech BNT162b2 (Comirnaty)** — COVID-19
  Spike.
- **Moderna mRNA-1273 (Spikevax)** — COVID-19 Spike.
- **Moderna mRNA-1345 (mResvia)** — RSV (PFA antigen).
- **Pipeline**: flu (mRNA-1010), CMV (mRNA-1647),
  HIV, malaria, cancer (personalised mRNA-4157
  + pembrolizumab).

### DNA

Plasmid DNA encoding antigen, electroporation or
needle-free injection.  Regulatory approval limited;
veterinary use established.  India authorised
ZyCoV-D for COVID-19 (2021).

### Newer: nanoparticle, peptide, mRNA-LNP, saRNA, circRNA

Active research areas; stay tuned.

## Adjuvants

Many vaccines need adjuvants to achieve adequate
immunogenicity.  Approved adjuvants:
- **Aluminium salts (alum)** — workhorse since 1926;
  in DTaP, Hib, Hep B, HPV, etc.
- **AS04** — alum + MPL (TLR4 agonist) — HPV
  Cervarix, Hep B Fendrix.
- **AS01B** — liposomes + MPL + QS-21 — Shingrix,
  RTS,S malaria.
- **MF59** — squalene + Tween 80 + Span 85 — Fluad
  (elderly flu).
- **AS03** — squalene + DL-α-tocopherol — Pandemrix
  (2009 H1N1; narcolepsy signal).
- **CpG-1018** — TLR9 agonist — Heplisav-B (Hep B).
- **Matrix-M** — saponin nanoparticle — Novavax COVID.

## Schedules

Standard infant + childhood + adolescent + adult
schedules vary by country.  Major milestones in a
typical Western infant + child schedule:

| Age | Vaccines |
|-----|----------|
| Birth | Hep B (1st), BCG (high-burden countries) |
| 2 mo | DTaP / IPV / Hib / Hep B (hexavalent), PCV, rotavirus |
| 4 mo | Same as 2 mo |
| 6 mo | Hexavalent + PCV (rotavirus 3rd if RotaTeq) |
| 12-15 mo | MMR + varicella + Hib boost + Hep A + MenACWY in some areas |
| 4-6 yr | DTaP + IPV + MMR + varicella boosters |
| 11-12 yr | Tdap, HPV (2-dose < 15 y), MenACWY, MenB |
| 16 yr | MenACWY booster |
| 19-26 yr | HPV catch-up, MenB if not done |
| ≥ 50-65 yr | Shingles (Shingrix), pneumococcal (PCV20 or PCV15+PPSV23), RSV (≥ 60 yr) |
| Annual | Influenza (≥ 6 mo) |
| COVID-19 | Per current ACIP / JCVI guidance |

## Herd immunity thresholds

R0 = basic reproduction number (average secondary
cases per primary case in a fully susceptible
population).  Herd immunity threshold = 1 - 1/R0.

| Disease | R0 | Threshold |
|---------|----|-----------|
| Measles | 12-18 | 92-95 % |
| Pertussis | 12-17 | 92-94 % |
| Diphtheria | 6-7 | 83-86 % |
| Mumps | 4-7 | 75-86 % |
| Rubella | 6-7 | 83-86 % |
| Polio | 5-7 | 80-86 % |
| Smallpox | 5-7 | 80-86 % |
| Influenza (seasonal) | 1.2-1.5 | 17-33 % |
| SARS-CoV-2 (Wuhan) | 2.5-3 | 60-67 % |
| SARS-CoV-2 (Omicron) | 8-10+ | 88-90 % |

For high-R0 pathogens like measles, even brief
coverage gaps (< 92 %) trigger outbreaks — the
2018-2019 measles resurgence in the US + Europe is
the canonical example.

## Cold chain + delivery

Vaccines vary in temperature requirements:
- **Standard cold chain** (2-8 °C): most vaccines.
- **Frozen** (-20 °C): MMR, varicella, Zostavax,
  smallpox, mpox.
- **Ultra-low** (-70 °C): Pfizer COVID-19 (initially),
  Spikevax (initially), some viral-vector vaccines.

mRNA vaccines have driven cold-chain innovation —
Pfizer's later formulations stable at -20 °C +
2-8 °C for limited periods.

LMIC delivery infrastructure (Gavi, UNICEF, WHO
PQ) has dramatically increased global access.

## Safety surveillance

- **VAERS** (US), **EudraVigilance** (EU), **Yellow
  Card** (UK), **Brighton Collaboration** (global) —
  passive reporting.
- **Vaccine Safety Datalink (VSD)** — ~ 12 M
  enrollees in active surveillance.
- **CISA** — Clinical Immunization Safety Assessment.
- **Phase 4 commitments** for new vaccines.

Adverse event signals + their resolutions are public
trust battlegrounds: rotavirus (Rotashield → intussusception
→ withdrawal 1999), narcolepsy + Pandemrix (H1N1
2009), myocarditis + COVID mRNA (~ 1-7 / 100 000 in
young males, mild + transient majority), thrombosis +
ChAdOx1 (~ 1 / 100 000 — VITT — pulled from many
markets).

## Vaccine-preventable disease eradication / control

- **Smallpox** — eradicated 1980.  Only vaccine to
  eradicate a human pathogen.
- **Polio** — wild type 2 eradicated 1999, type 3
  in 2019; type 1 still circulating in Afghanistan +
  Pakistan + occasional cVDPV outbreaks.
- **Measles** — elimination achieved + lost in many
  regions; high-coverage gaps drive resurgence.
- **Rubella** — eliminated in WHO Americas region
  since 2015.
- **Maternal-neonatal tetanus** — eliminated in
  most countries.
- **HPV cervical cancer** — Australia projected to
  eliminate by 2035 (high vaccination + screening
  coverage).
- **Malaria** — RTS,S (Mosquirix) + R21 (Matrix-M)
  WHO recommended for African children since 2021 +
  2023; partial efficacy + multi-dose schedule.

## SARS-CoV-2 vaccine landscape — what changed

The pandemic compressed vaccine timelines from
~ 10-15 years to ~ 11 months (BNT162b2 emergency
authorisation Dec 2020).  Made possible by:
- **Pre-existing platforms** (mRNA, viral vector,
  protein subunit).
- **Sequence available within days** of pathogen
  characterisation.
- **Parallel rather than sequential** trial phases.
- **Manufacturing at risk** (massive scale-up before
  approval).
- **Global trial collaboration** (industry +
  government + academia + CEPI + Operation Warp Speed).

Lessons applied to mpox, RSV, future pandemics.
The 100-day-mission target (CEPI) aims for a
100-day vaccine for any new pathogen.
