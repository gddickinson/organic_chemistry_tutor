# Modern animal biotech + One Health

Animal biotechnology has expanded from agricultural
breeding + veterinary medicine into transformative
domains: gene drives for vector control,
xenotransplantation, CRISPR livestock, conservation
genetics, and the integrated One Health framework
linking human + animal + ecosystem health.

## Gene drives + Oxitec mosquitoes

A genetic system that biases inheritance such that
a desired allele spreads through a population
faster than Mendelian inheritance allows.

### CRISPR-based gene drives

Mechanism:
- Cas9 + sgRNA encoded next to the desired
  modification.
- Heterozygous offspring inherit one wild-type +
  one drive allele.
- Cas9 + sgRNA cut the wild-type allele.
- Homology-directed repair using the drive allele
  as template → both chromosomes carry the drive
  → > 95 % inheritance vs Mendelian 50 %.

Applications proposed:
- **Suppress disease vectors** — *Anopheles*
  malaria mosquitoes, *Aedes aegypti* dengue +
  Zika + chikungunya vectors.
- **Modify vectors to refuse pathogen carriage**.
- **Suppress invasive species** — rodents on
  islands, brown tree snakes on Guam.
- **Engineer agricultural pests**.

### Oxitec mosquitoes

Oxitec (Oxford spin-out) released:
- **OX513A** — *Aedes aegypti* with engineered
  tetracycline-repressible lethality; offspring
  die without dietary tetracycline → population
  suppression.  Released in Cayman, Brazil,
  Florida, others.
- **Friendly™ mosquito (OX5034 + Oxitec 2nd gen)** —
  female-killing variant; only male Aedes survive
  release; daughters die before adulthood.

Pros: targeted, no chemicals, reversible (release-
dependent), specific to one species.

Cons: requires continuous release (vs gene-drive
self-propagating); social-licence + regulatory
complexity.

### Target Malaria + other gene-drive consortia

Field trials of CRISPR-based suppression drives in
*Anopheles gambiae* + *A. coluzzii* + *A. arabiensis*
ongoing in confined settings.  Open-air releases
not yet authorised.

Concerns:
- Ecological consequences of vector elimination.
- Cross-border spread.
- Resistance evolution (drive-resistant alleles
  arise rapidly).
- Governance + community consent — Cartagena
  Protocol, dual-use research, biosecurity.

## Xenotransplantation

Transplanting organs across species — addressing
the chronic shortage of human donors.

### Pig as donor of choice

- Anatomic + physiological similarity.
- Reproductive efficiency + breeding scalability.
- Less ethically contested than NHPs.

### Major hurdles + solutions

- **Hyperacute rejection** by anti-α-Gal antibodies
  → CRISPR knockout of GGTA1 (α-Gal).
- **Acute vascular rejection** + complement
  activation → knockout β4GalNT2 + CMAH; transgenic
  expression of human complement regulators (CD46,
  CD55, CD59).
- **Cellular rejection** → tissue-specific
  immunomodulation + immunosuppression regimens
  + costimulation blockade.
- **PERV (porcine endogenous retroviruses)** →
  CRISPR genome-wide knockout (eGenesis, Niu et al.
  2017).
- **Coagulation incompatibility** → human
  thrombomodulin + EPCR transgenes.

### Clinical milestones

- **Revivicor (United Therapeutics)** — pioneer of
  10-gene-edited pig donors.
- **eGenesis** — > 60-edited PERV-knockout pigs;
  Boston-based.
- **2022** — first whole-pig-heart transplant in a
  living human (David Bennett, U. Maryland);
  patient died ~ 2 months post-transplant from
  multifactorial causes including PCMV.
- **2024** — first pig-kidney transplant in living
  human (Massachusetts General Hospital, Mar 2024);
  multiple subsequent recipients with mixed
  outcomes.
- **Decedent recipients** — pig-organ transplants
  into brain-dead human bodies for proof-of-concept
  + immunology research.

Trajectory: routine clinical use perhaps within
5-10 years if remaining hurdles addressed.

## CRISPR-edited livestock

Beyond xenotransplantation:
- **Disease resistance** — PRRSV-resistant pigs
  via CD163 knockout (Genus); avian-influenza-
  resistant chickens (Roslin Institute trials);
  TB-resistant cattle.
- **Production traits** — myostatin-knockout cattle
  + sheep + pigs (double-muscling).
- **Welfare improvements** — polled (hornless)
  cattle via CRISPR-introduced POLLED allele
  (Recombinetics; FDA initially regulated as drug
  → setback; UK + Argentina permits).
- **Heat tolerance** — cattle with SLICK gene for
  short-hair phenotype.
- **Allergen-free milk + eggs** — research stage.
- **Surrogate sires** — sterile males implanted with
  donor germline stem cells produce donor sperm
  (Roslin + Iowa State).

Regulatory landscapes:
- US — FDA initial position (gene-edited animal =
  drug) softened post-2020 to APHIS process for
  some traits.
- Argentina + Brazil — process-based, edits without
  foreign DNA largely deregulated.
- EU — historically restrictive; under-revision.
- UK — Genetic Technology (Precision Breeding) Act
  2023 enables commercial development of
  precision-bred animals + plants.
- Japan + Australia + China — variable but
  generally permissive.

## Animal CRISPR for medicine

Beyond livestock:
- **Disease models** — humanised mouse strains;
  pig models of cystic fibrosis + cardiovascular
  + Alzheimer's; non-human-primate models limited
  + ethically constrained.
- **Cell + tissue source for human therapy** —
  pig islet + pig-corneal-tissue xenotransplantation
  protocols.
- **Bioactive compound producers** — chicken-
  laid pharmaceutical proteins (egg whites);
  goat-milked spider silk (Nexia historical).

## Animal models in drug development

Despite alternatives, animal models remain
critical:
- **Mouse** — most-used; ~ 95 % of preclinical
  studies.
- **Rat** — physiology + pharmacology + safety.
- **Dog + non-human primate** — cardiovascular +
  CNS pharmacology + toxicology.
- **Zebrafish + fly + worm** — early-screening
  + genetic modifier studies.
- **Pig** — cardiovascular + xenotransplantation +
  some disease models.

The 3Rs (Replacement, Reduction, Refinement) frame
ethical animal use:
- Replacement — non-animal alternatives where
  possible (organ-on-chip, organoids, in silico).
- Reduction — fewer animals via better statistics
  + design.
- Refinement — minimising suffering.

FDA Modernization Act 2.0 (2022) eliminated the
strict animal-testing requirement for new drug
approval — accepting alternative methods (in vitro,
organoid, organ-on-chip) where validated.  Likely
trajectory for next decades.

## Conservation biotech

CRISPR + genomics meet conservation:
- **Genetic rescue** — gene-flow augmentation
  (Florida panther rescued by Texas cougar
  introductions).
- **Disease-resistance engineering** — chytrid-
  resistant frog studies; white-nose-resistant bat
  exploration.
- **Coral-resilience breeding** — heat-tolerant
  genotype selection + assisted gene flow.
- **De-extinction** — Colossal Biosciences mammoth
  + thylacine + dodo programmes.  Mammoth target
  = elephant editing for cold-tolerance phenotype
  (~ 60 cold-related genes).
- **Cryo banks** — Frozen Zoo (San Diego); embryo +
  sperm + somatic-cell preservation for endangered
  species.
- **iPSC + stem-cell technologies** — produce
  gametes from skin biopsies for breeding programmes.

## One Health

Recognition that human, animal, and environmental
health are interconnected → integrated frameworks +
surveillance + policy.

### Key threads

- **Zoonotic disease emergence** — ~ 75 % of
  emerging pathogens are zoonotic; SARS, MERS,
  SARS-CoV-2, Nipah, Ebola, mpox, avian flu, HIV.
- **AMR transmission** across human + animal +
  environment.
- **Foodborne disease** — *Salmonella*, *Campylobacter*,
  *E. coli* O157, *Listeria* — zoonotic origins.
- **Vector-borne disease** — climate-driven range
  shifts of mosquitoes, ticks, sandflies; novel
  exposures.
- **Wildlife trade + pandemic risk**.
- **Animal welfare + production-system disease
  spread**.
- **Climate-change health impacts** — heat, food
  insecurity, vector range, harmful algal blooms.

### Quadripartite

WHO + FAO (food + agriculture) + WOAH (animal
health) + UNEP (environment) — Joint Plan of
Action 2022-2026 + 2030 strategic framework.

### Pandemic preparedness

- **PREDICT + DEEP VZN** — wildlife virome
  surveillance.
- **CEPI 100-day mission** — vaccine in 100 days
  for any new pandemic pathogen.
- **WHO Bacterial / Viral Priority Pathogens
  Lists**.
- **Pandemic Treaty** + **IHR amendments**
  (under negotiation 2024-2025).

### Planetary health

A broader framing — human health depends on
flourishing natural systems.  Lancet Commission
on Planetary Health (2015 + 2022 update) +
Rockefeller Foundation initiatives.

## Animal-biotech ethics + governance

Active ethical questions:
- **Welfare implications** of edited livestock.
- **Ecological consequences** of gene drives.
- **Equity** — biotech benefits for high-income vs
  LMIC populations.
- **Wildlife biodiversity** vs technological
  interventions.
- **Cultural + Indigenous values** in conservation
  decisions.
- **Cloning + de-extinction** — value, hubris,
  unintended consequences.
- **Xenotransplantation** consent + tracking +
  long-term monitoring.

International + national governance:
- Cartagena Protocol on Biosafety.
- Convention on Biological Diversity + Nagoya
  Protocol.
- National GE / GMO + biosecurity regulations.
- IUCN policy on synthetic biology + conservation.
- Multi-stakeholder consultations + community
  consent (Target Malaria's engagement model).
