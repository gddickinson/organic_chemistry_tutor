# Comparative genomics + animal evolution

Sequencing technology has become cheap enough +
fast enough that we can now compare entire genomes
across the animal tree.  The result has rewritten
much of evolutionary biology + opened windows on
extinct lineages, recent human evolution, and the
genomic basis of phenotypic diversity.

## The animal genome diversity range

Genome sizes span 6 orders of magnitude across
animals:
- **Smallest** — *Pratylenchus coffeae* nematode
  ~ 20 Mb.
- **Smallest vertebrate** — pufferfish (*Takifugu*)
  ~ 400 Mb.
- **Human** — ~ 3.2 Gb.
- **Largest mammal** — red viscacha rat ~ 8.4 Gb.
- **Largest sequenced** — lungfish ~ 130 Gb;
  *Polychaos dubium* amoeba estimated > 670 Gb
  (controversial).

Genome size correlates poorly with morphological
"complexity" (the C-value paradox).  Most
variation comes from transposable elements +
intronic + intergenic + repetitive sequences.

## Gene number across animals

Surprisingly conserved (~ 14 000-30 000 protein-
coding genes) across most animals:
- Sponge — ~ 18 000.
- Sea anemone — ~ 18 000.
- *C. elegans* — ~ 20 000.
- *Drosophila* — ~ 14 000.
- Sea urchin — ~ 23 000.
- Zebrafish — ~ 26 000.
- Mouse + human — ~ 20 000.

Most novelty is in REGULATORY DNA + cis-elements +
isoform diversity + non-coding-RNA worlds, not in
new protein-coding genes.

## Whole-genome duplications (WGDs)

Major WGD events shaped vertebrate evolution:
- **1R + 2R** at the base of vertebrates +
  jawed-vertebrates (~ 500-450 Ma).
- **3R** teleost-specific (~ 320 Ma) — explains many
  fish-specific gene duplicates.
- **4R** in salmonids (~ 80 Ma) + carp + sturgeon
  + axolotl (independent).
- Polyploidy is rare in vertebrates beyond fish +
  amphibians but common in invertebrates +
  especially plants.

WGD provides genetic raw material for innovation —
neofunctionalisation, subfunctionalisation, dosage
balance, gene loss.

## Transposable elements (TEs)

A major + often dominant fraction of animal genomes:
- ~ 50 % of human genome derived from TEs.
- ~ 80 % of maize genome.
- > 70 % of some amphibian genomes.

Categories:
- **Class I (retrotransposons)** — copy via RNA
  intermediate.  LINE-1 (still active in humans),
  SINEs (Alu in primates), LTR-retrotransposons.
- **Class II (DNA transposons)** — cut + paste.
  Mostly inactive in mammals; very active in plants
  + insects + fish.
- **Endogenous retroviruses (ERVs)** — past viral
  infections fixed in the germline; ~ 8 % of human
  genome.

TEs:
- Drive structural-variation + chromosomal
  rearrangement.
- Provide cis-regulatory elements (TE-derived
  enhancers + promoters).
- Source of new exons (exonisation).
- Contribute to gene-duplication.
- Are silenced by KRAB-ZFP + KAP1 + DNA methylation
  + piRNA pathways.

Iconic examples:
- **Syncytin** — placental fusion gene from ERV;
  evolved independently in multiple mammalian
  lineages.
- **RAG1/2** — V(D)J recombinase originated from
  Transib transposon.
- **Telomerase reverse transcriptase** — ancient
  TE-derived.

## Regulatory innovations

Cis-regulatory variation explains far more
phenotypic diversity than coding changes:
- **Hox cis-regulation** — limb + body-plan
  evolution mediated by enhancer evolution.
- **HACNS1** — limb-specific human-accelerated
  enhancer.
- **AS3MT-related arsenic-tolerance** in Andean
  populations.
- **EDAR variant** — hair + sweat-gland morphology
  in East Asians.
- **Lactase persistence** — multiple independent
  origins linked to dairy-farming cultures via
  cis-regulatory mutations.
- **Pelvic loss in stickleback** — Pitx1 enhancer
  deletion in freshwater populations.
- **Beak shape in Galapagos finches** — BMP4 +
  CaM expression differences.

The **deeply-conserved noncoding element (CNE)**
landscape across vertebrates encodes much of
developmental regulation — most CNEs are
millions-of-years-old enhancers.

## Pan-genomes

The realisation that one reference genome is
inadequate:
- Different individuals + populations carry
  variable + dispensable genes.
- Human pan-genome (HPRC + T2T consortia) reveals
  ~ 100 Mb of additional sequence beyond the GRCh38
  reference.
- Plant + crop pan-genomes (rice, soybean, maize,
  wheat) show even more dramatic gene-content
  variation.
- Pan-genome assembly is now standard for major
  species.

## Ancient DNA (aDNA) revolution

aDNA recovery has transformed evolutionary biology
+ archaeology:
- **Methods** — bone-powder DNA extraction; library
  prep optimised for damaged + short fragments;
  enrichment with bait-capture; bioinformatic
  authentication of damage patterns + contamination.
- **Pääbo Nobel 2022** — Neanderthal + Denisovan
  genomes; introgression with anatomically modern
  humans (Neanderthal alleles ~ 2 % in non-African
  populations; Denisovan alleles enriched in
  Melanesians + Tibetans, including the EPAS1
  high-altitude allele).
- **Population history** — the Bronze-Age Yamnaya
  steppe expansion; rapid population turnovers in
  Europe + India; African genetic diversity +
  ancient population structure.
- **Dog + horse + cattle + pig domestication**
  histories — multiple origins, complex admixture.
- **Plant aDNA** — Pleistocene mammoth-steppe
  vegetation; agricultural origins.
- **Pathogen aDNA** — Black Death *Yersinia
  pestis*, ancient TB, Salmonella in pre-Columbian
  Americas, ancient HBV / variola.

aDNA has opened new windows on Pleistocene fauna +
megafauna extinction debates + early human
migrations.

## Recent human evolution + adaptation

- **Lactase persistence** — multiple independent
  origins in dairying populations (~ 7 K years ago
  in Europe; later + independent origins in East
  Africa + Arabia).
- **High-altitude adaptation** — EPAS1 / EGLN1 in
  Tibetans (Denisovan-introgressed); BHLHE41 in
  Andean Quechua.
- **Skin + eye + hair pigmentation** — multiple loci
  (SLC24A5, SLC45A2, MC1R, OCA2, HERC2) under
  selection in different populations.
- **Diet-related** — AMY1 copy number + starch
  consumption; FADS-cluster + n-3 fatty-acid
  metabolism.
- **Disease-resistance** — HbS, G6PD, DARC + malaria;
  CCR5-Δ32 + plague + smallpox + HIV.

Selective sweeps + balancing selection + introgression
all leave detectable genomic signatures.

## Comparative genomic puzzles + insights

### Transposable-element life-cycle

LINE-1 still actively transposes in human germline
+ some somatic tissues (especially brain) — possible
contribution to neuronal mosaicism.

### Single-cell genome variation

Within an individual, neurons + tumour cells +
some other cell types show extensive mosaic
genomic + epigenomic variation.

### Hybridisation + introgression

More common than once thought, even among modern
species (Sumatran orangutan + cattle hybrids;
gene flow between modern + archaic humans;
*Rhagoletis* hybrid speciation).

### Gene loss

Animals lose genes constantly:
- Cetaceans lost olfactory receptors + many
  taste-receptor genes adapting to aquatic life.
- Snakes lost limb-development genes.
- Cave fish lost vision + pigmentation pathway
  genes.
- Pythons + boas lost amino-acid biosynthesis genes
  + acquired others.
- Humans lost ~ 35 olfactory-receptor + many
  bitter-taste-receptor genes vs other primates.

Gene loss can be adaptive — "less is more" hypothesis.

### Convergent molecular evolution

- **Echolocation** in bats + whales — Prestin
  + KCNQ4 + Tmc1 amino-acid convergence.
- **Cold tolerance** in Antarctic icefish + Arctic
  cod — antifreeze glycoprotein convergence.
- **Hypoxia tolerance** in high-altitude humans +
  bar-headed goose — convergent hemoglobin
  mutations.
- **Mimic octopus** colour-change + body-pattern
  signalling.

## Genomic insights into specific lineages

### Cnidarians + the nervous-system origin

Genomes of *Nematostella* + *Hydra* + *Aurelia*
reveal ancient signalling pathways + developmental
genetics, suggesting the Eumetazoan ancestor had a
remarkably complete genetic toolkit.

### Cephalopod genomes

Heavily edited transcriptome (~ 60 % of mRNAs
recoded by ADAR enzymes — much higher than other
animals).  Massively expanded protocadherin family
underpinning unique cephalopod nervous-system
diversity.

### Insect genomes + species explosions

> 1.2 million described insect species → > 5 000
sequenced genomes (i5K + projects).  Lineage-specific
duplications of olfactory + chemosensory + immune
gene families drive ecological diversification.

### Vertebrate-specific innovations

- Adaptive immune system (RAG / MHC / Ig genes).
- Neural crest + Hox cluster duplication.
- Forebrain expansion in mammals + birds.
- Regional brain specialisation.

## Open challenges + frontiers

- **Cell-type evolution** — when did each cell type
  arise?  Single-cell comparative genomics
  emerging.
- **Gene-regulatory-network evolution** —
  understanding regulatory rewiring at network
  scale.
- **Genotype-to-phenotype mapping** — for complex
  traits + behaviours.
- **Pan-genomes for every species** — capturing
  variability + structural variants.
- **Long-read assemblies + complete genomes**
  (T2T-style) for major lineages.
- **Single-cell omics across phyla** — comparative
  cell-type evolution.
- **Functional annotation** — most non-coding DNA
  function still uncertain.
