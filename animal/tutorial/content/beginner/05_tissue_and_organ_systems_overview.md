# Tissue + organ systems overview

Vertebrate bodies are organised into four
fundamental tissue types that together make up
~ 11 organ systems.  This lesson lays out the
hierarchy at a glance; later AB-3.0 lessons go
deeper on physiology + comparative anatomy.

## The four tissue types

### Epithelial

Lines surfaces + cavities + glands.  Cells are
tightly joined + sit on a basement membrane.  Three
shape categories × three layering categories →
classification:

- **Squamous** (flat) — alveoli, capillaries,
  glomerular endothelium, mesothelium.
- **Cuboidal** — kidney tubules, glands.
- **Columnar** (tall) — most of GI tract, female
  reproductive tract.

- **Simple** — single cell layer.
- **Stratified** — multiple layers (skin, oral
  cavity, oesophagus, vagina).
- **Pseudostratified** — appears multilayered but
  every cell touches basement membrane (respiratory
  tract).
- **Transitional** — stretchable; bladder.

Functions:
- Protection (skin, mucosae).
- Absorption (intestinal villi).
- Secretion (glands; exocrine + endocrine).
- Filtration (kidney).
- Sensory reception (taste buds, olfactory
  epithelium).

### Connective

Most diverse tissue type.  Cells dispersed in
extracellular matrix (ECM) of fibres + ground
substance.

Sub-classes:
- **Loose connective** — areolar (under skin +
  around organs), adipose (energy + insulation +
  endocrine), reticular (lymphoid stroma).
- **Dense connective** — regular (tendons, ligaments
  — collagen aligned), irregular (dermis — collagen
  in many directions).
- **Cartilage** — chondrocytes + chondroitin /
  hyaluronic / ECM.  Hyaline (joints), elastic
  (ear), fibrocartilage (intervertebral discs,
  pubic symphysis).
- **Bone** — osteoblast / osteocyte / osteoclast +
  hydroxyapatite + collagen ECM.  Compact + spongy
  organisation.
- **Blood** — RBCs + WBCs + platelets in plasma.
- **Lymph** — interstitial-fluid-derived; circulates
  through lymphatic vessels.

### Muscle

Three types:
- **Skeletal** — voluntary, striated, multinucleate
  myofibres, attached to bone via tendons.
- **Cardiac** — involuntary, striated, single-
  nucleate, intercalated discs (gap junctions +
  desmosomes), myocardium.
- **Smooth** — involuntary, non-striated, spindle-
  shaped, single-nucleate, in walls of GI / blood
  vessels / urinary / reproductive / respiratory.

All three use actin + myosin for contraction;
calcium signalling triggers cycling.

### Nervous

Two cell types:
- **Neurons** — excitable cells; soma + dendrites +
  axon; transmit electrical (action potentials) +
  chemical (synaptic) signals.
- **Glia** — supportive; many subtypes:
  - **Astrocytes** — CNS metabolic + BBB + synaptic
    support.
  - **Oligodendrocytes** (CNS) + **Schwann cells**
    (PNS) — myelin sheath formation.
  - **Microglia** — CNS immune cells.
  - **Ependymal cells** — line CNS ventricles + spinal
    canal; CSF dynamics.
  - **Satellite cells** — peripheral neuron support.

## The 11 mammalian organ systems

A canonical list (covered in detail in **AB-2.0
Organ systems** catalogue + the AB-3.0 intermediate
"Comparative animal physiology" lesson):

### 1. Cardiovascular

Heart + blood vessels + blood.  Closed circulation
in vertebrates.  Functions: O2 + CO2 + nutrient
+ waste + heat + hormone + immune-cell distribution.

### 2. Respiratory

Lungs + airways + diaphragm.  Gas exchange (O2
intake, CO2 release).  Pulmonary surfactant
reduces alveolar surface tension.

### 3. Digestive

Mouth → oesophagus → stomach → small intestine →
large intestine → rectum + accessory organs (liver,
pancreas, gallbladder, salivary glands).

Functions: ingestion, mechanical + chemical
digestion, absorption, defecation.

### 4. Urinary

Kidneys + ureters + bladder + urethra.  Functions:
filtration, reabsorption, secretion, water balance,
acid-base balance, blood-pressure regulation
(renin), erythropoietin production.

### 5. Nervous

CNS (brain + spinal cord) + PNS (cranial + spinal
nerves) + autonomic + enteric divisions.  Functions:
sensing, integrating, motor control, behaviour,
cognition.

### 6. Endocrine

Pituitary, thyroid, parathyroid, adrenals, pancreas
(endocrine portion), gonads, pineal, thymus.
Functions: hormonal regulation of metabolism,
growth, reproduction, stress, water balance.

### 7. Immune (lymphatic / immune)

Lymph nodes, spleen, thymus, bone marrow, MALT,
GALT.  Innate + adaptive immunity (covered in
**AB-3.0 intermediate "Animal immunology
essentials"**).

### 8. Musculoskeletal

Bones + joints + tendons + skeletal muscles.
Movement, protection, hematopoiesis,
calcium-phosphate homeostasis.

### 9. Integumentary

Skin + appendages (hair, nails, sweat + sebaceous
glands).  Barrier, thermoregulation, sensation,
vitamin-D synthesis.

### 10. Reproductive (female + male)

Female: ovaries, oviducts, uterus, vagina, vulva,
mammary glands.
Male: testes, epididymis, vas deferens, seminal
vesicles, prostate, penis.

### 11. Lymphatic

Sometimes counted as part of the immune system or
separately.  Lymphatic vessels + lymph nodes +
lacteals + thoracic duct.  Returns interstitial
fluid to blood; absorbs dietary lipids
(chylomicrons).

## Levels of organisation

The organisational hierarchy:
1. Atoms.
2. Molecules (proteins, lipids, sugars, nucleic
   acids).
3. Macromolecular complexes (ribosomes, complex I).
4. Organelles (mitochondria, ER, nucleus).
5. Cells (~ 200 distinct cell types in a human
   body).
6. Tissues (4 types as above).
7. Organs (heart, kidney, lung).
8. Organ systems (11 above).
9. Organism.

Each level has emergent properties not reducible to
sub-component parts — the central thesis of
systems biology.

## Cross-system integration

Real physiology is integrated across systems:
- **Hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis** — endocrine
  + nervous + cardiovascular + immune.
- **Gut-brain axis** — enteric nervous system +
  microbiome + vagal + endocrine + immune signalling.
- **Cardiopulmonary** — heart + lung as functional
  unit; failure in one cascades.
- **Hepatorenal** — liver + kidney share many
  failure-mode interactions; Kim-1 + NGAL + serum
  creatinine + bilirubin tracking.
- **Neuro-immune** — CNS-driven inflammation,
  microglia, vagal anti-inflammatory pathway.
- **Bone-marrow-immune-haematopoietic** — blood cell
  production + immune-cell + thrombopoiesis
  coordinated.

Modern medicine + research increasingly emphasises
**multi-system + systems-level** approaches over
single-organ specialism.

## Comparative organ systems

The AB-2.0 catalogue lists 13 comparative-anatomy
entries covering invertebrate + vertebrate variants:
- **Open circulation** (insects, crustaceans, most
  molluscs) vs **closed** (vertebrates +
  cephalopods + annelids).
- **Single-circuit** (fish) vs **double-circuit**
  (amphibian / reptile / bird / mammal) heart.
- **Gills** (fish) vs **tracheae** (insects) vs
  **lungs** (vertebrates) vs **air sacs** (birds).
- **Ruminant** (cattle, sheep) vs **avian** vs
  **hindgut** (horse, rabbit) digestion.
- **Nerve nets** (cnidarians) vs **centralised
  brains** vs **cephalopod brains** (largest among
  invertebrates).
- **Protonephridia** (flatworms) vs **metanephridia**
  (annelids, molluscs) vs **Malpighian tubules**
  (insects) vs **kidneys** (vertebrates).
- **Eye evolution** — eyespots → cup eyes → camera
  eyes (independently in many phyla).
- **Insect ecdysone signalling** + moulting.
- **Hermaphroditism** + **parthenogenesis** in
  diverse invertebrates.
- **Ectothermy** vs **endothermy** + how each
  shapes ecology + behaviour.
- **Regeneration outliers** — planarian + axolotl
  + hydra full-body regeneration vs limited
  vertebrate regeneration.
