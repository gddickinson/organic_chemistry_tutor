# What animal biology is

Animal biology — zoology — is the study of the
metazoan kingdom: the multicellular, heterotrophic,
mostly motile, mostly sexually-reproducing organisms
that have spent the last ~ 600 million years
evolving from a tiny choanoflagellate-like ancestor
into ~ 1.5 million described species + perhaps 7-10
million estimated total.

## Defining "animal"

Animals are characterised by:
- **Multicellularity** with cell-cell adhesion +
  intercellular signalling.
- **Heterotrophic** (consume organic matter).
- **Lack a cell wall** (in contrast to plants, fungi,
  protists).
- **Diploid life cycle** dominated by the
  multicellular phase.
- **Embryonic development** through stereotyped
  stages (cleavage → blastula → gastrulation →
  organogenesis).
- **Collagen** as the major extracellular matrix
  protein — unique to animals.
- **Specialised cell types** for movement, sensing,
  digestion, gas exchange (most species).
- **A nervous system** (in all but sponges +
  placozoa).

## Animal phylogeny in nine phyla

The Animal Biology Studio's AB-1.0 catalogue covers
all nine major phyla:

- **Porifera** (sponges) — ~ 9 000 species; sister
  group to all other animals; no true tissues; choano-
  cytes pump water through pores.
- **Cnidaria** (jellyfish, anemones, corals,
  hydroids) — ~ 11 000 species; radial symmetry; two
  germ layers; nematocysts (stinging cells).
- **Platyhelminthes** (flatworms) — ~ 30 000
  species; bilateral symmetry; acoelomate; many
  parasitic (tapeworms, flukes, *Schistosoma*).
- **Nematoda** (roundworms) — ~ 25 000 described
  but possibly > 1 million species; pseudocoelomate;
  *C. elegans* is the canonical model organism.
- **Mollusca** (snails, clams, octopus) —
  ~ 85 000 species; second-largest phylum; calcium-
  carbonate shells in many.
- **Annelida** (segmented worms) — ~ 22 000
  species; earthworms, leeches, polychaetes; true
  segmentation.
- **Arthropoda** — > 1 200 000 species; the largest
  phylum by far; insects + crustaceans + spiders +
  scorpions + horseshoe crabs + myriapods + many
  extinct groups.
- **Echinodermata** (starfish, urchins, sea
  cucumbers) — ~ 7 000 species; secondary radial
  symmetry; deuterostomes; water-vascular system.
- **Chordata** — ~ 65 000 species; sea squirts +
  lancelets + vertebrates including all mammals.

Plus dozens of smaller phyla — gastrotrichs,
rotifers, nemerteans, brachiopods, bryozoans,
priapulids, kinorhynchs, loriciferans,
tardigrades, onychophorans, and others.

## Why animals matter

### Ecology

Animals occupy almost every trophic level:
- **Primary consumers** (herbivores) move plant
  biomass into food webs.
- **Secondary + tertiary consumers** (carnivores +
  omnivores) regulate populations + drive trophic
  cascades.
- **Detritivores** + **decomposers** (along with
  bacteria + fungi) recycle nutrients.
- **Pollinators** — bees, butterflies, beetles,
  bats, hummingbirds, sunbirds — sustain ~ 87 %
  of flowering plant species.
- **Seed dispersers** — frugivores + ant-mediated
  + caching mammals + birds.
- **Ecosystem engineers** — beavers, elephants,
  termites, corals, woodpeckers.

### Human relevance

- **Food** — livestock, poultry, fish + shellfish
  + insects + game.
- **Companion** — dogs, cats, horses + pets.
- **Working animals** — historically draft + transport;
  guide / service / detection animals today.
- **Disease vectors** — mosquitoes, ticks, fleas,
  triatomines, black flies — > 1 billion human
  cases / year.
- **Biomedical models** — mouse + rat + zebrafish +
  fly + worm + xenopus + monkey + dog underpin
  almost all biomedical research.
- **Cultural + symbolic** — animals pervade religion,
  mythology, art, literature, sport, identity.

### Biotechnology + biomimetics

- **Insect-derived enzymes + peptides** — silk,
  spider silk, snake-venom-derived ACE inhibitors
  (captopril design from *Bothrops jararaca*).
- **Marine-organism natural products** —
  bryostatin, ziconotide (cone-snail toxin →
  analgesic), trabectedin (sea-squirt → cancer
  drug).
- **Animal-derived therapeutics** — heparin (pig
  intestine), insulin (historically pig + cow),
  hyaluronic acid, surfactants, blood clotting
  factors.
- **Animal models for drug development** — every
  clinical drug passed through animal testing.
- **Biomimetic materials + designs** — Velcro
  (burdock), gecko-adhesive, shark-skin antifouling,
  termite-mound architecture, bird-flight aerodynamics,
  dragonfly aviation.

## Major model organisms

| Model | Phylum | Why studied |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| *Caenorhabditis elegans* | Nematoda | First whole-animal genome (1998), defined cell lineage, apoptosis (Brenner / Sulston / Horvitz Nobel 2002) |
| *Drosophila melanogaster* | Arthropoda | Genetics (Morgan, Mendel-fly), developmental biology (Nüsslein-Volhard / Wieschaus / Lewis Nobel 1995), learning, ageing |
| Zebrafish (*Danio rerio*) | Chordata | Vertebrate development, transparent embryo, large clutches, regeneration |
| *Xenopus laevis* + *tropicalis* | Chordata | Embryology, oocyte microinjection, signal-pathway biochemistry |
| Mouse (*Mus musculus*) | Chordata | Mammalian model par excellence; genetic engineering (knockouts, transgenics), disease models |
| Rat (*Rattus norvegicus*) | Chordata | Physiology, pharmacology, behaviour |
| Yeast (*S. cerevisiae*) — fungal not animal | — | Cell-cycle (Hartwell / Hunt / Nurse Nobel 2001), genetics |
| Sea urchin (*Strongylocentrotus*) | Echinodermata | Embryology, fertilisation |
| Hydra | Cnidaria | Regeneration, stem cells |
| *Aplysia* | Mollusca | Neuroscience, learning + memory (Kandel Nobel 2000) |
| *Octopus vulgaris* | Mollusca | Cognition, intelligence, soft-body robotics inspiration |
| Cnidarian *Nematostella vectensis* | Cnidaria | Evo-devo, regeneration, nervous-system origin |

## Brief evolutionary history

- **~ 700-800 Ma** — divergence of metazoans from
  choanoflagellate-like ancestors.
- **~ 600-580 Ma** — Ediacaran fauna (soft-bodied
  + enigmatic).
- **~ 540 Ma** — **Cambrian explosion** — most
  animal phyla appear in the fossil record within
  20-25 Myr.
- **~ 540-485 Ma** — early Paleozoic; trilobites,
  early arthropods, brachiopods, primitive
  vertebrates (jawless fish).
- **~ 420 Ma** — first jawed fish.
- **~ 380-360 Ma** — first tetrapods (Devonian);
  *Tiktaalik*.
- **~ 300 Ma** — Carboniferous; first amniotes;
  giant insects (Meganeura).
- **~ 250 Ma** — Permian-Triassic mass extinction;
  end-Permian "Great Dying" wiped out > 90 % of
  marine species.
- **~ 230-65 Ma** — Mesozoic; dinosaurs + first
  mammals + first birds + flowering-plant
  co-evolution.
- **~ 65 Ma** — K-Pg mass extinction; non-avian
  dinosaurs extinct; mammalian radiation.
- **~ 6 Ma** — hominin / chimp split.
- **~ 300 K** — anatomically modern *H. sapiens*.

## Modern animal-biology subfields

- **Comparative anatomy** + **morphology**.
- **Physiology** — cellular to organismal.
- **Developmental biology + evo-devo**.
- **Genetics + genomics + comparative genomics**.
- **Neuroscience** — molecular to systems to behavior.
- **Endocrinology + immunology**.
- **Ecology + behaviour + ethology**.
- **Conservation biology** + IUCN-driven priorities.
- **Animal welfare science**.
- **One Health** — human + animal + environmental
  health interconnections.
