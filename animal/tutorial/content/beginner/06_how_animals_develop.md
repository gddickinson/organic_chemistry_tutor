# How animals develop

A single fertilised egg becomes a complex
multicellular animal through a stereotyped
sequence of cellular + molecular events.
Developmental biology has been a foundational
discipline since Aristotle; the modern era began
with Hans Spemann's organiser experiments in the
1920s + accelerated dramatically with molecular
genetics in the 1980s + 90s.

## The big picture

Generic vertebrate development:

1. **Fertilisation** — sperm meets egg → zygote
   (2N).
2. **Cleavage** — rapid cell divisions without growth
   → cells get smaller; produces a multicellular
   blastula.
3. **Gastrulation** — cell movements that establish
   germ layers (ectoderm, mesoderm, endoderm) +
   body axes.
4. **Neurulation** (in chordates) — formation of the
   neural tube from ectoderm.
5. **Organogenesis** — tissues + organs differentiate
   + form.
6. **Growth + maturation** — the embryo grows + organ
   systems mature; sexual maturity defines transition
   to adult.

## Fertilisation

Many species-specific aspects, but core principles:
- **Species-specific recognition** — sperm bind egg
  via species-specific proteins (sea-urchin bindin,
  mammalian ZP3 + IZUMO-JUNO, fly bouncer).
- **Acrosome reaction** — sperm releases enzymes
  to penetrate egg coats (zona pellucida in
  mammals, jelly coat in many invertebrates).
- **Membrane fusion** — sperm + egg plasma
  membranes fuse; sperm nucleus enters cytoplasm.
- **Block to polyspermy** —
  - **Fast block** — egg-membrane depolarisation
    (sea urchin) or zona-modification (mammals).
  - **Slow block** — cortical-granule release →
    enzymatic ZP modification.
- **Pronuclear fusion** + zygotic genome activation
  (within hours-days depending on species).

## Cleavage patterns

The first ~ 5-10 cell divisions partition the egg
without growth.  Pattern depends on yolk
distribution + cytoplasmic determinants:

- **Holoblastic** — complete cleavage; yolk-poor
  eggs.
  - **Radial** (echinoderms, vertebrates) — cleavage
    planes perpendicular to AP axis.
  - **Spiral** (most lophotrochozoans) — oblique;
    determinate fates.
  - **Bilateral** (ascidians, nematodes) — first
    division establishes left-right symmetry.
  - **Rotational** (mammals) — slow + asynchronous
    + blastomere compaction.
- **Meroblastic** — partial cleavage; yolk-rich
  eggs.
  - **Discoidal** (birds, reptiles, fish) — cleavage
    only in animal-pole cytoplasm sitting atop yolk.
  - **Superficial** (insects) — nuclei divide
    syncytially before cellularisation.

## Blastula

Multicellular hollow ball or solid mass:
- **Coeloblastula** — hollow (sea urchin).
- **Stereoblastula** — solid (some annelids).
- **Discoblastula** — disc atop yolk (birds).
- **Blastoderm** — peripheral layer of nuclei
  (insects, before cellularisation).
- **Mammalian blastocyst** — inner cell mass (ICM →
  embryo) + trophoblast (→ placenta) + blastocoel
  (cavity).

## Gastrulation — the most important morphogenetic
event

"It is not birth, marriage, or death but
gastrulation that is the most important moment in
your life." — Lewis Wolpert.

Cell movements:
- **Invagination** — sea urchin endoderm + cnidarian
  endoderm.
- **Involution** — frog dorsal mesoderm rolls inward
  over the dorsal lip.
- **Ingression** — individual cells migrate from
  blastula surface to interior (sea urchin
  primary mesenchyme; chick + mammal hypoblast).
- **Epiboly** — outer-layer cells spread over yolk
  / interior (zebrafish + early frog).
- **Delamination** — sheet splits into two layers.

Result:
- **Ectoderm** outside.
- **Endoderm** inside (forms gut).
- **Mesoderm** between (forms muscles + bone +
  gonads + many organs).
- **Body axes** established: anterior-posterior,
  dorsal-ventral, left-right.

## Body-axis establishment

### Anterior-posterior (AP)

- **Drosophila** — maternal *bicoid* mRNA at
  anterior pole; *nanos* at posterior; gradients
  pattern the embryo.
- **Vertebrates** — Nodal + BMP + Wnt + retinoic
  acid + FGF gradients pattern AP axis; Hox
  expression along AP.

### Dorsal-ventral (DV)

- **Drosophila** — Dorsal protein gradient; Toll
  signal.
- **Vertebrates** — BMP gradient (high BMP →
  ventral; antagonists Noggin / Chordin / Follistatin
  → dorsal).
- The dorsal-ventral axis in flies + vertebrates is
  inverted (dorsal in vertebrates ≈ ventral in
  flies) — Geoffroy Saint-Hilaire's 1822 prediction
  vindicated by molecular evidence: BMP-4 (vertebrate
  ventral) is homologous to Decapentaplegic (Dpp)
  (fly dorsal).

### Left-right (LR)

- **Vertebrates** — established by ciliary flow at
  the node → leftward Nodal expression; downstream
  Pitx2 → asymmetric heart, gut, etc.
- Disrupted in *situs inversus* + Kartagener
  syndrome (PCD; defective ciliary motility).

## Spemann organiser + induction

Hans Spemann + Hilde Mangold's 1924 experiment:
transplanted dorsal blastopore lip from one frog
embryo to another → induced a SECOND axis
+ second nervous system.  Founded the concept of
**embryonic induction** — one tissue inducing fate
in adjacent tissue via secreted signals.

Spemann's organiser secretes:
- **Noggin, Chordin, Follistatin** — BMP antagonists
  → dorsalises ectoderm to neural plate.
- **Cerberus, Dickkopf** — Wnt antagonists →
  anteriorises.
- **Frzb** — Wnt antagonist.

The vertebrate organiser is functionally equivalent
across species (Hensen's node in birds + mammals,
shield in fish, chordamesoderm in amphibians).

## Pattern formation principles

### Morphogen gradients

A signal molecule diffuses from a localised source +
forms a concentration gradient → cells respond
differentially based on position (Wolpert's French
flag model).

Classical morphogens:
- **Bicoid** (fly AP).
- **Sonic Hedgehog (Shh)** (vertebrate ventral
  neural tube + limb ZPA).
- **BMPs + Wnts + FGFs** (multiple roles).
- **Retinoic acid** (vertebrate AP, hindbrain).

### Reaction-diffusion (Turing)

Alan Turing's 1952 model: an activator + inhibitor
with different diffusion rates spontaneously form
periodic patterns.  Explains:
- Animal-coat patterns (zebra stripes, leopard
  spots).
- Hair-follicle spacing.
- Digit patterning in vertebrate limb (Sheth
  + Ros 2012 *Science*).

### Lateral inhibition

A cell adopting a fate signals neighbours to NOT
adopt the same fate (Notch-Delta signalling).
Generates spaced patterns of differentiated cells —
neurons among glia, sensory bristles in fly, hair
cells in inner ear.

### Positional information + Hox

Hox-gene expression provides AP positional
information; cells "know where they are" + adopt
appropriate fates.

## Neurulation + neural crest

In chordates, dorsal ectoderm folds + closes →
**neural tube** → CNS.

A unique vertebrate cell population — **neural crest
cells** — arises at the dorsal neural-tube edges
+ migrates extensively to form:
- Peripheral nervous system + glia.
- Pigment cells (melanocytes).
- Most facial cartilage + bone.
- Adrenal medulla.
- Aorticopulmonary septum + smooth-muscle cells of
  great vessels.
- Enteric nervous system.

Neural crest is sometimes called a "fourth germ
layer" + is a vertebrate-defining innovation.

## Organogenesis — building organs

Each organ has a stereotyped developmental programme:

- **Heart** — primary heart field + secondary heart
  field; tubular heart → looping → chamber
  formation; ~ E8.0-E12.5 in mouse.
- **Limb** — limb-bud + AER (apical ectodermal ridge,
  FGF-producing) + ZPA (zone of polarising activity,
  Shh-producing) + progress zone; pattern in 3
  axes; digit number controlled by Shh-Hox-FGF
  feedback.
- **Eye** — optic vesicle out-pockets from forebrain
  + induces lens from overlying ectoderm; lens-
  retinal feedback; pigment-epithelium ectoderm.
- **Kidney** — ureteric bud + metanephric mesenchyme
  reciprocal induction; Wnt + GDNF + Pax2 + Wt1
  signalling.
- **Pancreas** — endoderm-derived; Pdx1 + Ngn3 +
  Pax6 + MafA / B differentiation hierarchy
  → β-cells, α-cells, etc.
- **Lung** — endoderm-derived + mesenchyme
  interactions; branching morphogenesis driven by
  FGF10 + Shh.

## Model organisms in development

### Drosophila

The genetic-screening workhorse.  Christiane
Nüsslein-Volhard + Eric Wieschaus 1980 *Nature*
saturation screen identified almost every gene
required for early embryo patterning →
1995 Nobel (with Edward Lewis for homeotic genes).

Discoveries:
- Maternal-effect + zygotic genes.
- Hierarchical patterning: maternal → gap →
  pair-rule → segment-polarity → homeotic.
- Toll pathway + DV.
- Notch + lateral inhibition.

### C. elegans

Sydney Brenner's choice for dissecting development +
behaviour at single-cell resolution.

Discoveries:
- Complete cell lineage of every adult cell
  (Sulston).
- Programmed cell death (apoptosis; Horvitz, ced
  genes).
- RNA interference (Fire + Mello).
- microRNAs (lin-4 + let-7; Ambros + Ruvkun).
Multiple Nobels (2002, 2006, 2024).

### Xenopus

Big eggs + easy microinjection → biochemistry +
embryology powerhouse.  Spemann organiser; signal-
pathway dissection.

### Zebrafish

Transparent embryos + external development +
large clutches → live-imaging + forward-genetic
screens.  Key for cardiogenesis + neurogenesis +
disease modelling.

### Mouse

The mammalian standard.  Knockouts + transgenics +
conditional alleles (Cre-loxP) + reporters →
genetic dissection of mammalian development +
disease.

### Sea urchin + ascidians

Classical embryology + emerging molecular work.

## Stem cells + regeneration

- **Embryonic stem cells (ESCs)** — pluripotent;
  derived from inner cell mass.
- **Induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs)** —
  Yamanaka 2006 reprogramming with OCT4 + SOX2 +
  KLF4 + MYC; 2012 Nobel.  Patient-specific
  pluripotent cells for disease modelling +
  regenerative therapy.
- **Adult / tissue stem cells** — niches in marrow,
  intestinal crypts, hair follicle, brain
  subventricular zone, liver.
- **Regeneration champions** — planarians + axolotls
  + hydra + zebrafish heart + newt limb.  Limited
  in mammals.

## Evo-devo + comparative development

Comparing development across species reveals:
- **Deep homology** — limbs evolved from ancestral
  lobed-fin programmes.
- **Heterochrony** — shifts in developmental timing
  (axolotl neoteny; human juvenilisation).
- **Heterotopy** — shifts in spatial expression
  (limb-bud gene expression in snakes loses limb
  outgrowth).
- **Co-option** — existing genes recruited to new
  developmental contexts.
