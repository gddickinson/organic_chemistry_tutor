# Conservation biology + biodiversity loss

The Anthropocene biodiversity crisis is one of the
defining challenges of contemporary biology.  Since
1970, average vertebrate population sizes have
declined by ~ 70 % (WWF Living Planet Index 2022).
The current extinction rate is ~ 100-1 000 ×
background — comparable to the previous five mass
extinctions.

## The IUCN Red List

The authoritative global registry of extinction
risk.  > 150 000 species assessed.  Categories:
- **Extinct (EX)** — confirmed gone.
- **Extinct in the Wild (EW)** — only captive
  populations remain.
- **Critically Endangered (CR)** — extremely high
  risk in wild.
- **Endangered (EN)** — high risk.
- **Vulnerable (VU)** — high risk in medium term.
- **Near Threatened (NT)**.
- **Least Concern (LC)**.
- **Data Deficient (DD)** — insufficient data.
- **Not Evaluated (NE)**.

Categories CR + EN + VU collectively are
**threatened**.  Of all assessed species:
- ~ 40 % of amphibians.
- ~ 26 % of mammals.
- ~ 37 % of sharks + rays.
- ~ 33 % of reef-building corals.
- ~ 13 % of birds.
- ~ 21 % of reptiles.
- ~ 14 % of plants assessed.

These are global averages — rates higher in
biodiversity hotspots + island endemics.

## Drivers of biodiversity loss

### 1. Habitat loss + degradation

The dominant driver for most groups:
- **Tropical deforestation** — Amazon, Congo, SE
  Asia, especially for cattle + soy + oil palm +
  timber.
- **Wetland drainage** — > 85 % of pre-industrial
  wetlands lost globally.
- **Coral-reef bleaching** + ocean acidification.
- **Grassland conversion** to row crops.
- **Urban expansion** + infrastructure.
- **Forest fragmentation** even when net cover
  preserved → edge effects + connectivity loss.

### 2. Climate change

- Range shifts faster than many species can track.
- Phenology mismatches with food + pollinators.
- Coral bleaching + tropical-mountain endemics.
- Marine heat waves → mass mortality.
- Polar ice loss → polar bears + walrus + arctic
  cod.

### 3. Direct exploitation

- **Overfishing** — > 30 % of marine fish stocks
  overfished.
- **Bushmeat hunting** in tropical forests.
- **Wildlife trade** — including legal (live
  reptiles, birds for pet trade) + illegal (rhino
  horn, pangolin scales, ivory, exotic medicinal).
- **Wildlife use** for traditional medicine.

### 4. Invasive species

- Cats on islands → many bird + reptile + mammal
  extinctions.
- Cane toad in Australia.
- Asian carp + zebra mussels in N. American
  waterways.
- Brown tree snake on Guam → most native birds
  extinct.
- Avocado + chytrid fungus globally → amphibian
  apocalypse.

### 5. Pollution

- Plastic + microplastics in oceans + air + soil.
- Pesticides — especially neonicotinoids + insect
  decline.
- Pharmaceutical residues affecting fish + amphibian
  populations.
- Light pollution — sea turtles + insects +
  songbirds.
- Noise pollution — cetacean strandings + altered
  bird song.

### 6. Disease

- Chytridiomycosis (*Batrachochytrium dendrobatidis*
  + *B. salamandrivorans*) — > 500 amphibian
  species affected globally.
- White-nose syndrome (*Pseudogymnoascus destructans*)
  in bats.
- Tasmanian devil facial tumour disease.
- Avian influenza H5N1 in seabirds + raptors.
- Wildlife AMR transmission.

## The "sixth extinction"

Coined by Elizabeth Kolbert + others.  Distinguishing
features:
- **Driver** — single species (*H. sapiens*) rather
  than asteroid / volcanism.
- **Pace** — orders of magnitude faster than typical.
- **Selectivity** — varied; large-bodied + slow-
  reproducing taxa hardest hit.
- **Reversibility** — none (extinction is forever
  unless de-extinction technology matures).

Estimates of magnitude vary — Pimm + Raven studies
suggest current extinction rates 100-1 000 × pre-
human background; the eventual scale depends
sensitively on coming decades' choices.

## Conservation strategies

### In situ conservation

- **Protected areas** — > 17 % of land + > 10 % of
  marine area in some form of protection.  Aichi /
  CBD / 30-by-30 targets.
- **Connectivity** — corridors between protected
  areas.
- **Ecosystem-based management** — fisheries, forests,
  watersheds.
- **Indigenous + community conserved areas (ICCAs)**
  — increasingly recognised as effective.

### Ex situ conservation

- **Zoos + aquariums** — captive breeding +
  research + education.
- **Botanical gardens** — for plants.
- **Seed banks** — Svalbard Global Seed Vault +
  Millennium Seed Bank (Kew).
- **Cryo banks** — Frozen Zoo (San Diego); embryos
  + sperm + somatic cells of endangered species.

### Reintroduction + recovery

- **California condor** — captive breeding from 22
  birds in 1987 → ~ 500 today.
- **Black-footed ferret** — captive breeding + plague
  vaccination + reintroduction.
- **Arabian oryx** — extinct in wild 1972; reintroduced
  from captive populations.
- **Przewalski's horse** — Mongolian steppe.
- **European bison + wolves** — recovery in
  multiple regions.

### Rewilding

Restoration of ecosystems via:
- Predator reintroduction (wolves to Yellowstone).
- Keystone species (beavers, bison).
- Passive recovery on abandoned lands.
- Megafaunal trophic restoration (Pleistocene
  Park, Russia — mammoth-steppe restoration).

### Conservation translocation + assisted colonisation

- Move populations to climate-suitable areas as
  climate shifts.
- Controversial — risks of invasive establishment +
  unintended consequences vs slow natural dispersal.

## Conservation genetics

Genetic + genomic tools have transformed
conservation:
- **Effective population size (Ne)** estimation —
  small Ne → inbreeding + drift → reduced fitness.
- **Pedigree management** in captive breeding.
- **Genetic rescue** — gene-flow augmentation
  (Florida panther rescued by Texas cougar
  introductions).
- **Microsatellite + SNP genotyping** for population
  structure + parentage.
- **Whole-genome sequencing** of endangered species
  + characterisation of inbreeding + deleterious
  mutation load.
- **eDNA (environmental DNA)** for non-invasive
  species monitoring.
- **De-extinction** — Colossal Biosciences mammoth
  + thylacine + dodo programmes (controversial +
  ethically debated).

sibling will deepen technique-level coverage of
eDNA, conservation genomics, + de-extinction
methods.

## Insect decline

A major recent finding (Krefeld 2017 + subsequent
studies):
- ~ 75 % decline in flying-insect biomass in some
  European protected areas over 27 years.
- Multiple drivers — habitat loss + agriculture +
  pesticides + light pollution + climate.
- Ecological consequences — pollinator services,
  food-web cascades, decomposition.
- Less data from tropics where most insect
  diversity lives.

## Marine biodiversity crisis

- **Overfishing** + habitat destruction (trawling).
- **Coral-reef collapse** — bleaching + ocean
  acidification + crown-of-thorns + cyclones.
- **Plastic + chemical pollution**.
- **Industrial whaling** legacy + bycatch impacts.
- **Marine protected areas** + fishery management
  + closed seasons + spatial planning.

## Climate-change impacts on biodiversity

- Range shifts polewards + upslope.
- Phenological mismatches with prey, pollinators,
  hosts.
- Tropical mountain extinctions ("escalator to
  extinction").
- Polar ice-dependent species (polar bear, walrus,
  emperor penguin, ivory gull).
- Coral bleaching.
- Wildfires + drought-induced mortality.

By 2100, projections range from ~ 1-3 million
plant + animal species committed to extinction
under current trajectories.

## Conservation policy

- **CITES** — international wildlife trade
  regulation.
- **CBD** — Convention on Biological Diversity;
  Aichi targets (mostly missed) + post-2020
  Kunming-Montreal Global Biodiversity Framework
  (2022; 30-by-30 + funding commitments).
- **Endangered Species Act (US)** + similar national
  laws.
- **Bonn Convention** — migratory species.
- **Ramsar Convention** — wetlands.
- **IPBES** (Intergovernmental Science-Policy
  Platform on Biodiversity + Ecosystem Services)
  — IPCC-equivalent for biodiversity.

## Equity + indigenous stewardship

- 80 % of remaining global biodiversity is on
  Indigenous-managed lands.
- Conservation increasingly recognises rights-based +
  community-led approaches as more effective +
  ethical than fortress conservation.
- Co-management + benefit-sharing + Free Prior +
  Informed Consent (FPIC) standards.

## One Health framework

Animal, human, + environmental health are
inseparable.  Conservation contributes to:
- Pandemic prevention (zoonotic-spillover risk
  reduction).
- Food security (pollinators + soil biota +
  fisheries).
- Climate mitigation (forest + soil carbon).
- Cultural + ecosystem services.

## Hopeful signs

- IUCN reports ~ 800 species recovered +
  downlisted since 1990.
- Some marine populations recovering with
  protection (humpback whale, Mediterranean
  bluefin tuna in some areas).
- Renewable-energy transition reducing some
  extractive pressures.
- Rewilding + protected-area expansion accelerating
  in many regions.
- Indigenous-led conservation gaining traction.
