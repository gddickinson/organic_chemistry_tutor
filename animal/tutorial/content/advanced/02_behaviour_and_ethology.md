# Behaviour + ethology

Ethology is the study of animal behaviour in
natural contexts.  Its modern foundation is
**Tinbergen's four questions** — the framework that
unites mechanism + ontogeny + phylogeny + adaptive
function.

## Tinbergen's four questions

For any behaviour, ask:

1. **Causation (mechanism)** — what physiological,
   neural, hormonal, sensory, motor systems produce
   it?
2. **Development (ontogeny)** — how does it emerge
   during the lifespan?  How much is genetic vs
   learned vs experience-dependent?
3. **Function (adaptive value)** — how does it
   contribute to survival + reproduction?
4. **Evolution (phylogeny)** — how did it arise +
   change over evolutionary time?

These four are complementary, not competing —
satisfying explanations span all four.

Niko Tinbergen + Karl von Frisch + Konrad Lorenz
shared the 1973 Nobel Prize in Physiology / Medicine
for founding modern ethology.

## Innate vs learned

Two extremes of a continuum:

### Innate (hardwired)

- **Fixed action patterns (FAPs)** — stereotyped
  behavioural sequences triggered by sign stimuli.
  Stickleback aggression to red belly; herring-gull
  chick pecking at red-spot bill.
- **Reflexes + taxes** — tropisms in invertebrates;
  startle responses.
- **Genetic basis** clear — selective breeding can
  modify (silver-fox tameness; aggressive vs docile
  mouse strains).

### Learned

Multiple mechanisms:
- **Habituation** — decrease in response to repeated
  inconsequential stimulus.
- **Sensitisation** — opposite; enhanced response.
- **Imprinting** — early-life learning during
  critical period (Lorenz's geese).
- **Classical (Pavlovian) conditioning** — neutral
  stimulus paired with unconditioned stimulus →
  conditioned response.
- **Operant (instrumental) conditioning** — behaviour
  reinforced or punished → frequency change
  (Skinner).
- **Trial + error**.
- **Latent learning** — without immediate reward.
- **Insight learning** — sudden problem-solving.
- **Cultural / social learning** — observation +
  imitation; e.g. chimpanzee tool-use traditions,
  bird-song dialects, killer-whale hunting cultures.

Most behaviours blend innate scaffolds + experience-
dependent shaping.

## Communication

Animals communicate via:

- **Visual** — colours, displays, postures, face +
  body movements.
- **Acoustic** — calls, songs, ultrasound,
  infrasound.  Bird song, frog calls, whale song
  + ultrasonic vocalisation in rodents +
  insects + bats.
- **Chemical (pheromones)** — mate recognition,
  alarm, trail-marking, queen-pheromones in
  social insects.
- **Tactile** — grooming, touching, vibrations
  through substrate.
- **Electrical** — electric-fish discrimination +
  communication.
- **Magnetic** — orientation cues; communication
  not really documented.
- **Multi-modal** — most complex signals combine
  modalities.

Honest vs deceptive signals — Zahavi's
**handicap principle**: costly signals are honest
because cheats can't fake them.  Peacock tail,
deer antlers, energetic display.

## Foraging behaviour

- **Optimal foraging theory** — animals maximise
  net energy gain per unit time.
- **Marginal value theorem** (Charnov) — when to
  leave a depleting patch.
- **Diet breadth** vs specialisation trade-offs.
- **Predator avoidance** + risk-sensitive foraging.
- **Tool use** — chimpanzees + corvids + sea otters
  + octopus.
- **Hunting strategies** — solitary stalkers vs
  pack hunting (wolves, lions, orcas) vs cooperative
  fishing (humpback whale bubble-net).

## Social behaviour

### Dominance hierarchies

- Linear ("pecking order") in chickens + many
  primates.
- Female-dominated matrilines in elephants +
  hyenas.
- Alpha-pair in wolves + dwarf mongoose +
  callitrichine monkeys.
- Coalition + alliance dynamics in chimpanzees +
  dolphins.

### Territoriality

- Solitary + paired + group territories.
- Defence costs vs benefits — territorial when
  resources clumped + defendable, less so when
  diffuse.
- Marking — songs, scent, visual display.

### Mating systems

- **Monogamy** — pair bonds; common in birds (~ 90 %
  socially monogamous; genetic monogamy rarer).
- **Polygyny** — male mates with multiple females
  (deer, lions, fur seals).
- **Polyandry** — female mates with multiple males
  (jacanas, some shorebirds, anglerfish, dunnocks).
- **Promiscuity** — multiple mates without lasting
  bonds.

Driven by parental-investment asymmetry, sex ratios,
resource defensibility, predation pressure.

### Parental care

Highly variable:
- None (most fish + invertebrates).
- Egg-guarding only.
- Extended care (most birds + mammals).
- Cooperative breeding (~ 9 % of birds, some
  mammals — meerkats, callitrichines, naked mole
  rats).
- Kin selection (Hamilton's rule: rB > C) explains
  cooperative breeding.

### Eusociality

Highest level of social organisation:
- Reproductive division of labour (queens vs workers).
- Overlapping generations.
- Cooperative care of young.

Examples: ants, bees, wasps, termites, naked mole
rats, snapping shrimp, some aphids.

Hamilton's haplodiploid hypothesis (sister-sister
relatedness 0.75 in haplodiploids → kin-selection
favouring sterile worker castes) explains eusociality
in Hymenoptera; alternative + complementary models
account for non-haplodiploid eusocial taxa.

### Altruism + cooperation

- **Kin selection** — Hamilton.
- **Reciprocal altruism** — Trivers.
- **Direct fitness benefits** + **indirect
  fitness** + **inclusive fitness**.
- **Group selection** revival in some contexts.
- **Multi-level selection** frameworks.

## Cognition

Once heretical to discuss in non-humans, now a
mature field.

### Examples

- **Tool use + tool making** — chimpanzees, New
  Caledonian crows (specific tool types), capuchins,
  sea otters, octopuses.
- **Self-recognition** (mirror test) — chimps,
  orangutans, dolphins, elephants, magpies, manta
  rays (debated).
- **Theory of mind** — chimpanzees + dogs +
  corvids show partial evidence.
- **Numerical cognition** — chimps, dolphins,
  pigeons, parrots, fish.
- **Spatial cognition + memory** — food-caching
  birds (Clark's nutcracker, scrub jay) recall
  thousands of cache locations.
- **Causal reasoning** — corvids solve novel
  problems requiring cause-effect inference.
- **Language** — Alex the African Grey parrot;
  Kanzi the bonobo; sign-language apes; primate
  vocalisations (limited grammar).
- **Tool-traditions in chimpanzee communities** —
  cultural variation.

### The "smart vs dumb" dichotomy is misleading

Cognitive abilities are domain-specific.  Bees solve
problems unsuitable for primates (hive-temperature
regulation, dance-language communication).  Octopus
intelligence emerged independently of vertebrate
brain architecture.

## Migration + navigation

Animals navigate vast distances using:
- **Sun + sky polarisation** (bees, birds during
  day).
- **Star compass** (warblers at night).
- **Magnetic field** (sea turtles, salmon, some
  birds).
- **Olfactory cues** (salmon home-stream identification).
- **Path integration** (insects, mammals).
- **Geomagnetic + gradient mapping** (sea turtles
  + birds use complex bicoordinate maps).

Long-distance migrants:
- Arctic tern — > 70 000 km / year (poles to poles).
- Bar-tailed godwit — non-stop > 12 000 km Alaska
  to New Zealand.
- Monarch butterfly — multi-generational migration
  Mexico ↔ Canada.
- Salmon — riverine + ocean migrations.
- Wildebeest — Serengeti ~ 1 600 km circuit.

## Sensory ecology

Animals sense + perceive worlds quite different
from human Umwelt:
- **UV vision** in insects + birds + reptiles +
  rodents.
- **Polarised light** detection in many invertebrates
  + some birds.
- **Magnetoreception** likely cryptochrome-mediated
  in birds + chemical-magnetoreception evidence
  in fish + invertebrates.
- **Echolocation** — bats + dolphins.
- **Electric fields** — sharks + rays + electric
  fish + platypus.
- **Substrate vibration** — spiders + insects;
  elephant low-frequency.
- **Pheromone-detection** — moths detect a single
  molecule.
- **Pit-organ infrared** — pit vipers + boas.

Each sensory modality opens a window onto the
animal's behaviourally-relevant world.

## Behaviour genetics

- **Quantitative trait loci (QTL) mapping** —
  trace behavioural variation to chromosomal
  regions.
- **Selective breeding** — alcohol preference +
  anxiety in mice; aggression in rats; tameness in
  silver fox (Belyaev).
- **Gene knockouts + transgenics** — *fosB* in
  maternal behaviour; *Drosophila per / tim* in
  circadian rhythm.
- **GWAS for human behaviour** — modest effect
  sizes per locus; polygenic.
- **Optogenetics + chemogenetics** — manipulate
  specific neurons during behaviour to test
  causality.

## Sociobiology + the modern debate

- **Sociobiology** (E.O. Wilson 1975) — applying
  evolutionary frameworks to animal + human
  behaviour.
- **Evolutionary psychology** — extends to human
  cognition + behaviour.
- **Behavioural ecology** — quantitative ecology of
  behaviour, mate choice, parental investment,
  optimal foraging.
- **Animal welfare science** — sentience, pain,
  emotion in non-human animals; informs ethics +
  policy.
- **Cultural evolution** + cumulative culture in
  apes + corvids + cetaceans.
