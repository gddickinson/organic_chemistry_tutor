# Plant climate-change biology

Plants are at the heart of the climate-change story
— they're both heavily IMPACTED by climate change +
a major COMPONENT of the global climate system via
the carbon + water cycles.  Plant climate-change
biology is now one of the most active areas of
plant science.

## What's changing for plants

### Atmospheric CO2

- **Pre-industrial** — ~ 280 ppm.
- **2026** — ~ 425 ppm + rising ~ 2.5 ppm / year.
- **2050 projection** — 500-600 ppm depending on
  emissions trajectory.

Direct effects on plants — covered below.

### Temperature

- **Global mean** — > 1.2 °C above pre-industrial.
- **Land warming** — faster than ocean (~ 1.6 °C).
- **Phenology shifts** — earlier spring; later
  autumn; species-specific advances.
- **Heat-stress events** — multi-day extreme heat
  causing crop failures (Europe 2003, Russia 2010,
  US 2012, India 2022).

### Hydrological shifts

- Precipitation changes (regional — wetter in some
  high latitudes, drier in many subtropics).
- Increased rainfall variability + drought / flood
  oscillations.
- Snowpack reduction → earlier streamflow.
- Vapour-pressure deficit (VPD) increases drive
  more evapotranspiration.

### Other

- Tropospheric ozone (phytotoxic).
- N deposition (fertilising in some regions, acid
  in others).
- Sea-level rise affecting coastal vegetation.
- Altered fire regimes.

## CO2 fertilisation effect

Higher atmospheric CO2 directly increases
photosynthesis in C3 plants (RuBisCO sees more
substrate; less photorespiration).  Free-air CO2
enrichment (FACE) experiments quantify the effect.

Findings:
- C3 plants — ~ 12-25 % photosynthesis enhancement at
  600 ppm vs 380 ppm.
- C4 plants — minimal direct effect (already
  CO2-saturated via C4 pump).
- Yield gains often LESS than photosynthesis gains
  (sink limitation, N-limitation, photosynthetic
  acclimation downward over time).
- Nutritional quality declines — protein + Zn + Fe
  + B vitamins drop in CO2-enriched grain
  ("hidden hunger" risk).

## C4 vs C3 in a warming world

Two competing forces:

- **Higher CO2 favours C3** — reduces photorespiration
  penalty.
- **Higher temperatures + drought favour C4 + CAM**
  — lower photorespiration + better water-use
  efficiency.

Net result varies by region + crop.  Models
generally predict:
- **Maize, sugarcane, sorghum (C4)** — modest yield
  changes; gains in some regions.
- **Wheat, rice, soybean (C3)** — yield gains in
  cool regions, losses in hot regions.
- Geographic suitability of crops shifts (vine
  expansion northward; wheat zones migrating).

## Drought + stomatal regulation

A central puzzle: at higher CO2, optimal stomatal
behaviour is to PARTIALLY CLOSE (still gain CO2 +
save water).  Most plants do exhibit this, with
~ 5-15 % stomatal-conductance reduction at +200 ppm.

Consequences:
- Reduced transpiration → less leaf cooling → MORE
  heat stress.
- Reduced transpiration → LESS local evapotranspiration
  → more land warming.
- Less moisture flux to atmosphere → potential
  feedbacks on regional precipitation.

## Stress responses + tolerance mechanisms

Plants face multiple compound stresses:
- Heat + drought.
- Heat + high light.
- Drought + salinity.
- Multiple pathogens + pests under altered
  climates.

### Heat stress

- HSP (heat-shock-protein) chaperones.
- Heat-shock factors (HSFs) regulate the response.
- Membrane fluidity + ROS damage.
- Pollen + reproductive development is
  particularly heat-sensitive — explains most
  yield losses in cereal heat events.

### Drought

- ABA-mediated stomatal closure.
- Osmoprotectants (proline, glycine betaine,
  trehalose, polyols).
- Late-embryogenesis-abundant (LEA) proteins.
- Aquaporin regulation.
- Root architectural plasticity (deeper roots).

### Cold + freezing

- Cold-acclimation gene expression (CBF / DREB
  TF cascade).
- Compatible solutes (sugar + proline + glycine
  betaine).
- Ice-binding proteins / antifreeze proteins
  (limited in plants).
- Membrane lipid composition tuning.

### Salt

- Na+ exclusion (plasma-membrane SOS1 antiporter
  + tonoplast NHX sequestration).
- Compatible solutes + K+ retention.
- Reduction of Na+ uptake by HKT1 transporters.

### Flooding / hypoxia

- Aerenchyma formation (gas-filled spaces).
- Adventitious-root development.
- Submergence-tolerance loci (SUB1 in rice — Pamela
  Ronald lab; ERF-VII transcription factors).
- Quiescence vs escape strategies.

## Phenology + range shifts

- **Spring leaf-out** advancing ~ 2-5 days / decade
  in many temperate species.
- **Flowering** advancing similarly; phenological
  mismatch with pollinators a documented concern.
- **Tree lines** moving polewards + upslope
  (~ 100-1 000 m / decade in some mountain
  ranges).
- **Boreal-temperate transition** northward.
- **Tropical species** ill-equipped for novel
  conditions; tropical mountain endemics
  particularly threatened ("escalator to extinction").

## Fire + disturbance regimes

- More extensive + intense fires in many regions
  (US west, Australia, Mediterranean, boreal).
- Fire-adapted species with serotinous cones
  (lodgepole pine, jack pine) face altered cone
  release dynamics.
- Post-fire recovery may not fully restore previous
  vegetation in some areas → biome transitions.
- Some species require fire (chaparral) but may face
  too-frequent fires now.

## Forest carbon sink

Land vegetation absorbs ~ 30 % of anthropogenic CO2
emissions (~ 3 Pg C / year net).  Major sinks:
- **Tropical forests** — large sink + large flux
  uncertainty.
- **Temperate forests** — recovering from past
  deforestation.
- **Boreal forests** — large stocks; vulnerable to
  fire + permafrost thaw.

Land-sink durability is uncertain — climate change
itself can flip a sink to a source via:
- Drought-induced mortality (Amazon 2005, 2010,
  2015 droughts).
- Fire-driven losses.
- Pest + pathogen outbreaks (mountain pine beetle,
  bark beetles, hemlock woolly adelgid).
- Permafrost thaw releases legacy soil carbon.

The Amazon basin has shown signs of sink-to-source
transition in southeast (Gatti 2021 *Nature*).

## Climate impact on agriculture

Crop modelling consensus:
- ~ 5-10 % yield decline per °C warming for
  C3 cereals globally without adaptation.
- Larger declines in tropical / subtropical regions.
- Variable + season-dependent in temperate.
- Heatwaves disproportionately damaging (multi-day
  35+ °C during pollination is catastrophic).

Adaptation responses:
- Earlier planting (where soil moisture allows).
- Shifting crop varieties + species mix.
- Irrigation expansion (water-resource constrained).
- Heat-tolerant + drought-tolerant breeding (hybrid
  + GM + CRISPR varieties).
- Agroforestry + cover-cropping for soil moisture +
  microclimate.
- Decision-support tools for farmers.

## Carbon-sequestration potential

Quantifying the carbon-sink potential of land:
- **Reforestation + afforestation** — IPCC scenarios
  use ~ 1-3 Pg C / year via reforestation.
- **Soil carbon sequestration** — agriculture-based
  practices (cover crops, no-till, biochar,
  perennial cropping).
- **Bioenergy with CCS (BECCS)** — controversial
  large-scale negative-emission technology.
- **Forest management** — managed forests may
  outperform unmanaged in some carbon metrics.

Limits:
- Biophysical constraints on tree-cover potential.
- Trade-offs with food security + biodiversity.
- Permanence (fire / pests / harvest / drought
  reversal).
- Social + governance challenges.

## Plant species + biodiversity loss

- Habitat loss + climate change drive ~ 1 in 5
  plant species toward extinction by century-end.
- Tropical mountain endemics + small-range species
  most vulnerable.
- Pollinator decline (bees + bats + birds + others)
  threatens ~ 87 % of flowering plant species
  dependent on animal pollination.
- IUCN + State of the World's Plants reports
  document trends.

## Ecological + evolutionary consequences

- **Range shifts + community reassembly** — novel
  community combinations forming.
- **Microevolutionary responses** — phenology +
  thermal tolerance + drought tolerance evolving in
  some species over decades (rapid contemporary
  evolution).
- **Mass mortality events** — drought + heat + fire
  + pest synergies.
- **Tipping points** — Amazon drought-fire feedback,
  boreal fire-CO2 feedback, shrub-grass feedback in
  Arctic.

## Adaptation tools

Plant biology contributes:
- **Modelling** at scales from leaf (Farquhar-von
  Caemmerer) to ecosystem (CASA, ED) to global
  (Earth System Models with land-vegetation
  components).
- **Monitoring** — eddy-covariance flux towers
  (FLUXNET ~ 800 sites), satellite remote sensing
  (MODIS, OCO-2 / 3, SIF, Sentinel).
- **Experiments** — FACE, OTC (open-top chambers),
  warming arrays, drought experiments, common-garden
  + reciprocal-transplant studies.
- **Crop breeding + biotech** — climate-tolerant
  varieties (covered in BT-3.0 advanced
  Plant Biotechnology lesson).
- **Policy + economics** — REDD+, voluntary +
  compliance carbon markets, payments for ecosystem
  services.
