# Plant cell architecture

The plant cell shares the basic eukaryotic plan with
animal cells but adds three distinctive features that
together define plant biology: a **cellulose cell wall**,
**plastids** (chloroplasts + others), and a **large
central vacuole**.

## The cell wall

The plant cell wall is a structural masterpiece that
provides shape, strength, defence, + selective
permeability while remaining flexible enough to
accommodate growth.

### Layered architecture

- **Middle lamella** — pectin-rich, glues adjacent
  cells together.  First wall layer to form during
  cytokinesis.
- **Primary cell wall** — laid down during cell
  expansion; flexible; cellulose + hemicellulose +
  pectin + structural proteins.
- **Secondary cell wall** — laid down after cell
  expansion stops in some cell types (xylem vessels,
  fibres, sclereids); thick + strong + often
  lignified.

### Major polymers

**Cellulose** (β-1,4-glucan) — the primary load-bearing
polymer.  Cellulose synthase complexes (CSCs) embedded
in the plasma membrane spin out 18-24 chain bundles
that crystallise into microfibrils ~ 3 nm thick.  Each
CSC is guided by underlying cortical microtubules,
giving cell-wall directional reinforcement.

**Hemicellulose** — a heterogeneous group:
- Xyloglucan (most dicots).
- Glucuronoarabinoxylan (grasses, Poaceae).
- Mannans + galactomannans + glucomannans.
- (1,3;1,4)-β-glucan (mixed-linkage glucan;
  Poaceae specifically).

Hemicelluloses cross-link cellulose microfibrils +
contribute to wall mechanics.

**Pectins** — Ca²⁺-cross-linked galacturonan-rich
gels.
- Homogalacturonan (HG).
- Rhamnogalacturonan I (RG-I).
- Rhamnogalacturonan II (RG-II) — boron-cross-linked
  dimer.

Pectins fill the void between cellulose-hemicellulose,
control porosity, regulate cell-cell adhesion + ion
exchange.

**Lignin** — only in secondary walls of certain cells.
Phenolic polymer (p-coumaryl + coniferyl + sinapyl
alcohol monolignols).  Provides compressive strength,
hydrophobicity, decay resistance.  Critical
evolutionary innovation enabling vertical-tree growth.

**Structural proteins** — extensins (HRGPs),
arabinogalactan proteins (AGPs), glycine-rich
proteins.  Cell-type-specific structural roles +
signalling.

### Wall enzymes

- **Expansins** — non-enzymatic loosening proteins;
  H+-activated; enable wall extension during growth.
- **Cellulases (endoglucanases)** + **xyloglucan
  endotransglycosylases (XETs)** — wall-loosening
  enzymes.
- **Pectin methylesterases (PMEs)** — de-methyl-
  esterify HG → Ca²⁺-crosslinkable form → wall
  stiffening.
- **Polygalacturonases** — degrade HG; involved in
  fruit ripening + abscission.

## The plastid family

Plastids are organelles with their own genomes
(circular, ~ 130 kb in most plants), descended from
the cyanobacterial primary endosymbiosis ~ 1.5 Ga ago.

### Chloroplast

- Photosynthesis.
- Inner + outer membranes.
- Stroma (matrix) — Calvin cycle enzymes + ribosomes
  + plastid genome.
- Thylakoid membrane — light reactions; folded into
  grana stacks connected by stromal lamellae.
- Pigments: chlorophyll a + b, carotenoids
  (β-carotene, lutein, zeaxanthin, violaxanthin).

### Other plastid types

- **Chromoplast** — pigment-rich; flower + fruit
  colours (carotenoids in tomatoes, peppers,
  carrots).
- **Amyloplast** — starch-storing (potato, root
  caps); also gravity-sensing in roots
  (statolith function).
- **Elaioplast** — lipid-storing (oilseeds + petals).
- **Proteinoplast** — protein-storing.
- **Leucoplast** — colourless; precursor to other
  plastid types.
- **Etioplast** — dark-grown chloroplast precursor;
  prolamellar body; converts to chloroplast on light
  exposure.
- **Gerontoplast** — senescent chloroplast.

All plastid types are interconvertible — chloroplasts
become chromoplasts during fruit ripening, etioplasts
become chloroplasts when seedlings break ground.

## The large central vacuole

In mature plant cells, the vacuole occupies 50-90 % of
cell volume.  Functions:

- **Turgor maintenance** — internal hydrostatic
  pressure that keeps leaves expanded + supports
  young stems.  Loss of turgor → wilting.
- **Storage** — sugars (sucrose), organic acids
  (citric, malic), ions (K⁺, Cl⁻, NO3⁻), pigments
  (anthocyanins giving red / blue colours), allelo-
  chemicals + defensive compounds, terpenes,
  alkaloids.
- **Lytic compartment** — analogous to animal
  lysosomes; acidic pH 5.0-5.5; hydrolases for
  protein + nucleic-acid + polysaccharide degradation.
- **Sequestration of toxins + heavy metals**.
- **pH regulation + ion homeostasis** of cytoplasm.

Tonoplast (vacuolar membrane) hosts:
- V-ATPase + V-PPase (vacuolar pyrophosphatase) —
  proton pumps establishing the pH gradient.
- TIPs (tonoplast intrinsic proteins) — aquaporins.
- Ion transporters + ABC pumps for sequestration.

## Plasmodesmata

Cytoplasmic bridges (50-60 nm diameter) that traverse
the cell wall + interconnect adjacent plant cells —
the **symplast** (cytoplasmic continuum) of the entire
plant.  Functionally analogous to animal gap
junctions.

Architecture:
- Plasma-membrane lining.
- Central desmotubule (modified ER).
- Cytoplasmic sleeve through which molecules + ions
  + RNAs + viruses + proteins can diffuse / be
  trafficked.
- Size-exclusion limit (SEL) regulated by callose
  deposition + viral movement proteins + neck
  proteins.

Functional roles:
- Hormone + signal transport.
- Phloem loading / unloading.
- Developmental coordination across cell territories.
- Viral cell-to-cell movement (most plant viruses
  encode movement proteins that increase the SEL).

## Other distinctive features

### Peroxisomes

Glyoxysomes (oilseeds — fatty-acid β-oxidation
+ glyoxylate cycle; convert oil to sugar during
seedling germination) + leaf peroxisomes
(photorespiration).

### Mitochondria + ETC

Standard with one twist: many plant mitochondria
have an alternative oxidase (AOX) that bypasses
Complexes III + IV → uncoupled respiration → heat
generation in skunk cabbage + arum lily flowers.

### Cytoskeleton

- **Actin microfilaments** — cytoplasmic streaming
  ("cyclosis") + organelle positioning + tip growth
  in pollen tubes / root hairs.
- **Microtubules** — cortical arrays guide cellulose-
  synthase trajectory + thus wall reinforcement
  direction.  Spindle + phragmoplast in division.
- No centrioles in higher plants; nucleation is
  diffuse / γ-tubulin-anchored.

### Cell division specifics

Plant cytokinesis is unusual:
- Phragmoplast — microtubule + actin scaffold
  midway between daughter nuclei.
- Vesicles (Golgi-derived; cellulose synthases +
  pectin precursors) coalesce into the cell plate.
- Cell plate expands centrifugally → fuses with
  parental wall.
- Plasmodesmata form during this process via
  ER-membrane trapping.

### Differentiation

Plants tend to develop POST-EMBRYONICALLY from
meristems — cell fates are largely determined by
positional cues (auxin gradients, intercellular
signals) rather than lineage.  This makes plants
remarkably regenerative — most cells can dedifferentiate
+ form callus → regenerate organs (the basis of
plant tissue culture + transgenic plant production).
