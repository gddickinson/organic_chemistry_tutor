# What botany is

Botany is the study of plants — their structure,
function, evolution, ecology, and relationships with
humans.  Plants are the foundation of almost every
terrestrial food web + the source of essentially all
biomass we burn or eat.

## Defining "plant"

Modern plant systematics (kingdom Plantae) covers:
- **Embryophytes** (land plants) — bryophytes,
  lycophytes, ferns + horsetails, gymnosperms,
  angiosperms.
- **Green algae** (Chlorophyta + Streptophyta) —
  the closest aquatic relatives + ancestors of
  land plants.
- **Red algae** (Rhodophyta) + **glaucophytes** —
  early-branching photosynthetic eukaryotes.

**Excluded** despite often being grouped with plants
informally: fungi (closer to animals), brown algae
(stramenopiles), oomycetes, lichens (fungi-algae
symbioses).

The unifying features of true plants:
- Photoautotrophs with primary plastids descended from
  the cyanobacterial endosymbiosis.
- Cellulose cell walls.
- Sexual reproduction with alternation of generations
  (gametophyte ↔ sporophyte).
- Chlorophyll a + b (in green plants).

## Why plants matter

### As primary producers

Plants + algae fix ~ 100 Pg C / year via photosynthesis.
This is the energy currency that drives essentially
every ecosystem + most fossil fuels.

### As food

Humans get ~ 80 % of caloric intake from just three
crops: rice, wheat, maize.  Adding potato, soybean,
cassava, sweet potato, sugarcane, sugarbeet,
sorghum, banana → ~ 95 % of human calories.

Crop diversification (yam, millet, quinoa, teff,
lentil, chickpea, fava, pulse legumes more generally)
matters for nutrition + climate-resilience + cultural
food sovereignty.

### As medicine

The pharmacopoeia is plant-derived to a striking
extent:
- **Morphine + codeine** — opium poppy (*Papaver
  somniferum*).
- **Quinine** — *Cinchona* bark (anti-malarial).
- **Artemisinin** — *Artemisia annua* (anti-malarial;
  Tu Youyou 2015 Nobel).
- **Taxol (paclitaxel)** — Pacific yew (*Taxus
  brevifolia*).
- **Vinblastine + vincristine** — Madagascar
  periwinkle (*Catharanthus roseus*).
- **Digitalis** — foxglove.
- **Aspirin** — willow bark (salicin) → salicylic
  acid → acetylsalicylic acid.
- **Atropine + scopolamine + hyoscyamine** —
  Solanaceae alkaloids.
- **Galantamine** — *Galanthus* (snowdrop).
- **Caffeine** — coffee + tea.
- **THC + CBD** — *Cannabis sativa*.

### As industrial feedstock

Cotton, flax, hemp (textile), rubber (*Hevea*), cork
(Quercus suber), turpentine (pines), gum arabic
(*Acacia*), agave fibre, bamboo construction.

### As fuel

Wood + crop biomass remain primary energy for ~ 2.5
billion people.  Modern biofuels — bioethanol from
sugarcane / corn, biodiesel from oilseed crops, algal
fuels — are a contested-but-active sector.

### As ecosystem engineers

Forest + grassland + wetland plants drive the
hydrological cycle, soil formation, flood control,
biodiversity, carbon sequestration, pollinator
support.

## Plant evolutionary history

A whirlwind:
- **~ 3.5 Ga** — first oxygenic photosynthesis
  (cyanobacteria); the Great Oxygenation Event
  ~ 2.4 Ga.
- **~ 1.6 Ga** — primary endosymbiosis: a
  heterotrophic eukaryote engulfs a cyanobacterium →
  primary plastid → ancestor of glaucophytes + red +
  green algae.
- **~ 470-500 Ma** — green plants colonise land
  (Ordovician); first embryophytes were liverwort-
  like bryophytes.
- **~ 425 Ma** — first vascular plants (Silurian);
  *Cooksonia*.
- **~ 380 Ma** — ferns + Lycopsida (Devonian);
  *Archaeopteris*.
- **~ 360 Ma** — first seed plants (gymnosperms);
  Carboniferous coal swamps.
- **~ 300 Ma** — first conifers, cycads, ginkgoes.
- **~ 130 Ma** — angiosperms (flowering plants);
  Cretaceous explosion.
- **~ 70-65 Ma** — angiosperm dominance; co-evolution
  with insects + birds + mammals.
- **~ 30-15 Ma** — grasses + grassland biomes →
  C4 photosynthesis evolved.

## Modern plant phylogeny snapshot

Major lineages:
- **Bryophytes** (~ 20 K species) — liverworts,
  mosses, hornworts.  Non-vascular; gametophyte
  dominant.
- **Lycophytes** (~ 1 K species) — clubmosses,
  spikemosses, quillworts.  Microphylls.
- **Monilophytes** (~ 12 K species) — ferns,
  horsetails, whisk ferns.  Megaphylls; vascular.
- **Gymnosperms** (~ 1 K species) — conifers, cycads,
  ginkgo, gnetophytes.  Naked seeds; mostly wind-
  pollinated.
- **Angiosperms** (~ 350 K species — > 90 % of land
  plants):
  - Basal angiosperms (Amborella + water lilies +
    star anise + Magnoliaceae).
  - Monocots (~ 70 K — grasses, palms, orchids,
    lilies).
  - Eudicots (~ 250 K — rosids + asterids dominate
    most temperate floras).

## How botanists work today

- **Field botany** — collection, taxonomic ID,
  ecological survey.  Remains foundational.
- **Herbarium science** — > 350 M specimens globally;
  digitisation underway (iDigBio, JSTOR Global Plants).
- **Plant systematics + molecular phylogenetics** —
  rDNA + plastid + nuclear-genome phylogenies.
- **Plant physiology** — photosynthesis, water
  relations, hormone signalling, stress responses.
- **Plant biochemistry** — secondary metabolite
  pathways, cell-wall biosynthesis.
- **Plant genetics + breeding** — quantitative
  trait loci (QTLs), genomic selection, marker-
  assisted breeding, transgenics, CRISPR-edited crops.
- **Plant cell + developmental biology** — meristems,
  organ patterning, polar auxin transport, signal
  cascades.
- **Plant ecology** — community + ecosystem +
  global biogeochemistry.
- **Ethnobotany + economic botany** — plant uses by
  human cultures.
- **Plant climate-change biology** — phenology,
  range shifts, carbon flux.

## Major model plants

- **Arabidopsis thaliana** — small mustard;
  workhorse genetic model since 1980s; genome
  sequenced 2000.
- **Oryza sativa** (rice) — major crop + monocot
  model.
- **Zea mays** (maize) — crop + transposon biology
  (Barbara McClintock).
- **Solanum lycopersicum** (tomato) — crop +
  fruit-development model.
- **Nicotiana benthamiana / tabacum** —
  agroinfiltration + transient expression workhorse.
- **Physcomitrium patens** — moss model for
  bryophyte biology.
- **Marchantia polymorpha** — liverwort model.
- **Selaginella moellendorffii** — lycophyte model.
- **Brachypodium distachyon** — small grass model
  for cereal biology.
- **Populus trichocarpa** — first tree genome (2006).
