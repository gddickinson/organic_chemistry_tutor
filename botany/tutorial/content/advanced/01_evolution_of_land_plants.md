# Evolution of land plants

The colonisation of land by plants ~ 470 million
years ago is one of the most consequential events
in Earth's history.  Land plants drove the modern
oxygen-rich atmosphere, the carbon-sink land surface,
the development of soils, the evolution of insects +
tetrapod vertebrates + mammals + ultimately humans.
Their phylogeny is a case study in major
evolutionary transitions.

## Sister + ancestor

Phylogenomics + chloroplast-encoded genes confirm
that land plants (embryophytes) descend from a
single colonisation event — the ancestor was a
freshwater **streptophyte alga** related to modern
*Zygnema* / *Spirogyra* (Zygnematophyceae) +
Charophyceae.

Key pre-adaptations of streptophyte algae for land
colonisation:
- Plasmodesmata.
- Phragmoplast-mediated cytokinesis.
- Phycobilin loss + chlorophyll a + b-only
  photosynthesis.
- Sex hormones + gametophyte-sporophyte life
  cycle precursors.
- Some evidence of cuticle + tolerance of
  desiccation.

## Bryophytes — the first land plants

Three lineages:
- **Liverworts (Marchantiophyta)** — earliest-
  diverging.  Thalloid (*Marchantia*) + leafy
  forms.
- **Mosses (Bryophyta)** — most diverse (~ 12 000
  species); leafy gametophyte; sporophyte borne
  on gametophyte.
- **Hornworts (Anthocerotophyta)** — single chloro-
  plast per cell; sporophyte with intercalary
  meristem (continued growth).

Innovations enabling land life:
- **Cuticle** — wax-cutin barrier minimising water
  loss.
- **Sporopollenin** — extremely durable spore-wall
  polymer; resists desiccation + UV + microbial
  decay.
- **Stomata** (in mosses + hornworts; lost in
  liverworts secondarily) — regulated gas exchange.
- **Multicellular embryo** — protected by maternal
  gametophyte tissue → "embryophytes".
- **Multicellular gametangia** (archegonia, antheridia)
  — protected gamete development.

Limitations:
- Gametophyte-dominant + sperm flagellated → require
  liquid-water film for fertilisation → terrestrial
  but tied to moisture.
- No true vascular tissue → small + low-statured.
- No roots — rhizoids attach but don't actively
  absorb minerals.

## Vascular tissue — the tracheophyte revolution

~ 425 Ma (Silurian), the **vascular plants
(tracheophytes)** evolved:
- **Tracheids** — water-conducting cells with
  lignified secondary walls.
- **Rhizoids → roots** — true absorption + anchor.
- **Sporophyte-dominant life cycle** — the
  diploid plant becomes the vegetative body.

This enabled erect growth, larger size, drier
habitats.  *Cooksonia* + Rhynie chert plants are
the iconic Silurian-Devonian vascular fossils.

## Lycophytes + monilophytes

- **Lycophytes** (clubmosses + spikemosses +
  quillworts) — microphylls (single vein), dichotomous
  branching, sister to all other tracheophytes.
- **Monilophytes** (ferns + horsetails + whisk ferns)
  — megaphylls (branched veins), monopodial
  branching.

Both groups dominated Carboniferous coal swamps
(the source of much of the world's coal — *Lepidodendron*
+ *Calamites* + tree ferns).

Both still have flagellated sperm + free-living
gametophyte → still tied to moisture for sexual
reproduction.

## Seeds — escape from water

The seed plants (spermatophytes) emerged ~ 360 Ma:
- **Pollen** — desiccation-resistant male
  gametophyte → wind transport possible.
- **Ovule** — protected female gametophyte attached
  to parent sporophyte.
- **Seed** — embryo + nutrient store + protective
  coat — survives dispersal + dormancy.

Sexual reproduction no longer needs water.

## Gymnosperms

~ 360-300 Ma diversification:
- **Conifers** (pines, spruces, firs, cedars,
  yews) — > 600 species; dominant in cold +
  boreal forests; needle leaves; resin defences;
  woody stems with tracheids only (no vessels).
- **Cycads** — palm-like; ancient lineage; live
  symbiosis with cyanobacteria in coralloid roots.
- **Ginkgo biloba** — sole survivor of the Ginkgophyta;
  living fossil; dioecious; still in cultivation
  worldwide.
- **Gnetophytes** (Ephedra, Welwitschia, Gnetum) —
  unusual; vessels in xylem; some convergent
  features with angiosperms.

Naked seeds borne on cone scales (or modified
structures); wind-pollinated mostly.

## Angiosperms — the dominant flora

~ 130 Ma origin (or earlier — Triassic estimates
exist).

Innovations:
- **Flowers** — modified shoots specialised for
  pollination + protection of ovule(s).
- **Carpels** — protect ovules within an ovary.
- **Double fertilisation** — produces nutrient
  endosperm, only laid down after fertilisation
  (vs gymnosperm gametophyte tissue, made
  pre-fertilisation regardless of need).
- **Vessels** — wider water-conducting cells →
  higher hydraulic efficiency.
- **Companion cells** — supplemental support for
  enucleate sieve elements.
- **Co-evolution with insect pollinators + frugivores**
  — drives the angiosperm radiation.
- **Faster reproductive cycle + smaller genomes
  often** — adaptive flexibility.

Major angiosperm groups:
- **Basal angiosperms** (Amborella + water lilies +
  star anise) — early-diverging; show ancestral
  features.
- **Magnoliids** — magnolias, avocados, peppers,
  bay laurel, cinnamon.
- **Monocots** (~ 70 K species) — single cotyledon,
  parallel veins, scattered vascular bundles, no
  secondary growth (most species).  Grasses, palms,
  orchids, lilies, gingers.
- **Eudicots** (~ 250 K species) — two cotyledons,
  net venation, vascular bundles in a ring,
  secondary growth via cambium.  Most familiar
  flowering plants (roses, beans, sunflowers,
  oaks, mints).

The Cretaceous → Cenozoic angiosperm radiation
restructured terrestrial ecosystems + created
the foundation for modern grasslands, deciduous
forests, tropical rainforests.

## Key innovations + their consequences

| Innovation | First appearance | Major consequence |
|------------|------------------|-------------------|
| Cuticle + sporopollenin | Bryophytes | Surviving on land |
| Stomata | Mosses + hornworts | Controlled gas exchange |
| Multicellular embryo | Bryophytes | Protected development |
| Vascular tissue | Tracheophytes | Vertical growth + bigger size |
| Lignin | Tracheophytes | Mechanical support, decay resistance |
| Roots | Tracheophytes | Active mineral uptake |
| Pollen | Seed plants | Sex without water |
| Seeds | Seed plants | Dispersal + dormancy |
| Flowers + carpels | Angiosperms | Pollinator co-evolution + ovule protection |
| Vessels + companion cells | Angiosperms | Hydraulic + transport efficiency |
| Double fertilisation | Angiosperms | Conditional endosperm investment |
| Fruits | Angiosperms | Animal-mediated dispersal |
| C4 photosynthesis | Late Cenozoic grasses | Hot / arid productivity |

## Major events in plant evolutionary history

- **~ 1.6 Ga** — Primary endosymbiosis (cyanobacterium
  → plastid).
- **~ 470 Ma** — Land colonisation (bryophyte-grade).
- **~ 425 Ma** — First vascular plants.
- **~ 410 Ma** — Rhynie chert preserves entire early
  land plant ecosystem.
- **~ 380 Ma** — First trees (*Archaeopteris*).
- **~ 360 Ma** — First seed plants.
- **~ 320 Ma** — Carboniferous coal swamps;
  *Lepidodendron* + *Calamites* + early conifers.
- **~ 250 Ma** — Permian-Triassic extinction; gymnosperm
  + lycophyte → fern dominance shift.
- **~ 200 Ma** — Earliest angiosperm-like fossils
  (controversial); definite by ~ 130 Ma.
- **~ 100 Ma** — Mid-Cretaceous angiosperm radiation.
- **~ 65 Ma** — K-Pg extinction; grassland precursors;
  angiosperm dominance solidified.
- **~ 30-15 Ma** — C4 grasses + grassland biomes
  expand globally.
- **~ 12 Ka** — Agriculture begins; humans become
  selective force on plants.
- **20th-21st centuries** — Green Revolution +
  GM crops + CRISPR; humans + climate dominate
  plant-evolution dynamics.

## Polyploidy + whole-genome duplication

Polyploidy (genome doubling) is rare in animals
but VERY common in plants — most major plant
lineages have polyploid origins:
- Modern wheat is hexaploid (genomes from three
  ancestral diploid wheats).
- Cotton is tetraploid (from Old + New World
  diploids).
- Strawberry, sugarcane, potato + many others.
- Whole-genome duplication (WGD) events leave
  signatures in synteny + Ks distribution; ALL
  flowering plants share at least 2 ancient WGDs;
  many lineages have additional more-recent events.

WGD provides genetic raw material for innovation —
gene neofunctionalisation, subfunctionalisation,
buffering against deleterious mutations.

## Convergent evolution

The same evolutionary "solution" has arisen
repeatedly:
- **C4 photosynthesis** evolved > 60× independently
  in 19+ families.
- **CAM photosynthesis** evolved > 60× independently.
- **Tree habit** — many independent origins
  across angiosperms (multiple origins within
  some genera).
- **Aquatic habit** — many independent origins.
- **Insectivorous habit** — sundews, pitcher plants,
  Venus flytraps, bladderworts, butterworts —
  multiple origins in nutrient-poor habitats.
- **Mimicry** — orchids mimicking bee females
  (multiple origins); rope-mimic vines.

## Future plant evolution under anthropogenic pressure

Humans are the dominant evolutionary force on
plants now:
- Direct selection (cultivation, breeding).
- Indirect selection (climate change, habitat
  conversion, herbicide use).
- Range shifts + assisted migration.
- De-domestication of crop relatives + weedy
  hybridisation.
- Loss of plant biodiversity at unprecedented
  rates.
