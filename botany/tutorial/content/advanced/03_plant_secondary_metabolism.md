# Plant secondary metabolism

Plants are master chemists — collectively producing
> 200 000 secondary metabolites that drive defence,
ecological signalling, pigmentation, + the entire
pre-modern human pharmacopoeia.  Three biosynthetic
families dominate: **alkaloids**, **terpenoids**,
**phenylpropanoids + flavonoids**.

## Why "secondary"?

The term was coined when these compounds appeared
"non-essential" for primary metabolism (growth +
basic energy + macromolecule biosynthesis).  We now
recognise that secondary metabolites are
ESSENTIAL for ecological + reproductive fitness —
the original "secondary" framing is historical.

Modern view: secondary metabolites are
species-specific or lineage-specific compounds
produced from primary metabolic precursors via
specialised enzyme pathways — defining the chemical
ecology of each plant species.

## Alkaloids — N-containing bioactives

~ 12 000 known structures.  Generally bitter +
pharmacologically active.  Most derive from
aromatic + aliphatic amino acids.

### Major sub-classes by precursor

- **Tropane alkaloids** (Solanaceae) — derived from
  ornithine.  *Atropa* / *Hyoscyamus* / *Datura* /
  *Brugmansia*.  Atropine (mAChR antagonist;
  *Atropa belladonna* "deadly nightshade"),
  scopolamine, hyoscyamine.  Cocaine (*Erythroxylum
  coca*) is also tropane-class.
- **Pyrrolizidines** — defence; livestock-toxic
  (Senecio + Crotalaria + Heliotropium).
- **Quinoline + isoquinoline** — derived from tyrosine.
  Morphine + codeine + thebaine (*Papaver
  somniferum*); colchicine (*Colchicum autumnale*);
  papaverine; berberine; tubocurarine.
- **Indole alkaloids** — derived from tryptophan.
  Vinblastine + vincristine (*Catharanthus roseus*);
  ergot alkaloids (*Claviceps*); strychnine; reserpine.
- **Purine alkaloids** — caffeine, theobromine,
  theophylline.  Coffee, tea, cacao, guarana, kola
  nut, yerba mate.
- **Pyridine + piperidine** — nicotine (*Nicotiana*);
  conine (*Conium maculatum* hemlock); piperine
  (*Piper nigrum*).
- **Steroidal alkaloids** — solanine (green potato);
  aconitine (*Aconitum*); steroidal glycoalkaloids in
  Solanaceae.
- **Sesquiterpene + diterpene alkaloids** — taxol +
  related (technically diterpenoids with N
  decoration).
- **β-carbolines** — harmine, harmaline (*Peganum
  harmala*; ayahuasca).

### Pharmaceutical relevance

The alkaloid catalogue underwrote a large fraction
of the early-modern pharmacopoeia:
- Morphine — analgesia (1804 isolation, the first
  pure alkaloid).
- Quinine — antimalarial (until synthetic
  alternatives).
- Atropine + scopolamine — antimuscarinics
  (mydriasis, motion sickness).
- Cocaine — local anaesthetic (largely replaced by
  amino-amides like lidocaine).
- Colchicine — gout + familial Mediterranean fever.
- Vincristine + vinblastine — chemotherapy (anti-
  microtubule).
- Galantamine — Alzheimer's (AChE inhibitor).
- Tubocurarine — neuromuscular blocker (replaced by
  synthetics).
- Reserpine — early antihypertensive.
- Caffeine + theophylline — stimulant + bronchodilator.
- Nicotine — smoking-cessation agonists; pesticide
  history (banned).

## Terpenoids — isoprene-derived

~ 30 000+ structures.  Built from 5-carbon isoprene
units (C5 H8) by two pathways:
- **MEP pathway** in plastids — produces monoterpenes,
  diterpenes, carotenoids, gibberellins.
- **MVA pathway** in cytosol — produces
  sesquiterpenes, sterols, brassinosteroids,
  triterpenes.

### Classes by isoprene-unit count

- **Hemiterpenes (C5)** — isoprene itself (released
  by leaves; atmospheric chemistry impact);
  dimethylallyl pyrophosphate.
- **Monoterpenes (C10)** — essential oils.  Limonene
  (citrus), menthol (mint), pinene (pine), camphor,
  geraniol, linalool, citronellal.
- **Sesquiterpenes (C15)** — many flavour + scent
  compounds + insecticides.  Farnesene, humulene
  (hops), bisabolol (chamomile), parthenolide
  (feverfew), artemisinin (anti-malarial,
  *Artemisia annua*; Tu Youyou 2015 Nobel).
- **Diterpenes (C20)** — taxol (*Taxus brevifolia*),
  abietic acid (resin), ginkgolides (*Ginkgo*),
  gibberellins (hormone).
- **Sesterterpenes (C25)** — rare; some sponge
  metabolites; some plants.
- **Triterpenes (C30)** — saponins, phytosterols,
  brassinosteroids, cucurbitacins, ginsenosides
  (*Panax ginseng*), avenacin (oat antifungal).
- **Tetraterpenes (C40)** — carotenoids (carotenes,
  xanthophylls).
- **Polyterpenes** — natural rubber (*Hevea
  brasiliensis*; cis-1,4-polyisoprene), gutta-percha
  (*Palaquium gutta*; trans-1,4-polyisoprene).

### Pharmaceutical + agricultural

- **Taxol** — first-line chemotherapy for breast +
  ovarian + lung cancer.  Total synthesis +
  semi-synthesis from baccatin III (10-deacetyl-
  baccatin III from yew needles) overcame supply
  problems.
- **Artemisinin** — antimalarial; ACT
  combinations are first-line therapy.  Engineered
  yeast strains produce artemisinic acid (Amyris
  + Sanofi industrial-scale).
- **Vinca alkaloids** — covered under indole alkaloids
  but biosynthetically partly terpenoid.
- **Limonene + pinene + linalool + menthol** —
  flavour + fragrance industry.
- **Pyrethrins** — natural insecticides from
  *Chrysanthemum cinerariifolium*; basis of synthetic
  pyrethroids.
- **Azadirachtin** — *Azadirachta indica* (neem);
  insect-feeding deterrent.
- **Carotenoids + lycopene** — nutraceuticals +
  industrial colorants.

## Phenylpropanoids + flavonoids

Derived from the shikimate pathway → phenylalanine →
trans-cinnamic acid via PAL → coumarins, lignins,
flavonoids.

### Phenylpropanoids (C6-C3)

- Cinnamic acid + p-coumaric acid + caffeic acid +
  ferulic acid + sinapic acid.
- Coumarins (warfarin from sweet clover dicoumarol;
  6-MOP psoralens).
- Hydroxycinnamic-acid esters + amides.

### Flavonoids (C6-C3-C6)

- **Anthocyanidins** — red / blue / purple pigments
  in vacuole (cyanidin, delphinidin, pelargonidin,
  malvidin, peonidin, petunidin).
- **Flavonols** — quercetin, kaempferol, myricetin
  (yellow + UV-protective + co-pigments).
- **Flavanols / proanthocyanidins** — catechin,
  epicatechin, condensed tannins.  Tea, cocoa, wine.
- **Flavones** — apigenin, luteolin, baicalein.
- **Flavanones** — naringenin, hesperidin (citrus).
- **Isoflavones** (Fabaceae specifically) — genistein,
  daidzein.  Soybean phytoestrogens.
- **Aurones, chalcones** — yellow pigments.

### Tannins

Polymeric phenolic; protein-binding (the astringent
mouthfeel of tea + red wine + unripe fruit).
- **Hydrolysable tannins** — gallic acid + ellagic
  acid esters.
- **Condensed tannins (proanthocyanidins)** —
  flavanol polymers.

Insect + microbial defence; tanning of leather
(historic industrial use).

### Lignins

Phenylpropanoid polymers in secondary cell walls
(see Plant cell architecture lesson).  Three monomer
types ratio differs across taxa:
- Gymnosperms — mostly G (guaiacyl) lignin.
- Angiosperms — G + S (syringyl) lignin.
- Grasses — G + S + H (p-hydroxyphenyl) lignin.

Lignin engineering (less recalcitrant lignin) is a
major target for biofuel + pulping industries.

## Other secondary-metabolite classes

- **Polyketides** — flavonoids overlap; also:
  6-methylsalicylic acid, parasorbic acid.
- **Cyanogenic glycosides** — linamarin (cassava),
  amygdalin (bitter almond, peach pit).  Release
  HCN on tissue damage.
- **Glucosinolates (Brassicaceae)** — sulphur-
  containing; the "mustard oil bomb"; sulforaphane
  + glucoraphanin in broccoli.
- **Saponins** — triterpene + steroid glycosides;
  soap-like foam; haemolytic (saponins lyse RBC
  membranes).  Quillaja bark, soapwort, yucca,
  ginsenosides.
- **Cardenolides + bufadienolides (cardiac
  glycosides)** — Digitalis (foxglove); inhibit
  Na/K-ATPase → contractile force in CHF.
- **Latex** — chicle (gum), opium poppy alkaloid
  store, *Hevea* rubber.
- **Resin acids** — pine resin, mastic, frankincense,
  myrrh.

## Engineering plant natural products

The diversity + scarcity of many medicinal plant
products motivates production via:

### Plant tissue + cell culture

- *Taxus* cell suspensions for paclitaxel (Phyton
  Biotech / Nippon Steel).
- *Coptis japonica* cell cultures for berberine.
- *Catharanthus roseus* cell suspensions for vinca
  alkaloids (limited success).

### Microbial heterologous production

- **Yeast (Saccharomyces cerevisiae)** is the workhorse.
- **Artemisinic acid in yeast** — Amyris + Sanofi;
  industrial scale; partial pathway in 12 genes;
  Keasling 2003-2013 development.
- **Opioids in yeast** — Smolke 2015; full pathway
  for thebaine + hydrocodone from sugars; ~ 23-step
  pathway in yeast.
- **Vinblastine precursors in yeast** — Smolke +
  others; partial pathway works.
- **Cannabinoids in yeast** — Keasling lab; multiple
  startups (Cellibre, Demetrix).

Synthetic biology approach:
1. Sequence + identify pathway from native plant.
2. Functional validation of each enzyme.
3. Refactor into yeast + balance fluxes.
4. Optimise titres + scale.

### Synthetic biology challenges

- **Cytochrome P450 expression** — many plant CYPs
  expressed poorly in yeast; engineering needed.
- **Compartmentalisation** — plant pathways often
  span ER + cytosol + plastid + vacuole; rewiring
  in yeast non-trivial.
- **Substrate channeling + multi-enzyme complexes**
  — natural plant pathways often spatially organised.
- **Toxic intermediates** — accumulating intermediates
  may inhibit downstream enzymes or kill the host.

### CRISPR + metabolic engineering in plants

- Knock out competing branches of native pathway →
  redirect flux.
- Over-express rate-limiting enzymes.
- Hairy-root cultures of *Catharanthus* for
  vincristine production.
- Engineering *Nicotiana benthamiana* for
  transient + scalable expression.
- Tobacco-based COVID-19 vaccines (Medicago,
  withdrawn 2023).
- Plant-made antibodies + biologics
  ("biopharming") — niche but functional
  (ZMapp Ebola).

## Discovery + bioprospecting

- **Ethnobotany** — traditional knowledge guides
  natural-product discovery.
- **High-throughput screening** of plant extract
  libraries against modern targets.
- **Genome-mining** — identify biosynthetic gene
  clusters (BGCs) in plant genomes; functional
  characterisation.
- **Multi-omics** — combine metabolomics +
  transcriptomics to find pathway candidates.
- **Synthetic-biology refactoring** — express
  pathways heterologously to confirm function.

The Nagoya Protocol on Access + Benefit-Sharing
governs how plant genetic resources collected from
biodiversity-rich countries are used + how benefits
flow back.
