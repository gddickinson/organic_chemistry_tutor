# Single-atom catalysis (SACs)

A **single-atom catalyst (SAC)** has the active metal
isolated as **individual atoms** dispersed on a support, not
as nanoparticles or extended phases. The atom-economy +
selectivity reach the limit; everything you load is
accessible.

The term came from Zhang + Liu's 2011 *Pt₁/FeOₓ* paper.
SACs have exploded since — > 10 000 papers by 2025.

## Why single atoms?

- **100 % atom utilisation** — every metal atom is at the
  surface (vs ~ 1 % for a 50-nm nanoparticle).
- **Uniform active sites** — each atom is in the same
  coordination environment → reduces selectivity scatter.
- **Coordination-tunable** — the support's lattice + dopant
  atoms (N, O, S) tune the metal's electronics like a
  ligand sphere — bridge between heterogeneous +
  homogeneous catalysis.
- **Maximum precious-metal economy** — Pt + Ir + Rh
  catalysts cost a fraction of conventional nanoparticles
  for the same activity.

## Synthesis methods

Stabilising single atoms against aggregation is the central
challenge:

- **Wet impregnation + reductive heat** — cheap; works only
  at very low metal loading (< 1 %).
- **Atomic layer deposition (ALD)** — sequential metal-
  precursor + reactant pulses; precise atomic placement.
- **Mass-selected ion deposition** — UHV; single Mg, Pt,
  Au, Cu atoms placed on graphene + TiO₂.
- **MOF / COF templating** — pre-organised metal nodes act
  as SACs (e.g., Cu / MOF-74 for CO₂ reduction).
- **N-doped carbon supports** — pyrolyse a Pt/Co/Fe
  precursor + nitrogen source (melamine, dicyandiamide,
  polyaniline) → M-N₄ / M-N₂ sites embedded in the carbon
  matrix. Most popular SAC fabrication today.

## Characterisation

A single atom looks the same as a small cluster on most
microscopes. The diagnostics:

- **Aberration-corrected STEM (HAADF)** — sub-Å resolution;
  bright single atoms visible vs the support background.
  Definitive imaging.
- **EXAFS / XANES** — coordination number + oxidation
  state. M-M scattering absent → single atoms (not
  clusters).
- **CO chemisorption + DRIFTS** — single sites give a
  single CO-stretch frequency vs broad band on
  nanoparticles.
- **Mössbauer (for ⁵⁷Fe)** — distinguishes Fe(II) / Fe(III)
  + coordination state.

## Reactions where SACs win

### CO₂ → CO electroreduction

- **Ni-N₄/C, Fe-N₄/C, Co-N₄/C** SACs — > 90 % FE for CO at
  -0.6 V; selectivity superior to corresponding metal NPs
  (which produce H₂ from competitive HER).

### CO₂ → CH₄ photoreduction

- **Ru-N₄ on g-C₃N₄** with light — multi-electron CO₂
  reduction to methane, > 60 % selectivity.

### Hydrogenation

- **Pt₁/FeOₓ** — hydrogenation of nitroaromatics with full
  selectivity (no over-reduction to N-H byproducts).
- **Pd₁/N-doped carbon** — selective semihydrogenation of
  alkynes to alkenes (rivals Lindlar).

### NH₃ synthesis (alternative to Haber-Bosch)

- **Ru / metal-oxide SACs** — N₂ activation at lower P + T
  than Haber-Bosch (target: ambient electrochemical NH₃).

### O₂ reduction (fuel-cell cathode)

- **Fe-N₄ / C** — replaces Pt in PEM fuel cells; matches
  Pt activity at 5 % the cost. Now in commercial Toyota
  + Hyundai fuel-cell vehicles.

### Organic transformations

- **Pd-N₄ / C** — Suzuki + Heck couplings; Pd loading
  reduced 10× vs nanoparticle Pd/C.
- **Au-Cu single atoms on TiO₂** — selective alcohol
  oxidation.

## Bridging homogeneous + heterogeneous

A single Pt-N₄ site embedded in graphene / carbon is
chemically intermediate between:

- **Pt-PR₃ complex** (homogeneous) — single atom + ligand
  field, soluble.
- **Pt nanoparticle** (heterogeneous) — many atoms,
  insoluble, recoverable.

SACs combine homogeneous-like discrete-site selectivity
with heterogeneous-like recovery + scaling. This is the
biggest conceptual win.

## Open challenges

- **Low loading** — keeping single atoms isolated requires
  < 5 wt %; total catalyst mass per kg of substrate is
  large.
- **Stability** — single atoms can sinter into
  nanoparticles under reaction conditions (heat, prolonged
  use). Coordination-engineered supports counter this.
- **Selectivity vs activity trade-off** — some reactions
  benefit from cluster effects (multiple adjacent sites
  for multi-electron transfer); SACs underperform there.
- **Mechanism debates** — is the single atom always the
  active site, or do reaction conditions form transient
  clusters? "Single-atom alloys" (Sykes, Tufts) show
  mixed phases.

## Industrial reality

By 2025:

- **Pajarito Powder + IRD Fuel Cells** sell Fe-N₄ / C
  cathode materials for PEM fuel cells.
- **Plant-scale** single-atom Pt₁ / γ-Al₂O₃ catalysts
  used in propane dehydrogenation (DOW Chemical, UOP).
- **Single-atom + flow chemistry** — packed-bed reactors
  with SAC deliver continuous-flow hydrogenation +
  oxidation at industrial scale.
