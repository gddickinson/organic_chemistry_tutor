# Catalytic enantioselective C-H functionalisation

The C-H activation lesson covered the basics + the
directing-group strategies. The frontier — enantioselective
C-H functionalisation — installs new C-C / C-N / C-O
bonds at C-H sites WITH high ee, often from achiral
substrates.

## Why this is hard

Standard C-H activation already gives selectivity issues
(regio, chemo). Adding **enantioselectivity** requires:

- A chiral catalyst that distinguishes between enantiotopic
  C-H bonds (in a prochiral substrate).
- Or that distinguishes the two prochiral faces during
  insertion of a new group.

The ee can be controlled at the:

1. C-H cleavage step (chiral metal center on metallation).
2. Functionalisation step (chiral ligand-controlled).

## Chiral Pd C-H activation (Yu)

Jin-Quan Yu (Scripps, 2008+) developed chiral mono-protected
amino acid (MPAA) ligands + chiral pyridyl-isoxazolines for
Pd(II) C-H activation:

```
ArH + RC(=O)X (acetate, halide) + Pd / chiral MPAA → Ar-R (with ee 80-95%)
```

Substrate must have a **directing group** (carboxylic acid,
amide, ester) ortho to which C-H is activated. The
prochiral C-H bond is in the para or ortho group.

### Examples

- **β-arylation** of α,α-disubstituted phenylalanines via
  chiral MPAA → > 90 % ee.
- **C-H borylation** with chiral pybox → enantioselective
  Bpin installation.
- **C-H lactonisation** of carboxylic acids — Yu (2018,
  *Nature*) → > 90 % ee γ-butyrolactones.

## Chiral Rh dirhodium catalysts

Rh₂(esp)₂ + chiral dirhodium catalysts (Davies, Doyle):

```
RC(=N₂)R' (diazo) + ArH + Rh₂(chiral)₂ → ArCR(R')H (C-H insertion product)
```

The Rh-carbene inserts into a C-H bond with chirality
controlled by the dirhodium catalyst's chiral ligands.

Davies's developed:

- **Donor-acceptor diazo compounds** (Ar-C(=N₂)-CO₂R) → more
  selective + functional-group tolerant than free metal
  carbenes.
- **Chiral dirhodium** → > 90 % ee for benzylic + allylic
  + α-N C-H bonds.
- **α-Methyl-α-aryl ester** synthesis (a common
  pharmacophore pattern) via C-H insertion.

## Chiral Ir + Pt C-H activation

- **Ir-catalysed asymmetric Bpin installation** (Hartwig,
  Boebel, Buchwald) — Ir + chiral pyridyloxazoline → > 90 %
  ee.
- **Pt(II) chiral C-H borylation** — emerging.

## Chiral photoredox HAT

For sp³ C-H bonds:

- **Chiral phosphate / Ir-photoredox** + chiral HAT catalyst
  → enantioselective benzylic + α-N C-H functionalisation
  (Knowles, Doyle, Meggers).
- **Chiral Ni / Ir-photoredox** for cross-coupling of
  benzylic radicals → enantioenriched products.
- **Chiral Cu-CDC (cross dehydrogenative coupling)** — Li
  + Doyle-type.

## Yu's β + γ + δ-C-H activation

Beyond the standard ortho:

- **Pd / chiral MPAA + 1,2-migration template** → meta-C-H
  functionalisation (rare without templating).
- **Pyridyl-template** + Yu's chiral catalyst → δ-C-H
  activation (5 atoms away from the directing group).
- **Pyridyl + sulfonate template** for ε-C-H (6 atoms).

These routes give access to remote functionalisation that
classical chemistry cannot.

## Chiral biocatalysis as an alternative

Engineered cytochrome P450 + chiral selectivity:

- **Arnold (Caltech)** — directed evolution of P450s for
  asymmetric C-H hydroxylation, halogenation,
  carbene-insertion, nitrene-insertion.
- **Codexis** + **Galaxy Labs** — commercial chiral
  hydroxylation enzymes.

By 2025, many drug-discovery programs use a combination:
Rh / Ir for directed C-H insertion, P450 for non-directed
hydroxylation.

## Industrial uses

- **Pirtobrutinib** (Pfizer BTK-i) — Ir-catalysed
  borylation + Yu's directed C-H activation in process
  development.
- **Late-stage SAR libraries** at major pharma companies
  → install F, OMe, NHR, OH at hard-to-access positions.
- **Chiral natural-product synthesis** — Davies's C-H
  insertion shortened multiple steroid + terpene
  syntheses.
