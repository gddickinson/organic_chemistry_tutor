# Total synthesis modern — Baran, Sarpong, Stoltz, Wood

Modern total synthesis (post-2005) shifted emphasis from
*can it be done?* to *can it be done short, scalable, and
with new chemistry?* The leaders: Phil Baran (Scripps),
Sarah Reisman + Brian Stoltz (Caltech), Richmond Sarpong
(Berkeley), John Wood (Baylor), Eric Carreira (ETH).

## Baran philosophy — ideality + scalability

Phil Baran's group at Scripps preaches **ideality**:

> An ideal synthesis features only **construction** steps
> (bond-forming) — no protecting-group manipulation,
> redox-economy, no functional-group interconversion just
> for the sake of the next step.

His landmark efforts:

- **Vinigrol (2009)** — 23 steps; wall of failure for two
  decades, finally cracked by an unconventional Heck-style
  cascade.
- **Ingenol (2013)** — 14 steps; commercial Picato (KK4-
  acetate) precursor was previously a 45-step semisynthesis.
  Baran's route reduced cost-of-goods enough for the FDA-
  approved drug to launch.
- **Ouabagenin (2013)** — 21 steps to a complex steroidal
  cardiotonic.
- **Baran electrochemistry programme** — electrochemical
  C-H functionalisation, deoxygenation, cross-couplings →
  reagent-free + scalable.

## Sarpong — diversity-oriented + complexity-oriented

Richmond Sarpong's hallmark: **late-stage skeletal
diversification**. Build a common polycyclic precursor,
then use selective C-C bond cleavage / rearrangement to
diverge into different natural products.

- **Skeletal editing** — break, swap, or re-form one ring
  bond on a complex framework, generating dozens of
  scaffolds from one substrate. Modern sub-field.
- **Phomactin total syntheses** — multiple natural
  products from a common bicyclic core via late-stage
  manipulation.

## Stoltz — Pd asymmetric catalysis

Brian Stoltz's group: enantioselective decarboxylative
allylation via Pd / chiral PHOX ligand → all-carbon
quaternary stereocentres in one step.

- **(-)-Cyanthiwigin F** total synthesis — 11 steps,
  asymmetric Pd-catalysed double allylation as the key
  step setting two quaternary centres.
- **(-)-Curcusone D** — 8 steps, photo-Nazarov + hydration
  cascade.

Reisman (also at Caltech) parallel programme on Ni-
catalysed asymmetric reductive cross-coupling.

## Wood — concise heteroatom-rich syntheses

John Wood (Baylor): N-rich + O-rich natural products in
short routes.

- **(+)-Ingenol via 8-step semisynthesis from prostratol**
  (Wood, 2014) — competitor to Baran's de-novo route.
- **Ingenol-3-mebutate / KK4-acetate** synthesis routes
  used in topical drug Picato.

## Sames + Du Bois — methodology-driven

Dalibor Sames (Columbia) + Justin Du Bois (Stanford):
total syntheses *that drive methodology development*.

- **Du Bois Rh-nitrene C-H amination** (covered in lesson
  9) — first applied in tetrodotoxin synthesis.
- **Sames N-Boc-piperidine + Pd-catalysed C-H activation
  in alkaloid synthesis**.

## Movassaghi + MacMillan — radical + photoredox

Mohammad Movassaghi (MIT) — biomimetic radical dimerisation
to build (+)-11,11'-dideoxyverticillin A + related dimeric
indole alkaloids.

David MacMillan (Princeton) — visible-light photoredox
unlocks new disconnections; collaborated with Doyle group
on metallaphotoredox Ni / Ir routes for complex API
synthesis.

## Modern process-chemistry total synthesis

- **Pfizer maraviroc** — 11-step asymmetric route to an
  HIV-entry-blocker drug.
- **Merck sitagliptin (Januvia)** — 3-step biocatalytic
  asymmetric transaminase route replacing 8-step Rh-cat
  hydrogenation.
- **Codexis biocatalysis cascades** — many APIs use
  transaminase / KRED / lyase cascades end-to-end.
- **Snapdragon Chemistry / Asymchem flow synthesis** —
  CRO services for kg-scale flow process development.

## A worked vignette — Baran's **(-)-vinigrol** (2009)

Vinigrol = a 4-membered + 8-membered + 5-membered ring
sesquiterpene; bridge structure thwarted attempts since
1987.

Baran's route used:

1. **Diels-Alder** to set initial bicyclic system.
2. Several stereoselective **alkylations** to install
   methyl groups.
3. Critical **type-II intramolecular Diels-Alder** to
   forge the 8-membered ring without strain (instead of
   trying to close it in a single step like prior attempts).
4. **Bridgehead methylenation**.
5. Elaboration of the 4-membered ring via radical cyclisation.

23 steps; longest linear sequence; broke the 23-year
"unmakeable" reputation.
