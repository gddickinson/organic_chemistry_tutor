# Sustainable polymer chemistry

The polymer industry produces ~ 400 million tonnes / yr.
Plastics are critical to modern life but most are single-use
+ persistent in the environment. Sustainable polymer
chemistry tries to reduce the input (renewable feedstocks),
the lifetime (controllable degradation), or both.

## Categories of "sustainable"

Three orthogonal axes:

| Axis | What it means | Examples |
|------|---------------|----------|
| **Renewable feedstock** | Made from biomass / CO₂, not petroleum | PLA, bio-PE, PEF, polycarbonates from CO₂ |
| **Biodegradable** | Microbially degraded under specified conditions | PLA, PHA, PBS, PCL |
| **Chemically recyclable** | Depolymerise back to monomer | PLA, PET (improved), polyacetals |

**Caution**: bio-derived ≠ biodegradable, and biodegradable
≠ chemically recyclable. PLA is bio-derived AND
biodegradable AND chemically recyclable, which is unusual.

## Renewable-feedstock polymers

### PLA (polylactic acid)

Monomer: lactic acid (from fermentation of corn / cassava /
sugarcane).

```
2 lactic acid → cyclic lactide → ROP → poly(lactide)
```

Properties: glass-clear, biodegradable in industrial
composters (60 °C, 80 % humidity, 12 weeks), workable
above Tg ~ 60 °C. Used in 3D printing, food packaging,
bioabsorbable medical sutures.

NatureWorks (Cargill / PTT) makes ~ 150 kt/yr.

### Bio-PE + bio-PET

Same polymer as petroleum versions, but ethylene from
sugarcane fermentation → ethanol → dehydration. Drop-in
replacement; not biodegradable but renewable.

Coca-Cola PlantBottle — 30 % bio-PET.

### PEF (polyethylene furanoate)

Replaces PET; furan-2,5-dicarboxylic acid (FDCA, from
biomass) + ethylene glycol. Better gas barrier than PET,
easier to recycle.

Avantium / Synvina (BASF JV) commercialising ~ 5 kt/yr
pilot. Not yet a mainstream replacement.

### Polycarbonates from CO₂

Direct copolymerisation of CO₂ + epoxides:

```
CO₂ + propylene oxide + Co/Cr-salen catalyst → poly(propylene carbonate)
```

Up to 50 wt% CO₂ in the polymer. Novomer + Aramco JV
commercialising; emerging niche in coatings + adhesives.

### Lignin → polymers

Lignin = 30 % of plant biomass; massive cheap feedstock.
Depolymerise to vanillin, syringol, guaiacol → polymerise
into polymers / replace BPA in epoxy resins. Several
startups (Ren Composite, Ligna, Lignin Industries).

## Biodegradable polymers

### PHAs (polyhydroxyalkanoates)

Bacterially-produced inside cells as energy reserves.
Polymerised by the bacterial enzyme + harvested by
extraction.

```
3-hydroxybutyrate → PHB (poly-3-hydroxybutyrate)
3-HB + 3-hydroxyvalerate → PHBV (copolymer)
```

Fully biodegradable in soil + marine environments.
Cost-prohibitive vs PLA but used in medical devices,
high-value food packaging.

Danimer Scientific + Bio-on + Newlight produce kt-scale.

### PBS (polybutylene succinate)

Made from succinic acid (fermentation) + 1,4-butanediol.
Compostable. Used as biodegradable mulch film, cutlery,
cups. Higher melting point (115 °C) than PLA.

### PCL (polycaprolactone)

ROP of ε-caprolactone. Biodegradable on long timescales
(months-years). Medical use: bioabsorbable sutures, drug-
delivery scaffolds.

## Chemically recyclable polymers

The newest frontier: design polymers that **fully
depolymerise** to monomer under mild conditions, then
re-polymerise.

### Trimethylene carbonate / γ-butyrolactone polymers

Hillmyer's polymers from γ-valerolactone + γ-butyrolactone
(both bio-derived) — 100 % recyclable to monomer.

### PET → BHET (Greenpeace / IBM)

PET enzymatic depolymerisation by **PETase** or
LCC (leaf-branch compost cutinase) at 65 °C, ~ 90 % yield
of mono-ethylene-terephthalate.

Carbios scaled this to 50 kt/yr enzymatic-recycled PET in
2024.

### CO₂-based polycarbonate recycling

Co-polymers of CO₂ + epoxide depolymerise back to cyclic
carbonate at moderate T → cleaner closed-loop than PET.

### Vitrimer chemistry (Leibler 2011)

Networks with **dynamic covalent bonds** (transesterification,
disulfide exchange, imine exchange) — heat → bonds shuffle
→ material flows like a thermoplastic. Cool → bonds re-
freeze → solid like a thermoset. Reprocessable +
self-healing.

## End-of-life facts

A reality check:

- Less than 10 % of all plastic ever produced has been
  recycled.
- Most "biodegradable" PLA only degrades in industrial
  composters, not in home compost or oceans.
- Mechanical recycling (melt + remold) degrades polymer
  MW each cycle.
- Chemical recycling (depolymerise to monomer) avoids MW
  degradation but is energy-intensive.
