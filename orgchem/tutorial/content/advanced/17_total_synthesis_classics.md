# Total synthesis classics — Woodward, Corey, Nicolaou

The 20th century saw total synthesis evolve from a curiosity
into the highest-art form of organic chemistry. This lesson
walks four landmark campaigns + their lessons for modern
synthesis.

## Why bother?

Total synthesis of a natural product proves:

- **Structural assignment** — connectivity + stereochemistry.
- **Methodology stress-test** — discovers + showcases new
  reactions in challenging contexts.
- **Training** — produces world-class synthetic chemists who
  populate industrial research groups.

Critics argue it consumes labour for outcomes that
fermentation or semisynthesis can achieve cheaper. Both
true — total synthesis is an academic + pedagogical act
more than a route to material.

## Woodward — strychnine (1954)

Robert Burns Woodward synthesised strychnine in 28 steps.
Strychnine: 7 fused rings, 6 stereocentres in a 24-carbon
indole-alkaloid skeleton. Until 1954 considered hopeless.

Woodward's key tactics:

- **Diels-Alder** to set 6-membered rings.
- **Intramolecular Mannich** for the curved-belt N-bridge.
- Stereocontrol via chair-like transition states (Woodward
  reasoned about TS shape years before X-ray of TS analogs).
- Iterative Fischer indole synthesis for the aromatic core.

Subsequent strychnine syntheses include Magnus (29 steps),
Overman (24), Vanderwal (12). The "shortest published" race
continues.

Beyond strychnine, Woodward's career: cortisol (1951),
chlorophyll (1960), tetracycline (1962), vitamin B₁₂
(11-year campaign with Eschenmoser, 1973). Won the 1965
Nobel Prize.

## Eschenmoser + Woodward — vitamin B₁₂ (1973)

The most ambitious total synthesis ever attempted: a corrin
ring with 9 stereocentres, central Co, axial CN, dimethyl-
benzimidazole nucleotide, and an octapeptide-like sidearm.

11 years; 100+ chemists across two labs (ETH Zürich + Harvard).

Significance: the campaign gave birth to **the Woodward-
Hoffmann rules**. Eschenmoser + Woodward needed to
understand the conrotatory vs disrotatory closure of a
hexatriene-like intermediate; the systematic answer came
from Robert Hoffmann's MO analysis. The 1981 Nobel Prize
followed (Hoffmann + Fukui).

## Corey — gibberellic acid (1978)

Elias J. Corey (Harvard) developed:

- **Retrosynthesis as a discipline** — formalised the
  systematic backward analysis (1957) that won him the
  1990 Nobel Prize.
- **Many named reagents + reactions**: Corey-Chaykovsky
  ylide (sulfur ylide cyclopropanation), Corey-Bakshi-
  Shibata reduction (asymmetric ketone reduction), Corey-
  Fuchs alkyne synthesis, PCC oxidation.
- **Total synthesis** of gibberellic acid (1978),
  prostaglandin F₂α (1969), erythronolide B (1978),
  ginkgolide B (1988).

Gibberellic acid: 5-membered + 6-membered fused rings
with γ-lactone bridge + axial methyl, vinyl, OH. Corey's
route: 24 steps, key Diels-Alder + Wittig steps. Set the
standard for stereocontrolled multi-step synthesis.

## Nicolaou — taxol (1994)

K. C. Nicolaou's group at Scripps + the Holton group at
Florida State synthesised taxol independently in 1994.
Taxol: 8-membered B ring + complex side chains;
microtubule-stabilising anti-cancer drug.

Nicolaou's route:

1. McMurry coupling to forge the 8-membered ring.
2. Diels-Alder to set the bicyclic A-B system.
3. Heck cyclisation for further ring closures.
4. Late-stage installation of the C-13 sidechain.

51 steps total. Industrial supply via **semisynthesis from
10-deacetylbaccatin III** (extracted from Taxus baccata
needles + acetylated → baccatin III + sidearm coupling).

Nicolaou's other targets: brevetoxin B, calicheamicin γ₁ᴵ,
azadirachtin, vancomycin, eleutherobin, CP-225,917, taxol.

## Total synthesis lessons

Lessons that reverberate through modern medicinal chemistry:

- **Plan from the end backward** — Corey's retrosynthesis.
- **Convergent > linear** — assemble fragments
  independently then join late.
- **Use stereochemistry to your advantage** — chair TS,
  Felkin-Anh, ring-fused chairs that lock geometry.
- **Don't fear protecting-group orthogonality** — use
  multiple tiers (Boc / Bn / TBS / Ac) so each PG can be
  removed independently.
- **Late-stage modifications** — install the sensitive
  group last (Yu's late-stage C-H activation; biocatalytic
  hydroxylation).
- **Avoid chromatography** when scaling up — crystallise
  intermediates, use telescoped flow.
