# MOFs + COFs — porous reticular frameworks

**Reticular chemistry** designs solid materials by linking
molecular building blocks into ordered networks. Two big
families:

- **Metal-organic frameworks (MOFs)** — metal cluster nodes
  + organic linkers.
- **Covalent organic frameworks (COFs)** — purely organic
  nodes + linkers via covalent bonds.

Both produce **crystalline, porous, designable** solids
with internal surface areas up to 7000 m²/g (one gram of
MIL-101 has the surface area of a football pitch).

## MOFs

### Building blocks

- **Secondary building units (SBUs)** — pre-formed metal
  clusters: Zn₄O octahedral (MOF-5), Cu₂(OOC)₄ paddlewheel
  (HKUST-1), Zr₆O₄(OH)₄ (UiO-66), Cr₃O(OOC)₆ (MIL-101).
- **Linkers** — di- / tri- / tetra-carboxylates +
  imidazolates + pyridines: BDC, BTC, BPDC, NDC + many.

### Synthesis

- **Solvothermal** — DMF or water, 80-200 °C, 12-72 h. Forms
  microcrystalline powders or single crystals.
- **Mechanochemical** — ball-milling, no solvent.
- **Continuous flow** — kg-scale industrial synthesis (BASF
  Basolite).

### Famous MOFs

- **MOF-5** (IRMOF-1, Yaghi 1999) — Zn₄O + BDC, 3500 m²/g.
- **HKUST-1** (Cu-BTC) — paddlewheel Cu₂(OOC)₄, 1500 m²/g,
  catalysis applications.
- **MIL-101** (Cr₃O-BDC, Férey 2005) — 5500 m²/g, enormous
  cages.
- **UiO-66** (Zr-BDC, Lillerud 2008) — water-stable
  (because Zr-O is strong), thermal stable to 500 °C.
- **ZIF-8** (Zn-imidazolate, Yaghi) — sodalite topology,
  hydrolysis-stable, low-cost.
- **NU-1501-Al** (Hupp + Farha 2020) — record 7140 m²/g
  surface area.

### Applications

- **Gas storage**: H₂ (vehicular fuel), CH₄, CO₂. MOF-5 +
  HKUST-1 hold > 100 g CH₄ / L at 35 bar.
- **CO₂ capture from flue gas + direct air capture (DAC)** —
  amine-functionalised MOFs (Mg₂(dobpdc), CALF-20, IRMOF
  variants). Climeworks, Carbon Engineering hybrid programs.
- **Catalysis**: Lewis-acid sites, encapsulated metal
  nanoparticles, photoactive linkers (porphyrins).
- **Separation**: olefin / paraffin separation by MOF
  membranes — replace energy-intensive cryogenic
  distillation.
- **Drug delivery**: MIL-100 + MIL-101 host ibuprofen /
  doxorubicin for slow release.
- **Sensors**: luminescent MOFs change emission on guest
  binding.

## COFs

Côté + Yaghi 2005 — Schiff-base + boronate-ester +
boroxine + triazine + olefin condensation between rigid
multi-functional organic monomers:

- **COF-1** — diboronic acid → boroxine network.
- **COF-5** — boronate ester (HHTP + benzene diboronic
  acid).
- **TpBD** — Schiff-base imine COF, water-stable.
- **CTF-1** — covalent triazine framework (cyanide
  trimerisation under ZnCl₂ flux).

### Why COFs?

- **No metals** — biocompatible.
- **Lower density than MOFs** — fully organic.
- **Conjugated electronic structure** — designable as
  semiconductors (CTF-1, TPP-COFs).
- **Tunable pore size + functionality** by linker design.

### Applications

- **Heterogeneous photocatalysis** — water splitting (CTFs),
  CO₂ reduction.
- **Lithium / sodium battery electrodes** — redox-active
  COFs cycle stably.
- **Supercapacitors** — high surface area + conductivity.
- **Drug delivery + sensing**.

## Reticular design principles

1. **Topology** — pick a target net (sql, hxl, fcu, soc,
   ftw, nbo). Each net needs specific node + linker
   geometry.
2. **Match SBU geometry to linker connectivity** — Zr₆ +
   BDC = fcu; Zn₄O + BDC = pcu.
3. **Isoreticular synthesis** (Yaghi) — same topology,
   different linker → tune pore size + chemistry without
   redesigning everything.
4. **Defects + disorder** — modulators (HCl, formic acid,
   benzoic acid) tune crystallinity + introduce missing-
   linker defects which become catalytic sites.

## Characterisation

- **Powder X-ray diffraction (PXRD)** — fingerprint for
  topology + crystallinity.
- **N₂ + Ar physisorption (BET)** — surface area, pore
  size, pore-size distribution.
- **TGA** — thermal stability + linker / metal ratio.
- **NMR (solid-state ¹³C, ¹H)** — verify chemistry.
- **SEM, TEM** — particle morphology.
- **Synchrotron PXRD + electron crystallography** — for
  MOFs / COFs that don't form single crystals.

## Industrial scale

- **BASF Basolite** — first commercial MOFs (HKUST-1, MOF-
  5, ZIF-8 variants).
- **NuMat / numerical Materials** — MOF storage cylinders
  for electronic-grade gases (arsine, phosphine).
- **Mosaic Materials** (acquired by ExxonMobil 2022) —
  Mg₂(dobpdc) for natural-gas CO₂ removal at point sources.
- **Climeworks + Svante** — direct-air-capture systems
  using sorbent + MOF hybrid contactors.

## Limits + challenges

- **Water + acid stability** — many MOFs hydrolyse
  (especially Zn-carboxylate). Zr + Hf MOFs are exceptions.
- **Powder vs monolith** — applications need shapeable
  forms; pellet pressing often crushes pore structure.
- **Cost** — most linkers are expensive vs zeolites or
  activated carbon. UiO-66 ~ $10/g lab scale.
- **Defect chemistry not fully understood** — same MOF from
  two labs can have different catalytic activity.
