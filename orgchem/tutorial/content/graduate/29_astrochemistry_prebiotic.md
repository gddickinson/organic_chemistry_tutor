# Astrochemistry + prebiotic chemistry

Where do the molecules of life come from? **Astrochemistry**
catalogues organic molecules in space (interstellar clouds,
comets, planetary atmospheres). **Prebiotic chemistry**
asks how the simple molecules of the early Earth could
have assembled into the complex molecules of biology
without enzymes.

## Astrochemistry — what's out there

Over 250 distinct molecules have been identified in the
interstellar medium (ISM) by 2025. Some surprises:

- **CO** — most abundant molecule after H₂; tracer of
  molecular cloud structure.
- **H₂O, NH₃, CH₃OH, HCN, HCOOH, CH₃CHO** — small
  prebiotic-relevant molecules in cold dense clouds.
- **PAHs (polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons)** — up to ~ 20 %
  of cosmic carbon. Detected by IR emission features at
  3.3, 6.2, 7.7, 8.6, 11.3 µm.
- **Glycine + amino acid candidates** — tentatively
  detected by ALMA submillimetre arrays in star-forming
  regions; confirmed in meteorites + comet 67P/CG.
- **Sugars** — glycolaldehyde + dihydroxyacetone detected
  in star-forming regions; ribose detected in lab analogues
  of comet ice irradiation.
- **C60 (buckminsterfullerene)** — first detected in
  cosmic dust 2010 (Cami et al.).

## Detection methods

- **Microwave + millimetre rotational spectroscopy** —
  molecules in cold ISM (10-30 K) emit narrow rotational
  lines at characteristic frequencies. ALMA is the
  premier observatory.
- **Infrared spectroscopy** — vibrational signatures of
  ices on dust grains (JWST is revolutionising this).
- **UV / optical** — diffuse interstellar bands (DIBs);
  most still unidentified, possibly large carbonaceous
  molecules.
- **Mass spectrometry** in situ — Rosetta's COSAC + ROSINA
  on comet 67P; Cassini's INMS at Saturn / Titan.
- **Sample return** — Hayabusa-2 (asteroid Ryugu) +
  OSIRIS-REx (asteroid Bennu) brought back grams of
  primitive material containing > 100 amino acids,
  nucleobases, sugars, PAHs.

## Where life-precursor chemistry happens

- **Cold molecular clouds** (10 K, 10⁴ atoms/cm³) — H, OH,
  CN radical chemistry on dust-grain surfaces.
- **Hot cores around protostars** (100-300 K) — sublimated
  ice mantles release complex organic molecules.
- **Protoplanetary disks** — pre-biotic chemistry inherited
  by planetesimals.
- **Comets + meteorites** — preserve early Solar-System
  organic inventory; CV / CM chondrite meteorites contain
  > 80 amino acids, nucleobases, sugars.
- **Hydrothermal vents on early Earth** — the most likely
  cradle of life.

## The Miller-Urey experiment (1953)

Stanley Miller (working with Harold Urey) sent electrical
sparks (mimicking lightning) through a flask containing
methane + ammonia + H₂ + water — produced amino acids in
days. **Reanalysed in 2008** with modern HRMS: found > 23
amino acids (vs the 5 originally reported).

The original CH₄ + NH₃ + H₂ atmosphere is now considered
unlikely; modern simulations use neutral CO₂ + N₂ + H₂O
(low yields without other inputs). But:

- Volcanic outgassing locally provides CH₄ + H₂S + NH₃ +
  H₂.
- Ice-comet impacts deliver organics directly.
- UV-driven photochemistry in upper atmospheres + ocean
  surfaces.

## Modern prebiotic synthesis

### Sugars from formaldehyde — formose reaction

```
n CH₂O + Ca(OH)₂ catalyst → mixture of sugars (glycoladehyde
                                              → glyceraldehyde
                                              → tetroses, pentoses, hexoses)
```

Formose is a complex autocatalytic mixture — yields ribose
+ many other sugars, no selectivity. Modern variants with
selective borate-stabilisation (Ricardo + Benner) give
much cleaner ribose.

### Nucleobases from HCN

HCN + H₂O / heat → adenine (HCN pentamerisation, Oró 1960),
guanine + cytosine (in trace amounts). Electric discharge
in HCN-NH₃-H₂O atmosphere gives all 5 standard
nucleobases.

### Sutherland's synthesis (2009)

Powerful 4-step route to ribonucleotides under pre-biotic
conditions: glycolaldehyde + cyanamide + 2-aminooxazole
(from glycolaldehyde + cyanamide) + glyceraldehyde + UV +
phosphate → cytidine ribonucleotide. Bypasses the
"nucleoside formation problem" by building the nucleobase +
sugar simultaneously around a phosphate.

### Powner-Sutherland network (2019)

Same starting materials assemble into nucleotides + amino
acids + lipids in one one-pot network. The first
demonstration that all three biopolymer building-block
classes can co-emerge.

## RNA world hypothesis

Crick + Orgel (1968) proposed RNA-only life: RNA as both
information carrier AND catalyst. Evidence:

- Ribozymes exist — the ribosome is itself a ribozyme
  (peptide bond formation by RNA, not by protein).
- Self-replicating RNAs designed in the lab (Bartel,
  Joyce).
- TPP-, riboflavin-, glycine-, lysine-binding riboswitches
  in modern bacteria are likely relics.

Modern updated: **RNA + peptide world** — short peptides
co-evolved with RNA, leading to today's protein-dominated
biology.

## Solar System prebiotic frontiers

- **Enceladus** (Saturn moon) — subsurface ocean +
  hydrothermal vents detected by Cassini; H₂ + CO₂ +
  organic molecules in plumes.
- **Europa** (Jupiter moon) — subsurface ocean; Europa
  Clipper mission launched 2024.
- **Mars** — past liquid water, organic molecules detected
  by Curiosity + Perseverance rovers.
- **Titan** (Saturn moon) — atmosphere + lakes of liquid
  methane + ethane; complex organic chemistry.
- **Bennu + Ryugu samples** — extant organic chemistry
  preserved from the early Solar System.
