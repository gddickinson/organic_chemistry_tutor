# Microbiome chemistry — gut metabolites + microbial natural products

The human gut harbours ~ 10¹³-10¹⁴ microbes (10× the
human cell count) producing thousands of small molecules
that affect host physiology, immunity, neurology,
cardiovascular disease, and drug metabolism. The chemistry
of the **microbiome** is a major frontier of biomedical
research.

## What microbes make

Categories of microbial metabolite:

- **Short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs)** — acetate,
  propionate, butyrate; from fermentation of dietary
  fibre.
- **Bile acid derivatives** — secondary bile acids
  (deoxycholic acid, lithocholic acid) from primary bile
  acid deconjugation + dehydroxylation.
- **Tryptophan metabolites** — indole, indole-3-aldehyde,
  IPA, kynurenine; AhR receptor agonists, gut-brain axis.
- **Vitamin synthesis** — K₂ (menaquinones), B12, biotin,
  riboflavin.
- **Polyamines** — putrescine, spermidine, spermine.
- **Bioactive lipids** — N-acyl amides (mimic
  endocannabinoids).
- **Trimethylamine + TMAO** — from L-carnitine + choline
  by gut microbes; TMAO linked to cardiovascular disease.
- **Bacterial natural products** — antibiotics, quorum-
  sensing molecules, bacteriocins.

## SCFAs — the fibre-fermentation product

Dietary fibre passes undigested through the small intestine
→ fermented by colon bacteria (Bacteroidetes, Firmicutes)
to acetate (~ 60 %), propionate (~ 20 %), butyrate (~ 20 %).

SCFA biological roles:

- **Butyrate** = preferred fuel for colonocytes; HDAC
  inhibitor → epigenetic regulation; anti-inflammatory.
- **Propionate** = liver gluconeogenesis substrate;
  satiety signalling.
- **Acetate** = circulates systemically; appetite
  regulation; lipogenesis substrate.

GPR43 + GPR41 are SCFA receptors expressed on
enteroendocrine + immune cells.

## Tryptophan metabolites + the gut-brain axis

Trp can take 3 fates in the gut:

1. **Serotonin pathway** — host enzymatic; ~ 90 % of
   serotonin is made in the gut.
2. **Kynurenine pathway** — IDO + TDO oxidative; immune-
   modulatory metabolites.
3. **Microbial pathway** — Lactobacillus, Bacteroides,
   Clostridium produce indole, IPA, indole-3-aldehyde,
   tryptamine. These are AhR (aryl hydrocarbon receptor)
   agonists modulating barrier integrity + immune
   homeostasis.

## Bile-acid metabolism

Liver makes ~ 0.5 g/day of cholic acid + chenodeoxycholic
acid (primary bile acids), conjugated to glycine /
taurine. ~ 95 % is reabsorbed in ileum + recirculated
("enterohepatic circulation").

Of the 5 % escaping into colon:

- **Bile salt hydrolases (BSH)** — bacterial enzymes that
  remove glycine / taurine.
- **7α-dehydroxylation** by Clostridium scindens / hylemoneae
  → secondary bile acids (deoxycholic, lithocholic).

Secondary bile acids signal through FXR + TGR5 receptors →
liver, gut, immune effects. Therapeutic angle: obeticholic
acid (FXR agonist, FDA-approved for primary biliary
cholangitis), other FXR agonists in development.

## TMA / TMAO — cardiovascular angle

Choline (eggs, red meat) + L-carnitine (red meat) →
trimethylamine (TMA) by gut bacteria → liver FMO3 oxidises
to TMAO.

Hazen's group (Cleveland Clinic 2011) showed TMAO levels
correlate with cardiovascular events. TMAO promotes
foam-cell formation + platelet hyperreactivity. Cancer +
chronic kidney disease links also published.

Drug development: gut-restricted TMA-lyase inhibitors that
block bacterial conversion (Tomera, 2019; FMC labs).

## Microbial natural products

Bacteria + fungi are the historic source of antibiotics +
many other drugs:

- **Penicillin** (Penicillium, Fleming 1928).
- **Streptomycin, Vancomycin, Tetracyclines** (Streptomyces).
- **Cyclosporin** (Tolypocladium fungus).
- **Rapamycin** (Streptomyces hygroscopicus from Rapa Nui).
- **Lovastatin** (Aspergillus terreus) — first statin.
- **Avermectin** (Streptomyces avermitilis; Ōmura, 2015
  Nobel).

Modern frontier: **biosynthetic gene cluster (BGC) mining**.
Sequence soil + ocean microbiomes → predict undiscovered
natural products from PKS / NRPS / RiPP / terpenoid BGCs
→ heterologous expression to make + characterise.

## Drug metabolism by microbes

Many drugs hit the gut microbiome before / instead of the
liver:

- **Digoxin** — *Eggerthella lenta* reduces it to inactive
  dihydrodigoxin in some patients.
- **Sulfasalazine** — gut bacterial azoreductase splits to
  active 5-ASA.
- **Irinotecan (camptothecin prodrug)** — bacterial
  β-glucuronidase reactivates the toxic SN-38 in colon →
  diarrhoea.
- **Levodopa** — *Enterococcus + Lactobacillus* decarboxylate
  → dopamine in the gut, reducing brain bioavailability.

Pharma drug discovery now considers the gut microbiome as
a metabolising organ + tries to predict / block bacterial
metabolism.

## Multi-omics methods

- **Metagenomics** — sequence DNA → who's there + what
  genes they have.
- **Metatranscriptomics** — sequence RNA → what's being
  expressed.
- **Metabolomics** — LC-MS / NMR of stool, blood, urine →
  small-molecule profile.
- **Culturomics** — high-throughput culture of "unculturable"
  microbes; Amrane et al. showed > 1000 new gut species.

## Therapeutic + diagnostic angles

- **Faecal microbiota transplant (FMT)** — gold standard
  for recurrent C. difficile infection (Rebyota, Vowst —
  FDA-approved 2022/23).
- **Live biotherapeutic products (LBPs)** — defined
  consortia of gut bacteria as drugs.
- **Postbiotics** — purified microbial metabolites
  (butyrate, propionate, IPA, urolithin A) sold as
  supplements / drugs.
- **Microbiome biomarkers** — IBD, C. difficile, colon
  cancer, neurological conditions all have characteristic
  microbiome signatures.
