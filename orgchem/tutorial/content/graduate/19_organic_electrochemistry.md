# Organic electrochemistry — synthesis with electrons

**Electrochemistry** drives chemical reactions by passing
current through a solution. Electrons are the **traceless
reagent** — no stoichiometric oxidant or reductant goes into
the flask, so no waste from spent reagents.

After decades on the periphery, organic electrochemistry has
boomed since ~ 2015 — Baran, Lin, Lehnherr, Lei, Waldvogel +
others built methodology + cheap potentiostats made it
accessible.

## Why now?

Three converging factors:

1. **Affordable instruments** — IKA ElectraSyn 2.0 ($3k)
   replaced complex bespoke setups + made every research
   group capable.
2. **Selectivity at potential** — small differences in
   E_red discriminate similar functional groups; tunable.
3. **Green chemistry** — replaces stoichiometric heavy-metal
   oxidants (CrO₃, MnO₂, OsO₄) + supports renewable-electron
   sourcing.

## Setup essentials

- **Cells**: undivided (cheap, simple) vs divided (membrane-
  separated; needed when one electrode would attack
  product).
- **Electrodes**: graphite, carbon, glassy carbon (cheap,
  inert), Pt (precious-metal but precise), Ni / Ni-foam
  (alkaline base electrolysis), reticulated vitreous carbon
  RVC (high surface area).
- **Electrolyte**: nBu₄NBF₄, LiClO₄, R₄N⁺ X⁻ in MeCN /
  DMF / DCM. Concentration ~ 0.1 M.
- **Mediators** (optional): TEMPO, NHPI, Fe(bpy)₃, ferrocene
  shuttle electrons between electrode + substrate, acting
  as homogeneous redox catalysts.

## Two operating modes

### 1. Constant current (galvanostatic)

Set current (mA) + electrolysis time → moles of charge
(F = 96 485 C/mol e⁻). Simple. The applied potential drifts
during the reaction but doesn't matter for many
conversions.

### 2. Constant potential (potentiostatic)

Set the working-electrode potential vs reference (Ag/AgCl
or Ag/AgNO₃ pseudo-reference) → only species with E_red ≤
applied potential reduce. Selective but slower + needs a
good reference electrode.

## Famous reactions + recent methodology

### Kolbe electrolysis

Two RCO₂⁻ → R-R + 2 CO₂↑ at the anode. The original
electroorganic reaction (1849!). Used at industrial scale
for sebacic acid → adipic acid + diester.

### Anodic functionalisation

- **Shono oxidation** (1975) — α-amine C-H → α-methoxy-
  amine; key step in alkaloid synthesis.
- **Anodic C-H halogenation** (Lei) — replaces stoichiometric
  oxidant + halogen source.
- **Electrooxidative C-H amination** (Xu) — pyridyl directs
  ortho-amination.
- **Aromatic fluorination** (Waldvogel) — Selectfluor
  alternative.

### Cathodic functionalisation

- **Birch-like reduction** (Baran 2019) — benzene → cyclo-
  hexadiene at -40 °C using Mg sacrificial anode + LiBr +
  glycine — replaces Li / NH₃.
- **Reductive coupling of pinacols** (Stahl, Lin) — ketone
  → pinacol homocoupling.
- **C-N cross-coupling** (Doyle, Sevov) — Ni-catalysed +
  electrochemically regenerated.

### Paired electrolysis

Use both anodic and cathodic chemistry simultaneously —
double atom economy + double current efficiency.

### Industrial electroorganic processes

- **Adiponitrile** (Monsanto, > 300 kt/yr) — acrylonitrile
  hydrodimerisation, the largest-tonnage electroorganic
  process. Feeds nylon-6,6.
- **Membrane chlor-alkali** — NaCl + H₂O → Cl₂ + NaOH +
  H₂. Largest electrochemistry on Earth.
- **Anodic oxidation of glucose to gluconic acid** — pilot
  scale.

## Mediated electrocatalysis

Direct electron transfer to substrate is sometimes slow.
Mediator catalysts (in solution) carry the electron between
electrode + substrate:

- **TEMPO** — alcohol → aldehyde / ketone / acid.
- **NHPI** (N-hydroxyphthalimide) — C-H oxidation.
- **Iodide / iodate** — halogenations.
- **Fe / Co / Mn polypyridyl** — mediated O₂ reduction.
- **Acridinium** — photo-electrocatalysis hybrid.

## Photoelectrocatalysis

Light + electricity together — frontier:

- Electrochemistry tunes the radical generation rate.
- Photoredox can extend the redox window of the
  electrochemistry.
- Lin + Knowles + Lei publishing actively.

## Scale-up + flow

- **Flow electrolysis** scales well — surface-area-to-volume
  ratio matters more than total current.
- **Vapourtec, Syrris, Asynt** sell modular flow electro
  cells.
- **Pilot plant** — Bayer, BASF, Pfizer have published
  electroorganic kg-scale processes.

## Beyond synthesis

- **Electroanalysis** — cyclic voltammetry (CV), differential
  pulse voltammetry (DPV) → measure redox potentials,
  diffusion coefficients, mechanisms.
- **Bioelectrochemistry** — enzyme electrodes (glucose
  sensors, lactate sensors).
- **Fuel cells + batteries** — every battery is an
  electrochemical cell.
