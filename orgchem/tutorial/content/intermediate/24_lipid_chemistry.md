# Lipid chemistry — fatty acids, glycerides, phospholipids

Lipids are a structural class defined by **hydrophobicity**
rather than a unifying chemical motif. Five subfamilies do
most of the work in biology:

- **Fatty acids** — long-chain monocarboxylic acids.
- **Triglycerides** (TAGs) — energy storage.
- **Phospholipids** — membrane structure.
- **Sphingolipids** — membrane signalling.
- **Sterols** — membrane fluidity + signalling.

## Fatty acids — naming + properties

A fatty acid is `CH₃-(CH₂)_n-COOH`. Saturated chains pack
well + are solid at room temperature; unsaturated chains
have cis double bonds that introduce kinks → liquid.

Notation: **C:D** where C is chain length + D is number of
double bonds. Position of the first double bond from the
methyl (ω) end:

| Name | Notation | ω | Source |
|------|----------|---|--------|
| Palmitic | 16:0 | – | palm oil, animal fat |
| Stearic | 18:0 | – | beef tallow |
| Oleic | 18:1 (cis-9) | ω-9 | olive oil |
| Linoleic | 18:2 (9,12) | ω-6 | seed oils; essential |
| α-Linolenic | 18:3 (9,12,15) | ω-3 | flax, walnut; essential |
| Arachidonic | 20:4 (5,8,11,14) | ω-6 | meat, fish; eicosanoid precursor |
| EPA | 20:5 (5,8,11,14,17) | ω-3 | fish oil |
| DHA | 22:6 (4,7,10,13,16,19) | ω-3 | brain + eye |

**Essential fatty acids** = the body can't make. Humans
can't introduce double bonds beyond ω-9, so ω-3 + ω-6 must
be dietary.

## Trans vs cis

Natural unsaturated fatty acids are **cis**. Industrial
hydrogenation of vegetable oils to make margarine creates
**trans** isomers (industrially called "partially
hydrogenated"), which are more linear → solid at room T but
also harmful (raise LDL, lower HDL, increase
cardiovascular risk). Banned in many countries since 2015.

## Triglycerides

A glycerol esterified with three fatty acids:

```
CH₂-O-CO-R₁
 |
CH-O-CO-R₂
 |
CH₂-O-CO-R₃
```

Energy density: **9 kcal/g** (vs ~ 4 kcal/g for carbs +
protein) because of the highly reduced C-H bonds.

### Saponification

```
TAG + 3 NaOH → glycerol + 3 RCO₂⁻Na⁺  (soap)
```

The salt of a long-chain fatty acid is amphiphilic →
micelles → cleaning action. Soap-making is the original
industrial chemistry.

## Phospholipids

A diglyceride with a phosphate ester at the third position;
the phosphate is further esterified to a polar headgroup
(choline, ethanolamine, serine, inositol):

```
CH₂-O-CO-R₁           (sn-1: usually saturated)
 |
CH-O-CO-R₂            (sn-2: usually unsaturated)
 |
CH₂-O-PO₃⁻-O-X        (X = headgroup)
```

| Headgroup | Name | Net charge at pH 7 |
|-----------|------|----------------------|
| Choline (-CH₂CH₂N(CH₃)₃⁺) | PC (phosphatidylcholine) | 0 (zwitterion) |
| Ethanolamine | PE | 0 (zwitterion) |
| Serine | PS | -1 |
| Inositol | PI | -1 (more if phosphorylated) |
| Glycerol | PG | -1 |
| – (phosphatidic acid) | PA | -1 to -2 |

Phospholipids self-assemble into **bilayers** in water (~ 5
nm thick) — the basic membrane structure. Hydrophilic
headgroups face water; acyl tails face each other inside.

## Sphingolipids

Built on sphingosine (instead of glycerol):

- **Ceramide** = sphingosine + fatty acid (amide).
- **Sphingomyelin** = ceramide + phosphocholine.
- **Cerebrosides** = ceramide + sugar.
- **Gangliosides** = ceramide + sialylated oligosaccharide.

Concentrated in the brain + nerve tissue. Lipid-rafts in
membranes are sphingolipid-rich.

## Sterols

A four-fused-ring (gonane) skeleton. Cholesterol is the
canonical animal sterol; ergosterol does the same job in
fungi; phytosterols (sitosterol) in plants.

Cholesterol's role:

- **Membrane fluidity** — packs between phospholipid acyl
  chains, increasing rigidity at body T but preventing
  freezing at low T.
- **Bile acid precursor** — cholic acid + glycocholate +
  taurocholate emulsify dietary fats.
- **Steroid hormone precursor** — cortisol, testosterone,
  estradiol, progesterone all biosynthetically derived.
- **Vitamin D₃ precursor** — 7-dehydrocholesterol + UV →
  pre-vitamin D₃ → vitamin D₃.

## Eicosanoids — local hormones from arachidonic acid

20-carbon (eicosa-) derivatives:

- **Prostaglandins (PGs)** — COX-1 + COX-2 enzymes; PGE2
  drives fever, inflammation. **Aspirin + ibuprofen** block
  COX.
- **Thromboxanes (TXs)** — TXA2 promotes platelet
  aggregation; aspirin's COX-1 acetylation in platelets
  → bleeding-time prolongation, basis of low-dose aspirin
  therapy.
- **Leukotrienes (LTs)** — 5-LOX enzyme; LTC4 + LTD4
  drive asthma. **Montelukast (Singulair)** blocks LT
  receptors.
- **Lipoxins, resolvins, protectins** — pro-resolution
  signals; modulate inflammation resolution.
