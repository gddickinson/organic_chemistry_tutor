# Aromaticity — what makes benzene special

Benzene (C₆H₆) sits at the centre of organic chemistry like a
planet — a flat 6-membered ring of sp² carbons with 6 π
electrons, doing chemistry that no isolated triene would ever
do. Understanding **why** benzene is so special is the gateway
to one of the largest substrate classes in organic synthesis.

## What aromaticity demands

Hückel's rule (1931): a molecule is **aromatic** if it has

1. A **fully conjugated** ring of p orbitals (every ring atom
   sp² or sp).
2. The ring is **planar**.
3. The π-system has **(4n + 2) electrons** for some integer n
   (n = 0 → 2 e⁻; n = 1 → 6 e⁻; n = 2 → 10 e⁻; …).

Benzene checks all three: 6 sp² carbons in a flat hexagon, 6
π electrons (2 per double bond × 3) → n = 1.

## Three flavours

- **Aromatic** — meets all three rules. **Stable**, doesn't
  add reagents like an alkene; instead does **electrophilic
  aromatic substitution (EAS)** so that aromaticity is
  preserved.
- **Antiaromatic** — meets 1 + 2 + has 4n electrons (cyclo-
  butadiene with 4 e⁻ is the textbook example). **Less stable**
  than the corresponding open-chain π system.
- **Non-aromatic** — fails one of 1, 2, 3. Just behaves like
  isolated π bonds.

## Why aromatic stability matters

Benzene is ~ 36 kcal/mol more stable than three isolated
alkenes — a huge **resonance stabilisation energy**. Practical
consequences:

- **EAS over addition**: bromine + benzene gives bromobenzene
  + HBr (substitution, aromaticity preserved), NOT a 1,2-
  dibromocyclohexadiene (would destroy aromaticity).
- **Acidic phenol** (C₆H₅OH, pKa ~ 10) is much more acidic
  than cyclohexanol (pKa ~ 16) because the phenolate is
  resonance-stabilised by the ring.
- **Polycyclic aromatics** (naphthalene, anthracene,
  phenanthrene, the buckyball, graphene) all share the
  aromaticity stabilisation.

## Heteroaromatics

Aromaticity isn't limited to all-carbon rings. Replace a CH
with a heteroatom + you get the heteroaromatic family:

| Ring | Heteroatom | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Pyridine (6-mem) | 1 N | basic, mp around water bp |
| Pyrimidine (6-mem) | 2 N | DNA bases (cytosine, uracil, thymine) |
| Furan (5-mem) | 1 O | important in natural-product chemistry |
| Thiophene (5-mem) | 1 S | conducting polymers, electronics |
| Pyrrole (5-mem) | 1 N | porphyrin macrocycles, indole, tryptophan |
| Imidazole (5-mem) | 2 N | histidine, biological catalysis |

Indole (a benzene fused to a pyrrole) is the core of
**tryptophan**, **serotonin**, **DMT**, and the auxin plant
hormone (indole-3-acetic acid).

## Named reactions on aromatics

Five seeded EAS reactions exist in the **Reactions** tab:

- **Nitration of benzene** (HNO₃ / H₂SO₄ → nitrobenzene).
- **Friedel-Crafts alkylation** (R-Cl / AlCl₃).
- **Friedel-Crafts acylation** (R-COCl / AlCl₃).
- **Suzuki coupling** (aryl bromide + boronic acid → biaryl).
- **Negishi / Heck / Stille** (other Pd-catalysed cross-
  couplings on aryl halides).
