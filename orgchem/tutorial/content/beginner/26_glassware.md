# Common glassware — touring the bench

A working organic-chemistry bench has 30-40 distinct pieces of
glassware. Knowing which is which — and when to use each —
saves you broken glass, spilled product, and frustrating
reactions. This lesson walks the dozen most common items.

## Reaction vessels

- **Round-bottom flask (RBF)** — the workhorse. Comes in
  10 mL, 25 mL, 50 mL, 100 mL, 250 mL, 500 mL, 1 L, 2 L, 5 L.
  Spherical bottom distributes heating evenly; ground-glass
  neck (24/29 standard for 100 mL+) accepts adapters,
  condensers, addition funnels.
- **Three-neck RBF** — for reactions needing simultaneous
  reflux + addition + thermometer. The two side necks are
  usually 14/20 (smaller); the centre is 24/29.
- **Erlenmeyer (conical) flask** — for crystallisation,
  workup. The conical shape lets you swirl + the wider mouth
  pours easily. **Not for vacuum** (flat bottom is weak).
- **Beaker** — open-top, scale-marked. Use for measuring,
  decanting, simple solvent transfers. Almost never used as
  a reaction vessel; the shape promotes solvent evaporation +
  poor heating.

## Condensers (solvent-recovery)

- **Liebig condenser** — straight inner tube + water jacket.
  The classic distillation condenser. Modest cooling; use for
  bp > 80 °C distillates.
- **Allihn ("bulb") condenser** — bulged inner tube to
  increase surface area. The standard reflux condenser.
- **Graham (coil) condenser** — spiral inner tube. More
  cooling area than Allihn; used for low-bp distillates.
- **Friedrichs (pony) condenser** — spiral inner with widest
  surface area. Best for very-low-bp solvents (Et₂O, DCM).
- **Vigreux column** — internal indentations that act as
  theoretical plates. Insert between RBF + distillation head
  to do **fractional distillation**.

## Adapters (couplings)

- **Distillation head** — three-port adapter that connects
  pot RBF, thermometer (top port), condenser (side port).
- **Claisen adapter** — Y-shaped two-port adapter; common
  use is to add a thermometer to a single-neck RBF without
  blocking the addition site.
- **Vacuum adapter ("cow")** — splits between condenser
  output + vacuum line + receiver flask.
- **Thermometer adapter** — converts a thermometer into a
  ground-glass joint.
- **Addition funnel** — gravity-fed dropwise addition. Top
  has a stopper (usually a drying tube or septum); bottom has
  a stopcock; ground-glass joint mates with a 3-neck RBF.

## Separation glassware

- **Separating funnel ("sep funnel")** — the aqueous /
  organic-phase separator. Pear-shaped with a stopper on top
  + a stopcock on the bottom. Vent often when shaking with
  volatile solvents.
- **Soxhlet extractor** — siphon-driven continuous solvent
  extraction. Cellulose thimble holds the solid analyte;
  refluxing solvent percolates through. Run for hours to
  days; cumulative extract concentrates in the pot.

## Filtration

- **Büchner funnel** — flat-plate ceramic funnel for vacuum
  filtration. Sits in a filter flask; uses a flat filter
  paper.
- **Hirsch funnel** — small Büchner-style funnel for tiny
  scales (1-10 mL). Same vacuum-filter principle.
- **Filter flask (vacuum flask)** — thick-walled flask with a
  side-arm for a vacuum hose. Holds the filtrate. **Always
  use a thick-walled vacuum flask** for vacuum work.

## Heating + stirring

- **Heating mantle** — half-spherical electrically-heated
  cradle that fits a specific RBF size. Indirect, even
  heating.
- **Hot plate / stir plate** — flat heated surface +
  magnetic stirring. Works with a flat-bottom flask or with
  a rounded flask sitting on an oil-bath dish.
- **Oil bath** — silicone-oil-filled crystallising dish on a
  hot plate. Lets you heat above water's boiling point
  (silicone good to ~ 250 °C).
- **Magnetic stir bar (stir bean)** — Teflon-coated bar
  that follows the magnet under a flat surface. Lozenge
  shape for RBFs; cross-shape ("magnetic stir egg") for
  Erlenmeyers.

## Try it in the app

- **Tools → Lab equipment…** (Ctrl+Shift+I) — full reference
  catalogue of every glassware type listed above + connection-
  port info (for the Phase-38c canvas).
- **Tools → Lab setups…** (Ctrl+Shift+U) — see how the
  glassware combines into named apparatus configurations
  (simple distillation, fractional distillation, reflux,
  Soxhlet, vacuum filtration, etc.).
- **Tools → Lab setup canvas…** (the Phase-38c canvas) — drag
  the glassware out of the palette + connect it to build your
  own setup. The Phase-38c.4 snap-validation will warn if
  you try to mate incompatible joints.
