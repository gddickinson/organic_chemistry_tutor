# Aqueous chemistry intro

Water is the universal solvent for biology + most analytical
chemistry. Knowing water's quirks lets you predict aqueous-
chemistry outcomes.

## Water's properties

- **Polar** (μ = 1.85 D) but small.
- **Strong H-bond donor + acceptor** (2 of each per molecule).
- **Autoionisation**: 2 H₂O ⇌ H₃O⁺ + OH⁻; K_w = 10⁻¹⁴ at 25 °C.
- **High dielectric constant** (ε = 80) — solvates ions
  effectively.
- **High specific heat + heat of vaporisation** — buffers
  temperature.
- **Density max at 4 °C** — ice floats, lakes freeze top-down.

## pH + ion product of water

```
Kw = [H⁺][OH⁻] = 10⁻¹⁴ at 25 °C
neutral pH = 7.00
acidic: pH < 7
basic: pH > 7
```

Kw rises with T → "neutral" pH at 37 °C is 6.81, not 7.00.
Physiological pH (7.4) is slightly basic by absolute
proton standard.

## Strong vs weak acids/bases

| Class | Examples | pKa |
|-------|----------|-----|
| Strong acid | HCl, HBr, HI, H₂SO₄, HClO₄ | < 0 (fully dissociated) |
| Weak acid | acetic, citric, lactic | 2-6 |
| Strong base | NaOH, KOH, Ca(OH)₂ | OH⁻ pKa = 15.7 |
| Weak base | NH₃, RNH₂, pyridine | conjugate pKa 9-11 |

## Solubility rules (rough)

- **Always soluble**: alkali-metal salts (Li, Na, K, NH₄⁺
  +ve) + nitrates + acetates.
- **Halides** soluble except Ag, Pb, Hg.
- **Sulfates** soluble except Ba, Pb, Sr, Hg, Ca.
- **Carbonates, phosphates, hydroxides, sulfides**
  generally INSOLUBLE except alkali-metal + ammonium.

These let you predict precipitation reactions.

## Common ion effect

Adding NaCl to a saturated AgCl solution shifts equilibrium
backward (precipitates more AgCl):

```
AgCl(s) ⇌ Ag⁺(aq) + Cl⁻(aq)        Ksp = 1.8 × 10⁻¹⁰
```

Used to recrystallise soluble salts by adding a common-ion
solvent + cool.

## Water in synthesis — two takes

### Avoid

Many reactions are killed by water:

- Grignard, organolithium, NaH (hydrolysis).
- Acid chlorides, anhydrides (hydrolysis).
- Schiff base / imine formation (need to remove H₂O).

### Embrace

Modern green chemistry uses water as solvent for:

- Click chemistry (CuAAC).
- Asymmetric organocatalysis (proline aldol).
- Enzyme reactions.
- Suzuki coupling with TPPTS (water-soluble Pd cat).
- "On-water" Diels-Alder (Sharpless 2005).

## Water in NMR

Water + H₂O signal at δ ~ 4.7 in ¹H NMR (CDCl₃ residual
H₂O at δ 1.6). For aqueous-only samples (D₂O):

- D₂O suppresses exchangeable NH/OH from your molecule.
- Use water-suppression pulses (presat, WET, WATERGATE)
  for biological NMR.
