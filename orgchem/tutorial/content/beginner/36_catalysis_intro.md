# Catalysis intro — what catalysts do

A **catalyst** is a substance that speeds up a reaction
**without being consumed**. Modern organic chemistry runs on
catalysis — every Nobel Prize in chemistry since 2000 has
involved catalysis at some level.

## What catalysts do (and don't do)

- They **lower the activation energy E_a** by providing an
  alternative reaction pathway.
- They **don't change ΔG** (the thermodynamics is fixed by
  the reactants and products, not the path).
- They are **regenerated** at the end of the catalytic cycle
  (otherwise they're stoichiometric reagents, not catalysts).
- Their **turnover number (TON)** measures total moles of
  product per mole of catalyst over the catalyst's lifetime.
- Their **turnover frequency (TOF)** measures TON per unit
  time.

## Three big catalyst categories

### 1. Acid + base catalysis

The simplest. A small amount of acid or base is added to
shuttle protons faster than the uncatalysed pathway.

- **Fischer esterification** (carboxylic acid + alcohol → ester)
  is acid-catalysed; the H⁺ activates the carbonyl + the
  leaving water.
- **Aldol condensation** is typically base-catalysed; OH⁻
  generates the enolate.

### 2. Metal catalysis

Transition metals (Pd, Ni, Cu, Ru, Rh, Ir, Au, Co) participate
via oxidative addition + reductive elimination + migratory
insertion + β-hydride elimination cycles.

The 2010 Nobel Prize (Heck + Negishi + Suzuki) recognised
**palladium-catalysed cross-coupling** — joining two carbon
fragments under mild conditions. Modern named reactions:
Suzuki, Negishi, Stille, Heck, Sonogashira, Buchwald-Hartwig,
Grubbs metathesis (2005 Nobel: Grubbs + Schrock + Chauvin).

### 3. Organocatalysis

A small chiral organic molecule does the catalysis without
metals. Recognised by the 2021 Nobel Prize (List + MacMillan).
Examples: proline-catalysed aldol reactions, MacMillan's
imidazolidinone catalysts for asymmetric Diels-Alder + Michael.

## Heterogeneous vs homogeneous

- **Homogeneous** — catalyst dissolves with the substrates
  (Pd(PPh₃)₄ in toluene).
- **Heterogeneous** — catalyst is a separate solid phase
  (Pd/C, Raney Ni, zeolites). Easier to filter off + recycle;
  harder to characterise mechanistically.

## Enzymes — nature's catalysts

Enzymes are protein catalysts. They achieve **10⁸ to 10²⁰ ×
acceleration** (orotidine 5'-phosphate decarboxylase wins at
~ 10¹⁷ ×) by stabilising the transition state via active-site
preorganisation. See the seeded *Chymotrypsin*, *Aldolase*,
*HIV protease*, *RNase A* mechanisms in the **Reactions** tab
for textbook enzyme-mechanism walks.
