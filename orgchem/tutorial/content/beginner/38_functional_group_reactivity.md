# Common functional groups and their reactivity — a survey

You've already met functional groups (lesson 4). This lesson
maps each group to its **typical reactivity pattern** — the
chemistry it most commonly does. Memorising this table makes
mechanism prediction tractable.

## The 12-group reactivity map

Group | Structure | Typical reactivity
---|---|---
Alkane | C–C, C–H | very low (radical halogenation, combustion)
Alkene | C=C | electrophilic addition, hydrogenation, oxidation, metathesis
Alkyne | C≡C | electrophilic addition, hydrogenation, terminal alkyne deprotonation
Alkyl halide | C–X | SN1, SN2, E1, E2, organometallic precursor
Alcohol | C–OH | acid-base, oxidation to carbonyl, dehydration to alkene, ester formation
Ether | C–O–C | inert (good solvents), occasionally cleaved by HI
Amine | C–NR₂ | basic + nucleophilic, alkylation, acylation
Aldehyde | R–CHO | nucleophilic addition (especially by Grignards, hydride), oxidation to acid, reductive amination
Ketone | R–CO–R' | nucleophilic addition, α-deprotonation to enolate, condensations (aldol, Wittig)
Carboxylic acid | R–COOH | acid (pKa ~ 5), Fischer esterification, amide formation, decarboxylation
Ester | R–COOR' | hydrolysis (saponification), transesterification, reduction to alcohol, Claisen condensation
Amide | R–CONR'₂ | very stable, hydrolysis (slow, acid- or base-catalysed)

## Reactivity hierarchy of carbonyl derivatives

Acid chloride > anhydride > ester > carboxylic acid > amide

Reading the hierarchy: more reactive groups can be converted to
less reactive ones, but not the reverse without forcing
conditions or extra reagents (coupling agents, dehydrating
acid catalysts).

## The "characteristic test" reactions

Each group has a quick chemical test that confirms its
presence:

- **Carboxylic acid** — fizzes when sodium bicarbonate is
  added (CO₂ released).
- **Aldehyde** — gives a positive Tollens test (silver mirror)
  or Fehling's test (red Cu₂O precipitate). Distinguishes
  aldehydes from ketones.
- **Phenol** — purple colour with FeCl₃.
- **Primary or secondary amine** — positive Hinsberg test
  (different solubilities of the sulfonamide product).
- **Alkene** — decolorises Br₂ in CCl₄.
- **Alkyne (terminal)** — gives a precipitate with AgNO₃
  (silver acetylide).

These are mostly historical pre-NMR diagnostics, but they're
still in textbooks + still useful for quick classroom
demonstration.

## Reactivity is context-dependent

The same functional group does different chemistry depending
on what's adjacent:

- A **simple alkene** undergoes electrophilic addition.
- An **α,β-unsaturated carbonyl alkene** undergoes Michael
  (1,4-conjugate) addition because the C=C is now an
  acceptor of nucleophiles.
- A **vinyl halide** (alkene + halide) doesn't do SN1 / SN2
  easily because the C–X is on an sp² carbon — but does
  Suzuki / Negishi cross-coupling beautifully.

## Try it in the app

- **Tools → Qualitative inorganic tests…** (Ctrl+Shift+Q) —
  while these are mainly inorganic, several functional-group
  tests (KMnO₄ for unsaturation, FeCl₃ for phenols) appear
  here too.
- **Reactions tab** → search by functional-group keyword
