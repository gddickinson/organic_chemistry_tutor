# Common organic solvents

The solvent is the silent third reagent in every reaction.
Picking the right solvent makes the difference between a clean
reaction in 30 minutes and a tar-and-byproducts disaster
overnight. This lesson surveys the dozen solvents you'll
encounter in any teaching lab.

## The polarity ladder (low → high)

|---------|----------|----|----|-------|
| Hexane | very low | 69 °C | non-polar | extraction, alkane chem |

| DCM (CH₂Cl₂) | low-mid | 40 °C | polar aprotic | extraction, washing |
| CHCl₃ | low-mid | 61 °C | polar aprotic | NMR (CDCl₃), extraction |
| Et₂O (diethyl ether) | mid | 35 °C | polar aprotic | Grignard, extraction |
| THF | mid | 66 °C | polar aprotic | organometallic, broad scope |
| EtOAc (ethyl acetate) | mid | 77 °C | polar aprotic | extraction, chromatography |
| Acetone | mid-high | 56 °C | polar aprotic | clean-up, recrystallisation |

## Aprotic vs protic

- **Protic** solvents have **O-H** or **N-H** bonds + H-bond
  donate. Examples: water, alcohols. Strong nucleophiles
  (RO⁻, RNH⁻) get **proton-quenched** in protic solvents, so
  protic solvents slow SN2.
- **Aprotic** solvents lack O-H / N-H. Examples: DMF, DMSO,
  THF, MeCN. They solvate cations well but don't tame anionic
  nucleophiles → SN2 + enolate chemistry runs faster.

## Picking a solvent — quick algorithm

1. **Will it dissolve the reactants?** Match polarity to your
   substrate's polarity (logP).
2. **Is it inert to the reaction?** Grignard ⇌ ketone goes
   wrong if you use a protic solvent (the Grignard
   deprotonates the solvent). Use Et₂O or THF.
3. **Does it have a reasonable boiling point** for your
   reflux temperature?
4. **Can you remove it cleanly?** Low-bp solvents rotovap off
   easily; high-bp ones (DMF, DMSO, NMP) need dilution +
   aqueous wash + column.
5. **Is it safe + cheap?** Avoid CHCl₃ (carcinogen), benzene
   (carcinogen), pyridine (only when essential). Prefer
   EtOAc, EtOH, water, EtOAc, 2-MeTHF for green chemistry.

## Common solvent pitfalls

- **DMF + water + heat** → slow hydrolysis to dimethylamine +
  formic acid. Use dry DMF for clean reactions.
- **DCM + amines + base** → DCM acts as an alkylating agent
  (very slow at room temp, but real for prolonged Et₃N
  contact). Standard for everyday work; problematic for
  long-term storage with amines.
- **THF peroxides** — old THF develops explosive peroxides
  (especially when concentrated). Test before distilling.
- **DMSO freezes at 18.5 °C** — keep it warm or it solidifies
  in the bottle.
- **Tris pH-drift** — Tris buffer drops ~ 0.03 pH units per °C
  warming. Always pH at the use temperature.
