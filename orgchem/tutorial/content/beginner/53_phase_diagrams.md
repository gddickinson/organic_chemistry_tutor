# Phase diagrams

A **phase diagram** maps which physical state (solid /
liquid / gas) is stable at a given (T, P). It tells you
whether you can sublime your compound, whether to expect a
liquid at your reaction temperature, and where the critical
point lies.

## Single-component diagram (water)

```
P |
  |        |     liquid       |
  |        |                  |  critical point (374 °C, 218 atm)
  |  solid |                  | /
  |        |triple point      | gas
  | (-273) |(0.01 °C, 0.006 atm)
  +--------+--------T---------+
```

Three regions + three boundaries:

- **Solid–liquid line**: melting/freezing.
- **Liquid–gas line**: boiling/condensing.
- **Solid–gas line**: sublimation/deposition.
- **Triple point**: all 3 coexist (water: 0.01 °C, 0.006 atm).
- **Critical point**: above this T, no liquid-gas
  distinction (water: 374 °C, 218 atm).

## Anomalies

Most substances: solid-liquid line tilts **right** (higher P
favours dense solid). Water: tilts **left** — ice is less
dense than liquid → high P melts ice (ice-skating
explanation, partly).

## CO₂ — practical sublimation

Triple point: -56.6 °C, 5.2 atm. At 1 atm, CO₂ goes
solid → gas (no liquid possible). Hence "dry ice" sublimes.

Sub-critical CO₂ at 73 atm + 31 °C → **supercritical CO₂**:

- Liquid-like density (good solvent).
- Gas-like diffusion (fast mass transfer).
- Tunable polarity by P (slightly).
- Volatile with no residue.

Used industrially:
- **Decaffeination** of coffee + tea.
- **Hops + flavour extraction**.
- **Polymer foaming** + supercritical-fluid chromatography
  (SFC).

## Binary phase diagrams

Two components, plot T vs composition. Famous shapes:

- **Eutectic** (NaCl/H₂O, ice-cream brine; alloys like
  Sn-Pb solder): one minimum at the eutectic composition;
  cool below to freeze.
- **Peritectic** (Fe-C system, steel): more complex
  multistep solidification.
- **Azeotropic** (EtOH/H₂O): liquid + vapour have same
  composition at one point — can't separate further by
  fractional distillation.

## Pharmaceutical phase diagrams

Drug + co-former screening uses ternary phase diagrams
(drug, coformer, solvent). The cocrystal region is bounded
by drug-solubility lines + coformer-solubility lines + the
cocrystal stability line.

Critical for cocrystal formulation choice: which
crystallisation conditions land you in the cocrystal
region instead of pure drug + pure coformer crystallising
out separately.
