# Welcome to Organic Chemistry

Organic chemistry is the study of **carbon-containing compounds** — how
they're built, how they react, and why some of them form the basis of
every living cell while others make up the plastics, drugs, and dyes that
shape daily life.

## What you'll learn here

This course runs from *zero background* to *graduate level*. Each lesson
is short, structured, and linked to interactive molecules in the database.
Click a molecule name in any lesson and the viewer on the left will snap
to its 2D skeleton and rotatable 3D model.

The early lessons cover **carbon hybridisation** (sp³ / sp² / sp), bond
energies, and how a compound's **pKa** sets its acid–base behaviour —
these three ideas anchor everything that follows.

## How to use this app

- **Left dock — Molecules**: the local database, searchable by name,
  formula, or SMILES. Click anything to display it.
- **Molecule Workspace tab**: the 2D structure on the left, interactive 3D on
  the right. Change display styles with the selectors above each view.
- **Right dock — Properties**: live-computed descriptors (MW, logP, TPSA,
  ring counts, Lipinski violations, …).
- **Right dock — Tutor (chat)**: type a question and a language model will
  *display* the relevant molecule and explain. It can open new lessons,
  search PubChem, compute formulas — everything in the app.
- **Tools → Empirical / Molecular Formula Calculator…**: recreates the
  classic textbook problem of deriving a molecular formula from element
  percentages and a molar mass.
- **Bottom dock — Session Log**: everything the app does, with a level
  filter.

## First exercise

1. Click **Caffeine** in the molecule browser.
2. Switch the 3D style from *stick* to *ball-and-stick* — drag to rotate.
3. Open the **Tutor** console and ask: *“Why does caffeine cross the
   blood-brain barrier so easily?”* Watch the tutor cite the logP value
   from the Properties panel.

You're ready. Pick the next lesson from the tree on the left.
